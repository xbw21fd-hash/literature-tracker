Topological structures in condensed matter systems unlock new possibilities for the development of nanoelectronic devices. 中文摘要

地球上几乎无法实现的太空​​微重力环境被认为对晶体生长具有积极影响，尤其是具有低层间滑动能垒的范德华层状材料。在这里，我们研究了中国空间站微重力环境下培育的范德华InSe半导体的结构和光电性质。原子级微观结构分析表明，这种独特的环境可以成功消除柔性InSe中自然存在的堆垛层错，直接激活具有优异保留稳定性的本征滑动铁电性。相应的铁电半导体场效应晶体管具有明显大的非易失性存储窗口、高开/关比和优异的迁移率。更重要的是，它们还可以为近红外非线性光源提供卓越的放大自发发射和极低的光子激发阈值。这些发现不仅提出了一种实现高质量范德华层状单晶（如 InSe）的非常规策略，而且还凸显了它们在结合内存和传感器功能的下一代发射极集成计算架构方面的潜力。

English Abstract

The space microgravity environment, scarcely attainable on Earth, is considered to have a positive effect on crystal growth, especially the van der Waals layered materials with low interlayer sliding energy barriers. Here, we investigate the structure and optical/electrical properties of van der Waals InSe semiconductor cultivated in microgravity environment on China space station. Atomic-level microstructure analyses reveal that this unique environment can successfully annihilate the naturally-existing stacking faults in flexible InSe, directly activating the intrinsic sliding ferroelectricity with excellent retention stability. The corresponding ferroelectric semiconductor field-effect transistors present obviously large non-volatile memory window, high on/off ratio and excellent mobility. More essentially, they can also give superior amplified spontaneous emission with exceptionally low excitation thresholds of photons for near infrared nonlinear light sources. These findings not only present an unconventional strategy for achieving high-quality van der Waals layered single crystals like InSe but also highlight their potential for next-generation emitter-integrated computing architectures combining memory and sensor functions.

中文摘要

当量子材料中的几个自由度具有相似的能级时，表现出破缺对称性的交织电子序通常是强耦合的。最近对 Kagome 超导体（例如 CsV3Sb5）的研究报告了与电荷密度波相关的旋转和时间反转对称性破缺。在这里，我们观察到 CsV3Sb5 电子结构对外部磁场的动量选择性响应。通过在可调谐磁场中进行角分辨光电子能谱，我们证明电子结构的响应与压磁性以及强轨道选择性兼容。我们的结果表明，时间反转对称性破缺的起源与电荷密度波序开始时的钒范霍夫奇点有关。我们还证明了超出电荷排序温度的波动的存在。我们的结果表明，磁场可以用作调谐旋钮，用于解开量子材料动量空间中相互交织的秩序。

English Abstract

When several degrees of freedom in quantum materials have similar energy scales, the intertwined electronic orders, which exhibit broken symmetries, are often strongly coupled. Recent studies on kagome superconductors, such as CsV3Sb5, report rotational and time-reversal symmetry breaking linked to a charge density wave. Here we observe a momentum-selective response of the electronic structure of CsV3Sb5to an external magnetic field. By performing angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy in a tunable magnetic field, we demonstrate that the response of the electronic structure is compatible with piezomagnetism along with strong orbital selectivity. Our results show that the origin of the time-reversal symmetry breaking is associated with the vanadium Van Hove singularities at the onset of the charge-density-wave order. We also demonstrate the presence of fluctuations beyond the charge ordering temperature. Our results reveal that magnetic fields can be used as tuning knobs for disentangling intertwined orders in the momentum space for quantum materials.
