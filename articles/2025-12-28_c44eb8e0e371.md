## Abstract
Considering transformation products (TPs) in environmental studies remains a huge challenge for scientists, from identification in samples via mass spectrometry through to inclusion in chemical regulation. This article introduces FAIR-TPs, a website to browse openly-available TP data collated from literature sources. The data is sourced from several community-contributed environmental datasets on the NORMAN Suspect List Exchange (NORMAN-SLE), plus a dataset from ChEMBL available through PubChem, with links to templates and contact details to encourage further community contributions. The data is compiled regularly using an open source workflow, archived and versioned on Zenodo under a CC-BY license, then processed and displayed on FAIR-TPs. FAIR-TPs currently contains 11,190 reactions involving 9,435 compounds from 11 sources. A graph-based representation of the transformations (compounds as nodes, reactions as edges) is stored in a Neo4j database as a Directed Graph and made publicly accessible online through a Django Web Application. Users can retrieve the shortest directed pathways between predecessors (parents) and successors (transformation products/metabolites), search by SMARTS substructures, or explore local reaction neighbourhood data on individual compounds. Interactive network visualizations provide ways for users to view multi-step transformations in a smooth, user-friendly interface, while exploring transformation pathways. The compound/reaction metadata provides links to further information about the chemicals and data sources. Key statistics, including number of reactions/compounds, top compounds, reaction types, mass differences are summarised from the current dataset. FAIR-TPs is designed to be a public resource to support suspect and non-target screening workflows, helping scientists identify data gaps and interpret complex transformation reactions. The FAIR-TPs website is openly available at https://fairtps.lcsb.uni.lu.

## 摘要（中文）
从通过质谱法识别样品到纳入化学监管，在环境研究中考虑转化产物 (TP) 对科学家来说仍然是一个巨大的挑战。本文介绍了 FAIR-TPs，这是一个浏览从文献来源整理的公开 TP 数据的网站。该数据源自 NORMAN 嫌疑人名单交换 (NORMAN-SLE) 上的多个社区贡献的环境数据集，以及通过 PubChem 提供的 ChEMBL 数据集，其中包含模板链接和联系方式，以鼓励社区进一步贡献。数据使用开源工作流程定期编译，在 CC-BY 许可下在 Zenodo 上存档和版本化，然后在 FAIR-TP 上进行处理和显示。 FAIR-TP 目前包含 11,190 个反应，涉及来自 11 个来源的 9,435 种化合物。基于图的转换表示（化合物作为节点，反应作为边）作为有向图存储在 Neo4j 数据库中，并通过 Django Web 应用程序在线公开访问。用户可以检索前体（亲代）和后继（转化产物/代谢物）之间的最短定向路径，通过 SMARTS 子结构搜索，或探索单个化合物的局部反应邻域数据。交互式网络可视化为用户提供了在流畅、用户友好的界面中查看多步骤转换的方法，同时探索转换路径。化合物/反应元数据提供有关化学品和数据源的更多信息的链接。从当前数据集中总结了关键统计数据，包括反应/化合物的数量、顶级化合物、反应类型、质量差异。 FAIR-TP 旨在成为支持可疑和非目标筛选工作流程的公共资源，帮助科学家识别数据差距并解释复杂的转化反应。 FAIR-TPs 网站可在 https://fairtps.lcsb.uni.lu 上公开访问。
