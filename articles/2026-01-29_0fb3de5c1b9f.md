## Abstract
arXiv:1905.01247v3 Announce Type: replace Abstract: In clean Dirac electron systems such as graphene, electron-electron interactions can dominate over other relaxation mechanisms such as phonon or impurity scattering. In this limit, collective electron dynamics can be described by hydrodynamic equations. The prerequisites for electron hydrodynamics have already been fulfilled in experiments, and signatures of hydrodynamic flow have been identified in transport measurements. Here, we derive the pressure-driven hydrodynamic flow profile across a de Laval nozzle profile for Dirac electrons in the subsonic and supersonic regimes. Based on this, we resolve the local voltage characteristics, which provide clear signatures of supersonic hydrodynamic flow. In particular, we identify two distinct features in the experimentally measurable potential profile: a pronounced asymmetry of the local voltage profile on opposite sides of the nozzle, and a sharp differential resistance signature induced by an electron shock wave on the exit side of the nozzle.

arXiv:1905.01247v3 公告类型：替换 摘要：在干净的狄拉克电子系统（例如石墨烯）中，电子-电子相互作用可以主导其他弛豫机制（例如声子或杂质散射）。在这个极限下，集体电子动力学可以用流体动力学方程来描述。电子流体动力学的先决条件已在实验中得到满足，并且流体动力学流动的特征已在输运测量中得到识别。在这里，我们推导出亚音速和超音速状态下狄拉克电子在拉瓦尔喷嘴轮廓上的压力驱动流体动力流动轮廓。在此基础上，我们解析了局部电压特性，从而提供了超音速流体动力流的清晰特征。特别是，我们在实验可测量的电势分布中发现了两个明显的特征：喷嘴相对侧的局部电压分布明显不对称，以及喷嘴出口侧的电子冲击波引起的尖锐的微分电阻特征。
