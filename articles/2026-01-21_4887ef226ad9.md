## Abstract
arXiv:2601.12717v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: Complex systems with nonreciprocal interactions are often stratified into layers. Ecosystems are a prime example, where species at one trophic level grow by consuming those at another. Yet the dynamical consequences of such stratified nonreciprocity -- where the correlation between growth and consumption differs across trophic levels -- remain unexplored. Here, using an ecological model with three trophic levels, we reveal an emergent asymmetry: nonreciprocal interactions between consumers and predators (top and middle level) destabilize ecosystems far more readily than nonreciprocity between consumers and resources (middle and bottom level). We analytically derive the phase diagram for the model and show that its stability boundary is controlled by energy flow across trophic levels. Because energy flows upward -- from resources to predators -- diversity is progressively lower at higher trophic levels, which we show explains the asymmetry. Lowering energy flow efficiency flips the asymmetry toward resources and remarkably expands the stable region of the phase diagram, suggesting that the famous "10% energy transfer" seen in natural ecosystems might promote stability. More broadly, our findings show that the location of nonreciprocity within a complex network, not merely its magnitude, determines stability.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.12717v1 公告类型：交叉 摘要：具有非交互作用的复杂系统通常会分层。生态系统就是一个典型的例子，其中一个营养级的物种通过消耗另一营养级的物种而生长。然而，这种分层非互惠性的动态后果（其中生长与消费之间的相关性因营养级别而异）仍未得到探索。在这里，使用具有三个营养级的生态模型，我们揭示了一种新兴的不对称性：消费者和捕食者（顶层和中层）之间的非互惠性相互作用比消费者和资源（中层和底层）之间的非互惠性更容易破坏生态系统的稳定。我们分析得出该模型的相图，并表明其稳定性边界是由跨营养级的能量流控制的。因为能量向上流动——从资源到捕食者——多样性在较高营养级逐渐降低，我们证明这解释了不对称性。降低能量流效率会翻转资源的不对称性，并显着扩大相图的稳定区域，这表明自然生态系统中著名的“10％能量转移”可能会促进稳定性。更广泛地说，我们的研究结果表明，复杂网络中非互惠性的位置（而不仅仅是其大小）决定了稳定性。
