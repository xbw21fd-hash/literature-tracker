## Abstract
arXiv:2602.10255v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Modular networks, such as critical infrastructures, are often built from distinct, densely connected modules (e.g., cities) that are sparsely interconnected. When such networks are gradually and randomly disrupted under a percolation process, they undergo two critical phase transitions. The first transition occurs when modules become isolated from one another, while the second corresponds to the collapse of the entire network, including the internal connectivity of the modules. Here, we study these phase transitions in modular multiplex networks and compare them with those observed in single-layer modular networks. We focus on models in which the modules are arranged and connected either as a Random Regular network or as a two-dimensional square lattice. We show here that these systems exhibit diverse transition behaviors, with some transitions occurring continuously and others abruptly; notably, one realistic model could display two distinct first-order transitions in the same system. For the modular Random Regular multiplex, we further characterize the spatial transition through its scaling behavior, revealing signatures of a mixed-order phase transitions. In addition, we analytically determine the critical threshold at which modules become disconnected. Our results highlight the crucial role of modular organization and the critical role of interdependence in shaping network vulnerabilities under failures.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.10255v1 公告类型：新 摘要：模块化网络（例如关键基础设施）通常是由稀疏互连的不同的、密集连接的模块（例如城市）构建的。当这种网络在渗滤过程中逐渐且随机地被破坏时，它们会经历两个关键的相变。第一个转变发生在模块彼此隔离时，而第二个转变对应于整个网络的崩溃，包括模块的内部连接。在这里，我们研究了模块化多路复用网络中的这些相变，并将它们与单层模块化网络中观察到的相变进行比较。我们专注于模块以随机规则网络或二维方格排列和连接的模型。我们在这里表明，这些系统表现出不同的转换行为，其中一些转换连续发生，而另一些则突然发生；值得注意的是，一个现实模型可以在同一系统中显示两个不同的一阶转变。对于模块化随机规则多重，我们通过其缩放行为进一步表征空间转变，揭示混合阶相变的特征。此外，我们通过分析确定模块断开连接的临界阈值。我们的结果强调了模块化组织的关键作用以及相互依赖在塑造故障下的网络漏洞方面的关键作用。
