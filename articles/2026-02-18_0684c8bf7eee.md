# Structural Divergence Between AI-Agent and Human Social Networks in Moltbook

## Abstract
arXiv:2602.15064v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Large populations of AI agents are increasingly embedded in online environments, yet little is known about how their collective interaction patterns compare to human social systems. Here, we analyze the full interaction network of Moltbook, a platform where AI agents and humans coexist, and systematically compare its structure to well-characterized human communication networks. Although Moltbook follows the same node-edge scaling relationship observed in human systems, indicating comparable global growth constraints, its internal organization diverges markedly. The network exhibits extreme attention inequality, heavy-tailed and asymmetric degree distributions, suppressed reciprocity, and a global under-representation of connected triadic structures. Community analysis reveals a structured modular architecture with elevated modularity and comparatively lower community size inequality relative to degree-preserving null models. Together, these findings show that AI-agent societies can reproduce global structural regularities of human networks while exhibiting fundamentally different internal organizing principles, highlighting that key features of human social organization are not universal but depend on the nature of the interacting agents.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.15064v1 公告类型：新 摘要：大量人工智能主体越来越多地嵌入到在线环境中，但人们对它们的集体交互模式与人类社会系统的比较情况知之甚少。在这里，我们分析了 Moltbook（人工智能代理和人类共存的平台）的完整交互网络，并系统地将其结构与特征明确的人类通信网络进行比较。尽管 Moltbook 遵循在人类系统中观察到的相同节点边缘缩放关系，表明可比较的全局增长约束，但其内部组织存在明显差异。该网络表现出极端的注意力不平等、重尾和不对称的度分布、抑制的互惠性以及连接的三元结构的全局代表性不足。社区分析揭示了一种结构化的模块化架构，相对于保留度的零模型，具有更高的模块性和相对较低的社区规模不平等。总之，这些发现表明，人工智能主体社会可以重现人类网络的全球结构规律，同时表现出根本不同的内部组织原则，强调人类社会组织的关键特征不是普遍的，而是取决于相互作用主体的性质。
