## Abstract
arXiv:2601.16738v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Molybdenum disulfide (MoS$_2$) is a prototypical layered transition-metal dichalcogenide whose electrocatalytic performance is governed by a delicate balance between crystallinity, defect density, and electronic conductivity. Here we report a systematic molecular beam epitaxy (MBE) study in which annealing temperature, deposition cycle number, and Mo/S thickness ratio were independently varied to control the structural and electronic properties of MoS$_2$ thin films. The successful epitaxial growth of atomically uniform MoS$_2$ directly on Si substrates enables strong interfacial coupling and efficient charge transfer, offering a viable route toward semiconductor-integrated catalytic architectures. X-ray diffraction, Raman spectroscopy, and X-ray absorption analyses reveal that higher annealing temperatures and excessive deposition cycles enhance crystallinity but reduce edge-site density and electrical conductivity, leading to diminished hydrogen evolution reaction (HER) activity. In contrast, intermediate cycle numbers and sulfur-deficient growth conditions yield heterostructures composed of MoS$_2$ with residual metallic Mo and sulfur vacancies, which activate otherwise inert basal planes while providing conductive pathways. These defect-engineered films deliver the best catalytic performance, achieving overpotentials as low as -0.33 V at -10 mA cm$^{-2}$, enlarged electrochemical surface area (ECSA) up to 8.0 cm$^2$, and mass-based turnover frequencies exceeding 23 mmol H$_2$ g$^{-1}$ s$^{-1}$, more than double those of stoichiometric counterparts. Our findings establish sulfur stoichiometry and growth kinetics as powerful levers to tune the interplay between structural order and catalytic activity in MBE-grown MoS$_2$ and point toward a broader strategy for engineering layered catalysts at the atomic scale.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.16738v1 公告类型：新 摘要：二硫化钼 (MoS$_2$) 是一种典型的层状过渡金属二硫属化物，其电催化性能由结晶度、缺陷密度和电子电导率之间的微妙平衡决定。在这里，我们报告了一项系统的分子束外延 (MBE) 研究，其中退火温度、沉积循环次数和 Mo/S 厚度比独立变化，以控制 MoS$_2$ 薄膜的结构和电子特性。原子均匀的 MoS$_2$ 直接在 Si 衬底上成功外延生长，实现了强大的界面耦合和高效的电荷转移，为半导体集成催化架构提供了一条可行的途径。 X 射线衍射、拉曼光谱和 X 射线吸收分析表明，较高的退火温度和过多的沉积循环会增强结晶度，但会降低边缘位点密度和电导率，从而导致析氢反应 (HER) 活性降低。相比之下，中间循环次数和缺硫生长条件产生由 MoS$_2$ 组成的异质结构，带有残留的金属 Mo 和硫空位，这会激活原本惰性的基面，同时提供导电通路。这些缺陷工程薄膜具有最佳的催化性能，在-10 mA cm$^{-2}$下实现低至-0.33 V的过电势，扩大电化学表面积（ECSA）高达8.0 cm$^2$，基于质量的周转频率超过23 mmol H$_2$ g$^{-1}$ s$^{-1}$，是化学计量同类产品的两倍多。我们的研究结果将硫化学计量和生长动力学作为调节 MBE 生长的 MoS$_2$ 结构顺序和催化活性之间相互作用的强大杠杆，并为在原子尺度上设计层状催化剂提供了更广泛的策略。
