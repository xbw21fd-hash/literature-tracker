# Measurement-induced crossover in quantum first-detection times

## Abstract
arXiv:2601.12102v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: The quantum first-detection problem concerns the statistics of the time at which a system, subject to repeated measurements, is observed in a prescribed target state for the first time. Unlike its classical counterpart, the measurement back action intrinsic to quantum mechanics may profoundly alter the system dynamics. Here we show that it induces a distinct change in the statistics of the first-detection time. For a quantum particle in one spatial dimension subject to stroboscopic measurements, we observe an algebraic decay of the probability of the first-detection time if the particle is free, an exponential decay in the presence of a confining potential, and a time-dependent crossover between these behaviors if the particle is partially confined. This crossover reflects the purely quantum nature of the detection process, which fundamentally distinguishes it from the first-passage problem in classical systems.

arXiv:2601.12102v1 公告类型：新 摘要：量子首次检测问题涉及系统经过重复测量后第一次在规定的目标状态下被观察到的时间的统计。与经典的对应物不同，量子力学固有的测量反作用可能会深刻地改变系统动力学。在这里，我们表明它会导致首次检测时间的统计数据发生明显变化。对于在一个空间维度上进行频闪测量的量子粒子，如果粒子是自由的，我们观察到首次检测时间的概率的代数衰减，在存在限制势的情况下观察到指数衰减，如果粒子被部分限制，则这些行为之间的依赖于时间的交叉。这种交叉反映了检测过程的纯粹量子性质，这从根本上区别于经典系统中的首次通过问题。
