## Abstract
arXiv:2602.15224v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Deep neural networks are strongly over-parameterized, often containing far more weights than required for their task. Although such redundancy can aid optimization, it leads to inefficient deployment and high computational cost, motivating model compression techniques. Among these, network pruning provides a clear and effective route to sparsity. We study pruning from a statistical-physics perspective, interpreting performance degradation under weight removal as a phase transition. Focusing on magnitude-based pruning with fine-tuning, we show that deep networks undergo a sharp transition from a cooperative, functional phase to a disordered phase with collapsed performance. This transition is characterized by scaling laws consistent with second-order critical behavior, with connectivity as the control parameter. Our findings suggest universal pruning-induced criticality across architectures and datasets. Finally, we show that there exists a large class of subnetworks sharing the same nodes' degrees with similar learning ability, thus linking model performance to its topological properties.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.15224v1 公告类型：新 摘要：深度神经网络严重过度参数化，通常包含的权重远远超过其任务所需的权重。尽管这种冗余可以帮助优化，但它会导致部署效率低下和计算成本高昂，从而激发了模型压缩技术的发展。其中，网络剪枝为稀疏性提供了一条清晰有效的途径。我们从统计物理学的角度研究修剪，将重量去除下的性能下降解释为相变。专注于基于幅度的修剪和微调，我们表明深度网络经历了从合作、功能阶段到性能崩溃的无序阶段的急剧转变。这种转变的特点是与二阶临界行为一致的缩放定律，以连通性作为控制参数。我们的研究结果表明，剪枝引起的跨架构和数据集的关键性是普遍存在的。最后，我们表明存在一大类共享相同节点度且具有相似学习能力的子网络，从而将模型性能与其拓扑特性联系起来。
