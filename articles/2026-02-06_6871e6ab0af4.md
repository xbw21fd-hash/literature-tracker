## Abstract
arXiv:2602.05836v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: In response to the 2008 global financial crisis, Science Foundation Ireland (SFI), now Research Ireland, pivoted to research with potential socioeconomic impact. Given that the latter can encompass higher technology readiness levels, which typically correlates with lower academic impact, it is interesting to understand how academic impact holds up in SFI funded research. Here we decompose SFI \textit{Investigator Awards} - arguably the most academic funding call - into $3,243$ constituent publications and field weighted citation impact (FWCI) values searchable in the SCOPUS database. Given that citation counts are skewed, we highlight the limitation of FWCI as a paper metric, which naively restricts one to comparisons of average FWCI ($\overline{\mathrm{FWCI}}$) in large samples. Neglecting publications with $\textrm{FWCI} < 0.1$ ($8.8\%$), SFI funded publications are well approximated by a lognormal distribution with $\mu = -0.0761^{+0.017}_{-0.0039}$ and $ \sigma = 0.933^{+0.011}_{-0.012}$ at $95 \%$ confidence level. This equates to an $\overline{\mathrm{FWCI}} = 1.433^{+0.029}_{-0.015}$ well above $\overline{\mathrm{FWCI}}=1$ internationally. Broken down by award, we correct $\overline{\mathrm{FWCI}}$ for small samples using simulations and find $\sim 67\%$ exceed \textit{median} international academic interest, thus exhibiting a positive correlation between the potential for socioeconomic impact and academic interest.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.05836v1 公告类型：交叉 摘要：为了应对 2008 年全球金融危机，爱尔兰科学基金会 (SFI)（现为爱尔兰研究中心）转向具有潜在社会经济影响的研究。鉴于后者可能包含较高的技术准备水平，而这通常与较低的学术影响力相关，因此了解 SFI 资助的研究如何保持学术影响力是很有趣的。在这里，我们将 SFI \textit{研究者奖}（可以说是最具学术性的资助呼吁）分解为 3,243 美元的组成出版物和可在 SCOPUS 数据库中搜索的领域加权引用影响力 (FWCI) 值。鉴于引用计数存在偏差，我们强调 FWCI 作为一种论文指标的局限性，它天真地限制了对大样本中平均 FWCI ($\overline{\mathrm{FWCI}}$) 的比较。忽略 $\textrm{FWCI} < 0.1$ ($8.8\%$) 的出版物，SFI 资助的出版物很好地近似于对数正态分布，$\mu = -0.0761^{+0.017}_{-0.0039}$ 和 $ \sigma = 0.933^{+0.011}_{-0.012}$ 在 $95 \%$置信水平。这相当于国际上 $\overline{\mathrm{FWCI}} = 1.433^{+0.029}_{-0.015}$ 远高于 $\overline{\mathrm{FWCI}}=1$。按奖项细分，我们使用模拟修正了小样本的$\overline{\mathrm{FWCI}}$，发现$\sim 67\%$超过\textit{median}国际学术兴趣，从而表现出社会经济影响潜力与学术兴趣之间的正相关关系。
