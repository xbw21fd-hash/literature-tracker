## Abstract
Over the past few decades, molecular dynamics simulations and first-principles calculations have become two major approaches to predict the lattice thermal conductivity ($kappa$L), which are however limited by insufficient accuracy and high computational cost, respectively. To overcome such inherent disadvantages, machine learning (ML) has been successfully used to accurately predict $kappa$L in a high-throughput style. In this review, we give some introductions of recent ML works on the direct and indirect prediction of $kappa$L, where the derivations and applications of data-driven models are discussed in details. A brief summary of current works and future perspectives are given in the end.
