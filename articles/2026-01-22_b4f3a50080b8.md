## Abstract
arXiv:2503.12235v2 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: Solar flares are the most powerful, magnetically driven, explosions in the heliosphere. The nature of magnetic energy release in the solar corona that heats the plasma and accelerates particles in a flare, however, remains poorly understood. Here, we report high-resolution coronal observations of a flare by the Solar Orbiter mission that reveal initially weaker but rapid reconnection events, on timescales of a few seconds at most, leading to a more prominent activity of a similar nature that explosively causes a flare. Signatures of this process are further imprinted on the widespread raining plasma blobs with short lifetimes, giving rise to the characteristic ribbon-like emission pattern associated with the flare. Our observations unveil the central engine of a flare and emphasize the crucial role of an avalanche-like magnetic energy release mechanism at work.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2503.12235v2 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：太阳耀斑是日光层中最强大的磁驱动爆炸。然而，日冕中加热等离子体并加速耀斑中的粒子的磁能释放的性质仍然知之甚少。在这里，我们报告了太阳轨道飞行器任务对耀斑的高分辨率日冕观测，这些观测揭示了最初较弱但快速的重新连接事件，最多在几秒钟的时间尺度上，导致了类似性质的更显着的活动，从而爆炸性地引起耀斑。这一过程的特征进一步印在广泛分布的寿命较短的雨状等离子体斑点上，从而产生与耀斑相关的特征性带状发射图案。我们的观察揭示了耀斑的中央引擎，并强调了类似雪崩的磁能释放机制在工作中的关键作用。
