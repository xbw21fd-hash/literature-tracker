## Abstract
arXiv:2512.24431v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Scanning Tunneling Microscopy (STM) enables fabrication of atomically precise structures with unique properties and growing technological potential. However, reproducible manipulation of covalently bonded atoms requires control over the atomic configuration of both sample and probe - a longstanding challenge in STM. Here, we introduce inverted-mode STM, an approach that enables mechanically controlled chemical reactions for atomically precise fabrication. Tailored molecules on a Si(100) surface image the probe apex, and the usual challenge of understanding the probe structure is effectively solved. The molecules can also react with the probe, with the two sides of the tunnel junction acting as reagents positioned with sub-angstrom precision. This allows abstraction or donation of atoms from or to the probe apex. We demonstrate this by using a novel alkynyl-terminated molecule to reproducibly abstract hydrogen atoms from the probe. The approach is expected to extend to other elements and moieties, opening a new avenue for scalable atomically precise fabrication.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.24431v1 公告类型：新 摘要：扫描隧道显微镜 (STM) 能够制造具有独特性能和不断增长的技术潜力的原子级精确结构。然而，共价键合原子的可重复操作需要控制样品和探针的原子构型——这是 STM 中长期存在的挑战。在这里，我们介绍倒置模式 STM，这是一种能够实现机械控制化学反应以实现原子级精确制造的方法。 Si(100) 表面上的定制分子对探针顶点进行成像，有效解决了理解探针结构的常见挑战。这些分子还可以与探针发生反应，隧道结的两侧充当以亚埃精度定位的试剂。这允许从探针顶点提取原子或向探针顶点捐赠原子。我们通过使用一种新型的炔基封端分子可重复地从探针中提取氢原子来证明这一点。该方法预计将扩展到其他元素和部分，为可扩展的原子精确制造开辟一条新途径。
