## Abstract
arXiv:2602.01788v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Hybrid systems consisting of metal-fullerene composites exhibit intriguing properties but often suffer from thermal instability. With proper control, such instability can be harnessed to enable the formation of sophisticated nanostructures with nanometric precision. These self-organization phenomena are not limited to thermal stimulation alone but can also be triggered by other external stimuli. In this work, we investigate the morphological evolution of thin films composed of evaporated C60 and sputtered nickel mixtures, focusing on how external stimuli influence both their structural and electrical properties. Thin films were prepared under controlled deposition conditions, and their surface morphology was analyzed using advanced characterization techniques. Progressive changes in film morphology were observed as a function of composition and external treatment, highlighting the interplay between metallic and molecular components. In particular, it was observed that, due to the annealing treatment, the sample undergoes strong phase separation, with the formation of structures tens of microns in diameter and an increase in electrical resistance, exhibiting insulating behavior. These findings provide insights into the mechanisms governing hybrid thin film formation and suggest potential applications in electronic, optoelectronic, and energy-related devices.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.01788v1 公告类型：新 摘要：由金属-富勒烯复合材料组成的混合系统表现出有趣的特性，但经常遭受热不稳定的困扰。通过适当的控制，可以利用这种不稳定性来形成具有纳米精度的复杂纳米结构。这些自组织现象不仅限于热刺激，还可以由其他外部刺激触发。在这项工作中，我们研究了由蒸发的 C60 和溅射的镍混合物组成的薄膜的形态演变，重点关注外部刺激如何影响其结构和电性能。在受控沉积条件下制备薄膜，并使用先进的表征技术分析其表面形态。观察到薄膜形态随着成分和外部处理的变化而逐渐变化，突出了金属和分子成分之间的相互作用。特别是，观察到，由于退火处理，样品发生强烈的相分离，形成直径数十微米的结构，电阻增加，表现出绝缘行为。这些发现提供了对混合薄膜形成机制的见解，并提出了在电子、光电和能源相关设备中的潜在应用。
