## Abstract
arXiv:2602.15442v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Ultrasound-matter interactions underpin numerous biomedical and soft-matter applications, yet simulating these phenomena is challenging due to the large separation of viscous and sonic time scales. Continuum methods capture large-scale wave propagation but cannot resolve microscale interactions, while particle-based approaches offer molecular resolution but struggle with efficiency and stability at larger scales. We introduce a particle-based virtual ultrasound machine that uses a novel smoothed dissipative particle dynamics variant with an implicit pressure solver and a negative-pressure stabilization scheme, required to mimic acoustic propagation across MHz-GHz frequencies. We demonstrate its capabilities by modeling the acoustophoresis of encapsulated microbubbles, a key mechanism in ultrasound-mediated drug delivery. Beyond this application, the approach establishes a generalizable platform for simulating wave-matter interactions in soft and biological materials, opening new directions for computational studies of acoustics-driven phenomena in science and engineering.

arXiv:2602.15442v1 公告类型：新 摘要：超声波与物质的相互作用支撑着众多生物医学和软物质应用，但由于粘性和声波时间尺度的巨大分离，模拟这些现象具有挑战性。连续体方法捕获大规模波传播，但无法解决微观相互作用，而基于粒子的方法提供分子分辨率，但在更大尺度上难以提高效率和稳定性。我们引入了一种基于粒子的虚拟超声机，它使用一种新颖的平滑耗散粒子动力学变体，具有隐式压力求解器和负压稳定方案，需要模拟 MHz-GHz 频率的声学传播。我们通过模拟封装微泡的声泳来展示其功能，这是超声介导的药物输送的关键机制。除了这一应用之外，该方法还建立了一个通用平台，用于模拟软材料和生物材料中的波与物质相互作用，为科学和工程中声学驱动现象的计算研究开辟了新的方向。
