## Abstract
arXiv:2601.18780v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Monte Carlo simulations of optical photon transport are computationally prohibitive for large-scale optical systems including detector arrays and PET systems, restricting practical use to single-crystal studies. This work presents an enhanced conditional generative adversarial network (optiGAN) replacing optical simulations at the crystal array level, extending our single-crystal approach to a 3x3 BGO array. We enhance the Wasserstein-GAN framework with Fourier feature encoding, a learnable latent mapping network, and a physics-informed loss enforcing momentum conservation. Training data is reduced eight-fold by exploiting symmetry. Evaluation employs three studies: a full array evaluation testing generalization from the fundamental domain to the complete geometry, a high-resolution study probing out-of-distribution generalization to untrained positions, and a pencil beam $\gamma$-photon study assessing practical applicability for experimental detector characterization. Performance is benchmarked against GATE10/Geant4 ground truth, using intrinsic fluctuations between independent Monte Carlo runs as baseline. OptiGAN achieves sliced Wasserstein similarity within 3$\sigma$-agreement of the baseline across all conditions, demonstrating successful generalization to the full array. The model transitions from electron-emission training data to realistic $\gamma$-photon interactions, producing flood maps that reproduce characteristic patterns including photopeak clusters and inter-crystal scatter lines. This proof-of-concept demonstrates that physics-informed generative models can accurately simulate optical photon transport in segmented scintillator arrays. The reproduction of experimentally relevant flood map features validates optiGAN for PET detector development and establishes a foundation for models generalizing across diverse array configurations.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.18780v1 公告类型：新 摘要：光学光子输运的蒙特卡罗模拟对于包括探测器阵列和 PET 系统在内的大规模光学系统来说计算量巨大，限制了单晶研究的实际应用。这项工作提出了一种增强的条件生成对抗网络 (optiGAN)，取代了晶体阵列级别的光学模拟，将我们的单晶方法扩展到 3x3 BGO 阵列。我们通过傅里叶特征编码、可学习的潜在映射网络和强制动量守恒的物理通知损失增强了 Wasserstein-GAN 框架。通过利用对称性，训练数据减少了八倍。评估采用三项研究：全阵列评估测试从基本域到完整几何形状的泛化，高分辨率研究探测分布外泛化到未经训练的位置，以及笔形束$\gamma$-光子研究评估实验探测器表征的实际适用性。性能以 GATE10/Geant4 地面实况为基准，使用独立蒙特卡罗运行之间的内在波动作为基线。 OptiGAN 在所有条件下都在基线的 3$\sigma$ 协议内实现了切片 Wasserstein 相似性，证明了对整个数组的成功泛化。该模型从电子发射训练数据过渡到真实的 $\gamma$-光子相互作用，生成可再现特征图案（包括光峰簇和晶体间散射线）的洪水图。这一概念验证表明，基于物理的生成模型可以准确模拟分段闪烁体阵列中的光学光子传输。实验相关洪水图特征的再现验证了 optiGAN 用于 PET 探测器开发的能力，并为泛化不同阵列配置的模型奠定了基础。
