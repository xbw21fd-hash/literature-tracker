## Abstract
arXiv:2508.06545v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Precise localisation of gamma-ray interactions is crucial for the performance of the Advanced GAmma Tracking Array (AGATA). The Pulse Shape Analysis (PSA) method used for the position estimation of gamma-ray interactions relies on a simulated signal database. The Pulse Shape Comparison Scanning (PSCS) method was used to scan AGATA crystals in order to produce an experimental database of signals. This paper presents a novel approach using Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) neural networks to determine the 3D interaction position of gamma rays within AGATA crystals, trained on data from IPHC Strasbourg, allowing for the construction of an experimental database. A custom masked loss function is introduced to enable training with incomplete position information. The database generated by this new method outperforms the existing simulated database, and the experimental database obtained from the conventional PSCS algorithm.

arXiv:2508.06545v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：伽马射线相互作用的精确定位对于高级伽马跟踪阵列 (AGATA) 的性能至关重要。用于伽马射线相互作用位置估计的脉冲形状分析（PSA）方法依赖于模拟信号数据库。使用脉冲形状比较扫描 (PSCS) 方法扫描 AGATA 晶体，以生成信号的实验数据库。本文提出了一种使用长短期记忆 (LSTM) 神经网络来确定 AGATA 晶体内伽马射线的 3D 相互作用位置的新颖方法，并根据 IPHC 斯特拉斯堡的数据进行训练，从而可以构建实验数据库。引入了自定义屏蔽损失函数，以便能够使用不完整的位置信息进行训练。这种新方法生成的数据库优于现有的模拟数据库和传统 PSCS 算法获得的实验数据库。
