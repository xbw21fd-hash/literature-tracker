## Abstract
arXiv:2601.18719v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Today, apart from some isolated R&D efforts, there are no gravitational wave (GW) experiments, yet which explore a large part of the vast frequency range above the LIGO/Virgo band. It is planned to establish an experiment at Deutsches Elektronen-Synchrotron (DESY) and at the Superconducting Quantum Materials and Systems (SQMS) Center at Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory (Fermilab) to search for high-frequency GWs in the frequency range of 10 kHz to 100 MHz. The basic idea is to use superconducting radiofrequency (SRF) cavities to detect tiny harmonic deformations induced by GWs which change the boundary conditions of the oscillating electromagnetic field. This paper summarizes the challenging environmental boundary requirements, and the R&D to operate a cavity using a low level RF (LLRF) system which pushes beyond state-of-the-art accuracy and resolutions and a seismic noise mitigated cryostat at 1.8 K. The focus of this paper is the warm and cold commissioning of a prototype cavity, built 20 years ago during the MAGO collaboration, and its first measurement in our collaborative research project.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.18719v1 公告类型：新 摘要：如今，除了一些孤立的研发工作外，还没有引力波 (GW) 实验探索 LIGO/Virgo 频段以上广阔频率范围的很大一部分。计划在德国电子同步加速器（DESY）和费米国家加速器实验室（费米实验室）的超导量子材料与系统（SQMS）中心建立一个实验，以寻找10 kHz至100 MHz频率范围内的高频引力波。其基本思想是使用超导射频（SRF）腔来检测由引力波引起的微小谐波变形，这些变形会改变振荡电磁场的边界条件。本文总结了具有挑战性的环境边界要求，以及使用低电平射频 (LLRF) 系统操作腔体的研发，该系统超越了最先进的精度和分辨率，以及 1.8 K 的地震噪声减轻低温恒温器。本文的重点是原型腔体的热和冷调试，该原型腔体是 20 年前在 MAGO 合作期间建造的，也是我们合作研究项目中的首次测量。
