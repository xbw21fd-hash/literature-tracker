## Abstract
arXiv:2602.05142v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Understanding how pedestrians adjust their movement when interacting with autonomous vehicles (AVs) is essential for improving safety in mixed traffic. This study examines micro-level pedestrian behaviour during midblock encounters in the NuScenes dataset using a hybrid discrete choice-machine learning framework based on the Residual Logit (ResLogit) model. The model incorporates temporal, spatial, kinematic, and perceptual indicators. These include relative speed, visual looming, remaining distance, and directional collision risk proximity (CRP) measures. Results suggest that some of these variables may meaningfully influence movement adjustments, although predictive performance remains moderate. Marginal effects and elasticities indicate strong directional asymmetries in risk perception, with frontal and rear CRP showing opposite influences. The remaining distance exhibits a possible mid-crossing threshold. Relative speed cues appear to have a comparatively less effect. These patterns may reflect multiple behavioural tendencies driven by both risk perception and movement efficiency.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.05142v1 公告类型：新 摘要：了解行人在与自动驾驶车辆 (AV) 交互时如何调整其运动对于提高混合交通中的安全性至关重要。本研究使用基于残差 Logit (ResLogit) 模型的混合离散选择机器学习框架，在 NuScenes 数据集中检查了街区中段遭遇时的微观行人行为。该模型结合了时间、空间、运动学和感知指标。其中包括相对速度、视觉隐约、剩余距离和定向碰撞风险接近度 (CRP) 测量。结果表明，尽管预测性能仍然中等，但其中一些变量可能会对运动调整产生有意义的影响。边际效应和弹性表明风险感知存在强烈的方向不对称性，前部和后部 CRP 显示出相反的影响。剩余距离呈现出可能的中间交叉阈值。相对速度提示的影响似乎相对较小。这些模式可能反映了由风险认知和行动效率驱动的多种行为倾向。
