## Abstract
arXiv:2601.17268v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Accurate short-term prediction of clouds and precipitation is critical for severe weather warnings, aviation safety, and renewable energy operations. Forecasts at this timescale are provided by numerical weather models and extrapolation methods, both of which have limitations. Mesoscale numerical weather prediction models provide skillful forecasts at these scales but require significant modeling expertise and computational infrastructure, which limits their accessibility. Extrapolation-based methods are computationally lightweight but degrade rapidly beyond 1-2 hours. This presents an opportunity for data-driven forecasting directly from observations using geostationary satellites and ground-based radar, which provide high-frequency, high-resolution observations that capture mesoscale atmospheric evolution. We introduce Stormscope, a family of transformer-based generative diffusion models trained on high-resolution, multi-band geostationary satellite imagery and ground-based weather radar over the continental United States. Stormscope produces forecasts at a temporal resolution of 10 minutes and 6 kilometer spatial resolution, which are competitive with state-of-the-art mesoscale NWP models for lead times up to 6 hours. Its generative architecture enables large ensemble forecasts of explicit mesoscale dynamics for robust uncertainty quantification. Evaluated against extrapolation methods and operational mesoscale NWP models, Stormscope achieves leading performance on standard deterministic and probabilistic verification metrics across forecast horizons from 1 to 6 hours. As Stormscope relies on globally available satellite observations (and radar where available), it offers a pathway to extend skillful mesoscale forecasting to oceanic regions and countries without strong operational mesoscale modeling programs.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.17268v1 公告类型：新 摘要：准确的短期云和降水预测对于恶劣天气预警、航空安全和可再生能源运营至关重要。此时间尺度的预报是通过数值天气模型和外推方法提供的，但这两种方法都有局限性。中尺度数值天气预报模型可以在这些尺度上提供熟练的预报，但需要大量的建模专业知识和计算基础设施，这限制了它们的可访问性。基于外推法的方法计算量小，但超过 1-2 小时后性能会迅速下降。这为直接利用对地静止卫星和地面雷达的观测进行数据驱动的预测提供了机会，这些观测提供了捕捉中尺度大气演化的高频、高分辨率观测结果。我们介绍 Stormscope，这是一系列基于变压器的生成扩散模型，在美国大陆上空的高分辨率、多波段对地静止卫星图像和地面气象雷达上进行训练。 Stormscope 能够以 10 分钟的时间分辨率和 6 公里的空间分辨率进行预报，与最先进的中尺度 NWP 模型相比，交付时间长达 6 小时。其生成架构能够对显式中尺度动力学进行大型集合预测，以实现稳健的不确定性量化。根据外推方法和可操作的中尺度 NWP 模型进行评估，Stormscope 在 1 至 6 小时的预测范围内的标准确定性和概率验证指标方面取得了领先的性能。由于 Stormscope 依赖于全球可用的卫星观测（以及可用的雷达），因此它提供了一种将熟练的中尺度预报扩展到没有强大的可操作中尺度建模程序的海洋地区和国家的途径。
