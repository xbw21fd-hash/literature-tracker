## Abstract
arXiv:2602.02756v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: The miniaturization of mechanical mechanisms is crucial to enable the development of compact, high-performance micromachines. However, the downscaling actuation of conventional gears and micromotors has remained limited by the inherent challenges of implementing mechanical/electrical powering. Here, we present the design, fabrication, and characterization of an optomechanical, gear-driven micromachine realized through two-photon polymerization 3D printing. The actuation is achieved using optical tweezers. The device integrates a microgear transmission system with an optically actuated part, enabling light-controlled micromachines. When illuminated by a highly focused laser source, the first gear generates rotational torque within the gear assembly, converting optical energy into directional mechanical work that can be transmitted to the coupled gear. We demonstrate the fabrication of micromachines using two-photon polymerization (2PP) laser writing, enabling the fabrication of spur gear trains and bevel gears that can produce out-of-plane rotations, which is not achievable with traditional micromachining fabrication techniques. The micromachines are composed of a single gear or a train of two or three gears without any unwanted adhesion between the components, leading to functioning systems. Experimentally, the fabricated micromachines were actuated using optical tweezers, demonstrating continuous gear rotation, effective motion transmission in gear trains, out-of-plane rotations, and the ability to amplify velocity or torque. Optical-tweezer actuation broadens the potential applications of these micromachines, particularly in biomedical and lab-on-a-chip systems, where precise, minimally invasive control at the microscale is essential.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.02756v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：机械机构的小型化对于开发紧凑、高性能的微型机械至关重要。然而，传统齿轮和微型电机的小型化驱动仍然受到实现机械/电力驱动的固有挑战的限制。在这里，我们展示了通过双光子聚合 3D 打印实现的光机械、齿轮驱动微型机器的设计、制造和表征。使用光镊实现驱动。该设备将微型齿轮传输系统与光学驱动部件集成在一起，从而实现光控微型机器。当受到高度聚焦的激光源照射时，第一个齿轮在齿轮组件内产生旋转扭矩，将光能转换成可传输到耦合齿轮的定向机械功。我们演示了使用双光子聚合（2PP）激光写入的微机械制造，能够制造能够产生平面外旋转的正齿轮系和锥齿轮，这是传统微机械制造技术无法实现的。微型机器由单个齿轮或两个或三个齿轮组成，部件之间没有任何不必要的粘附，从而形成正常运行的系统。实验上，使用光镊驱动所制造的微型机器，展示了连续的齿轮旋转、齿轮系中的有效运动传输、平面外旋转以及放大速度或扭矩的能力。光镊驱动拓宽了这些微型机器的潜在应用，特别是在生物医学和芯片实验室系统中，在这些系统中，微尺度的精确、微创控制至关重要。
