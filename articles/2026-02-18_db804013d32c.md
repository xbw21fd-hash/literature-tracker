## Abstract
arXiv:2510.25520v3 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: The stereotypical reaching motion of the octopus arm has drawn growing attention for its efficient control of a highly deformable body. Previous studies suggest that its characteristic bend propagation may share underlying principles with the dynamics of a whip. This work investigates whether whip-like passive dynamics in water can reproduce the kinematic features observed in biological reaching and their similarities and differences. Platform-based whipping tests were performed in water and air while systematically varying material stiffness and driving speed. Image-based quantification revealed that the Ecoflex Gel 2 arm driven at 150 rpm (motor speed) reproduced curvature propagation similar to that observed in octopus reaching. However, its bend-point velocity decreased monotonically rather than exhibiting the biological bell-shaped profile, confirming that the octopus reaching movement is not merely a passive whipping behavior. The absence of propagation in air further highlights the critical role of the surrounding medium in forming octopus-like reaching motion. This study provides a new perspective for understand biological reaching movement, and offers a potential platform for future hydrodynamic research.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2510.25520v3 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：章鱼手臂的典型伸展动作因其对高度变形身体的有效控制而受到越来越多的关注。先前的研究表明，其特征弯曲传播可能与鞭子的动力学具有共同的基本原理。这项工作研究了水中鞭状被动动力学是否可以重现生物到达时观察到的运动学特征及其异同。基于平台的鞭打测试在水中和空气中进行，同时系统地改变材料刚度和行驶速度。基于图像的量化显示，以 150 rpm（电机速度）驱动的 Ecoflex Gel 2 臂再现了类似于章鱼伸展时观察到的曲率传播。然而，它的弯曲点速度单调下降，而不是表现出生物钟形轮廓，证实章鱼的到达运动不仅仅是一种被动的鞭打行为。空气中传播的缺失进一步凸显了周围介质在形成章鱼状到达运动中的关键作用。这项研究为理解生物到达运动提供了新的视角，并为未来的流体动力学研究提供了潜在的平台。
