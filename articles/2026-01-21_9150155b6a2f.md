# Counting unlabelled multigraphs with three nodes

## Abstract
arXiv:2601.13163v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Unlabeled multigraphs have diverse applications across scientific fields, from transportation and social networks to polymer physics. In particular, multigraphs are essential for studying the relationship between the spatial organization and biological function of chromatin, which is often folded into complex polymer networks whose structure is closely tied to patterns of gene expression. A fundamental yet challenging aspect in applying graph theory to these areas is the enumeration of multigraphs, especially under structural constraints For example, when coupled with the statistical mechanics of polymer networks, the ability to identify traversable and connected multigraphs provides powerful tools for predicting statistically favored motifs that may arise within chromatin networks. In this work, by counting the adjacency matrices, we derive polynomial expressions that enumerate all connected, undirected, and unlabeled multigraphs with three nodes and fixed degree, and provide a method to efficiently generate them.

arXiv:2601.13163v1 公告类型：新 摘要：未标记的多重图在科学领域具有多种应用，从交通和社交网络到聚合物物理学。特别是，多重图对于研究染色质的空间组织和生物功能之间的关系至关重要，染色质通常折叠成复杂的聚合物网络，其结构与基因表达模式密切相关。将图论应用于这些领域的一个基本但具有挑战性的方面是多重图的枚举，特别是在结构约束下。例如，当与聚合物网络的统计力学相结合时，识别可遍历和连接的多重图的能力为预测染色质网络中可能出现的统计上有利的基序提供了强大的工具。在这项工作中，通过计算邻接矩阵，我们推导了多项式表达式，该多项式表达式枚举了具有三个节点和固定次数的所有连通的、无向的和无标记的多重图，并提供了一种有效生成它们的方法。
