## Abstract
arXiv:2512.06189v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Agent-based models (ABMs) have emerged as distinguished tools for epidemic modeling due to their ability to capture detailed human contact patterns and can, thus, support decision-makers in times of outbreaks and epidemics. However, as a result of missing correspondingly resolved data transmission events are often modeled based on simplified assumptions. In this article, we present a framework to assess the impact of these simplifications on epidemic prediction outcomes, considering superspreading and workplace transmission events. We couple the VADERE microsimulation model with the large-scale MEmilio-ABM and compare the outcomes of four outbreak events after 10 days of simulation in a synthetic city district generated from German census data. In a restaurant superspreading event, where up to four households share tables, we observe 17.2 % more infections on day 10 after the outbreak. The difference increases to 46.0 % more infections when using the simplified initialization in a setting where only two households share tables. We observe similar outcomes (41.3 % vs. 9.3 % more infections) for two workplace settings with different mixing patterns between teams at work. In addition to the aggregated difference, we show differences in spatial dynamics and transmission trees obtained with complete or reduced outbreak information. We observe differences between simplified and fully detailed initializations that become more pronounced when the subnetworks in the outbreak setting are mixing less. In consequence and aside from classical calibration of models, the significant outcome differences should drive us to develop a more profound understanding of how and where simplified assumptions about transmission events are adequate.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.06189v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：基于代理的模型（ABM）因其能够捕获详细的人类接触模式而成为流行病建模的杰出工具，因此可以在疫情爆发和流行病时为决策者提供支持。然而，由于缺少相应解析的数据，传输事件通常基于简化的假设来建模。在本文中，我们提出了一个框架来评估这些简化对流行病预测结果的影响，考虑超级传播和工作场所传播事件。我们将 VADERE 微观模拟模型与大规模 MEmilio-ABM 结合起来，并在根据德国人口普查数据生成的合成城区模拟 10 天后，比较四次爆发事件的结果。在一次餐厅超级传播事件中，多达四个家庭共享餐桌，我们观察到疫情爆发后第 10 天感染人数增加了 17.2%。在只有两个家庭共享餐桌的环境中使用简化初始化时，感染率差异增加到 46.0%。我们在工作团队之间混合模式不同的两个工作场所环境中观察到相似的结果（感染率分别为 41.3% 和 9.3%）。除了聚合差异之外，我们还显示了通过完整或减少的疫情信息获得的空间动态和传播树的差异。我们观察到简化初始化和完全详细初始化之间的差异，当爆发环境中的子网络混合较少时，这种差异变得更加明显。因此，除了模型的经典校准之外，显着的结果差异应该促使我们更深刻地理解关于传输事件的简化假设如何以及在何处是足够的。
