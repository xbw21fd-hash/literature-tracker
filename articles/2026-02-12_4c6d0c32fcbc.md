## Abstract
arXiv:2602.10174v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: We introduce a multiscale measure of network instability based on the joint use of Detrended Cross-Correlation Analysis (DCCA) and Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) filtering. The proposed metric, the Elastic Detrended Cross-Correlation Ratio (Elastic DCCR), is defined as a finite-difference measure of the logarithmic sensitivity of the average MST length to the observation scale. It captures how the structure of cross-correlation networks deforms across different investment horizons. When applied to a network of global equity indices, the Elastic DCCR rises sharply during episodes of financial stress, reflecting increased short-term coordination among investors and a contraction of correlation distances. The measure reveals scale-dependent reconfigurations in network topology that are not visible in single-scale analyses, and highlights clear differences between stressed and stable market regimes. The approach does not assume covariance stationarity and relies only on scale-dependent detrended correlations; as a result, it is broadly applicable to other complex systems in which interaction strength varies with scale.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.10174v1 公告类型：新 摘要：我们引入了一种基于联合使用去趋势互相关分析 (DCCA) 和最小生成树 (MST) 过滤的网络不稳定的多尺度度量。所提出的指标“弹性去趋势互相关比”(Elastic DCCR) 被定义为平均 MST 长度对观测尺度的对数灵敏度的有限差分度量。它捕捉了互相关网络的结构在不同投资期限内如何变形。当应用于全球股票指数网络时，弹性 DCCR 在金融压力时期急剧上升，反映出投资者之间短期协调的增强和相关距离的缩小。该措施揭示了网络拓扑中与规模相关的重新配置，这在单尺度分析中是不可见的，并强调了压力市场体制和稳定市场体制之间的明显差异。该方法不假设协方差平稳性，仅依赖于尺度相关的去趋势相关性；因此，它广泛适用于相互作用强度随尺度变化的其他复杂系统。
