## Abstract
arXiv:2502.12020v3 Announce Type: replace Abstract: We numerically demonstrate a network of coupled oscillators that can learn to solve a classification task from a set of examples -- performing both training and inference through the nonlinear evolution of the system. We accomplish this by combining three key elements to achieve learning: A long- term memory that stores learned responses, analogous to the synapses in biological brains; a short- term memory that stores the neural activations, similar to the firing patterns of neurons; and an evolution law that updates the synapses in response to novel examples, inspired by synaptic plasticity. Achieving all three elements in wave-based information processors such as metamaterials is a significant challenge. Here, we solve it by leveraging the material multistability to implement long-term memory, and harnessing symmetries and thermal noise to realize the learning rule. Our analysis reveals that the learning mechanism, although inspired by synaptic plasticity, also shares parallelisms with bacterial evolution strategies, where mutation rates increase in the presence of noxious stimuli.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2502.12020v3 公告类型：替换 摘要：我们以数值方式演示了一个耦合振荡器网络，该网络可以从一组示例中学习解决分类任务 - 通过系统的非线性演化执行训练和推理。我们通过结合三个关键要素来实现学习来实现这一目标：存储学到的反应的长期记忆，类似于生物大脑中的突触；存储神经激活的短期记忆，类似于神经元的放电模式；受突触可塑性启发，根据新的例子更新突触的进化法则。在基于波的信息处理器（例如超材料）中实现所有三个要素是一项重大挑战。在这里，我们通过利用材料的多稳定性来实现长期记忆，并利用对称性和热噪声来实现学习规则来解决这个问题。我们的分析表明，这种学习机制虽然受到突触可塑性的启发，但也与细菌进化策略有相似之处，即在有害刺激下突变率会增加。
