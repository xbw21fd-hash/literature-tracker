## Abstract
arXiv:2511.16474v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Plastic inorganic semiconductors are promising candidates for high-performance stable flexible electronics. Germanium-based chalcogenide materials are well-known for excellent semiconducting properties, notably superior carrier mobility. However, the intrinsic half-bond nature of metavalent bonds between Ge atoms and chalcogens accounts for their inherent brittleness. Here, we report an ultra-high plasticity germanium-based chalcogenide of GaGeTe. GaGeTe single crystals can sustain a large engineering strain up to 7.5% without fracture. Through a comprehensive multi-scale investigation ranging from macroscopic characterization to atomic-resolution transmission electron microscopy, we reveal that the exceptional plasticity is due to a synergistic effect of interlayer gliding and intralayer lattice distortion. DFT calculations further elucidate the chemical origin. The existence of Ga atoms avoids the formation of brittle metavalent Ge-Te bonds. Instead, a robust honeycomb lattice formed by sp3-hybridized covalent Ga-Te, Ge-Ge, and Ga-Ge bonds preserves high bond strength under large tensile strains in simulations, thereby underpinning the macroscopic plastic behavior. This systematic study on the origin of the plasticity in GaGeTe introduces bond engineering as a potential approach for constructing plastic inorganic semiconductors.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2511.16474v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：塑料无机半导体是高性能稳定柔性电子产品的有希望的候选者。锗基硫族化物材料以其优异的半导体特性，尤其是卓越的载流子迁移率而闻名。然而，Ge原子和硫属元素之间的元价键的固有半键性质解释了它们固有的脆性。在这里，我们报道了一种超高塑性的锗基硫族化物GaGeTe。 GaGeTe 单晶可以承受高达 7.5% 的大工程应变而不断裂。通过从宏观表征到原子分辨率透射电子显微镜的全面多尺度研究，我们揭示了特殊的可塑性是由于层间滑动和层内晶格畸变的协同效应。 DFT 计算进一步阐明了化学起源。 Ga原子的存在避免了脆性的元价Ge-Te键的形成。相反，由 sp3 杂化共价 Ga-Te、Ge-Ge 和 Ga-Ge 键形成的坚固蜂窝晶格在模拟中的大拉伸应变下保持了高键强度，从而支撑了宏观塑性行为。这项关于 GaGeTe 塑性起源的系统研究引入了键合工程作为构建塑性无机半导体的潜在方法。
