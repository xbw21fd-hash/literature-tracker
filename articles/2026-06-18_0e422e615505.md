## Abstract
Author(s): Seth W. Kurfman, Denis R. Candido, Brandi Wooten, Yuanhua Zheng, Michael J. Newburger, Shuyu Cheng, Roland K. Kawakami, Joseph P. Heremans, Michael E. Flatté, and Ezekiel Johnston-Halperin The study of spin-thermal processes is limited by the small number of magnetic materials where these effects are easily distinguished and the limited regimes wherein the theory is tractable. This paper incorporates the temperature dependence of magnon nonconserving processes, demonstrating quantitative agreement with established data sets and leading to the prediction of a high‑efficiency regime defined by low‑magnetization, ultra‑low‑damping magnetic materials. This prediction is validated in the organic ferrimagnetic semiconductor V[TCNE] x , which shows spin-thermal effects comparable to yttrium iron garnet. [Phys. Rev.
