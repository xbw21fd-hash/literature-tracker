## Abstract
arXiv:2601.05433v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: This study, conducted among more than 250 physics and chemistry teachers in Morocco, analyzes the impact of experimentation on student learning and attention in middle and high school. The results show that the majority of teachers favor digital simulations, except for simple experiments such as electrical circuits. This choice is linked to material constraints, class size, and safety requirements. Simulations are perceived as practical and flexible, allowing experiments to be repeated or slowed down to facilitate understanding. However, teachers emphasize the need for specific ICT training in order to better integrate these tools into their practices. The most effective strategy identified is based on a hybrid approach: using simulations to explain abstract phenomena and real experiments to develop experimental skills, methodological rigor, and critical thinking. This complementary approach appears to be a promising solution for enriching science education and overcoming the constraints encountered in schools.

arXiv:2601.05433v1 公告类型：新 摘要：这项研究在摩洛哥 250 多名物理和化学教师中进行，分析了实验对初中和高中学生学习和注意力的影响。结果表明，除了电路等简单实验外，大多数教师都青睐数字模拟。这种选择与材料限制、班级规模和安全要求有关。模拟被认为是实用且灵活的，允许重复或放慢实验以促进理解。然而，教师强调需要进行特定的信息通信技术培训，以便更好地将这些工具融入到他们的实践中。最有效的策略是基于混合方法：使用模拟来解释抽象现象，使用真实实验来培养实验技能、方法论的严谨性和批判性思维。这种补充方法似乎是丰富科学教育和克服学校遇到的限制的一个有前途的解决方案。
