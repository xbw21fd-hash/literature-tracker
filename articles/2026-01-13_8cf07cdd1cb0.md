## Abstract
This research aims to investigate and optimize lithium extraction from hard rock ore found in the Jammu Reasi district. With lithium being a crucial element for battery technologies in electric vehicles and energy storage systems, the discovery of an estimated 5.9 million tonnes of lithium-containing ore in India presents a significant opportunity to reduce dependence on imports. However, lithium extraction from hard rock deposits is more complex and energy-intensive than lithium brine sources, necessitating efficient and cost-effective processing methods. Mineralogical characterization using Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) and X-ray Diffraction (XRD) confirmed the presence of lithium at a concentration of 3.29 wt%, primarily in the form of LiAl₂SiO₆. Further analysis revealed that lithium remains extractable from the ore, and in the final processed sample, lithium is still present at around 3.29 wt%. This highlights the potential for optimizing extraction techniques to maximize lithium recovery. If maximum extraction efficiency is achieved, India can become self-sufficient in lithium production, eliminating the need for imports. Moreover, local extraction from the mine can provide economic benefits and employment opportunities to communities in the region. A sulfuric acid digestion method was employed to leach lithium into the solution, followed by quantitative analysis using Atomic Absorption Spectroscopy (AAS) to determine lithium recovery efficiency. Given the complex mineralogical composition, alternative separation strategies such as nanofiltration using the DK membrane series for selective aluminium ion retention and alkali digestion with sodium hydroxide to precipitate aluminium hydroxide (Al (OH)₃) are suggested for further extraction process optimization. The subsequent conversion of the extracted lithium into battery-grade lithium hydroxide and lithium iron phosphate involves additional chemical refining steps, requiring precursors such as cobalt sulfate, nickel sulfate, and magnesium sulfate. The optimization of these processes is essential to improve extraction efficiency, reduce costs, and minimize environmental impact. The successful refinement of lithium from the Reasi reserves has the potential to establish a domestic supply chain, enhance India's energy security, and contribute to the advancement of clean energy technologies.

## 摘要（中文）
这项研究旨在调查和优化从 Jammu Reasi 地区发现的硬岩矿石中提取锂。由于锂是电动汽车和储能系统电池技术的关键元素，印度发现了估计 590 万吨的含锂矿石，这为减少对进口的依赖提供了重要机会。然而，从硬岩矿床中提取锂比锂盐水源更加复杂且能源密集，因此需要高效且具有成本效益的加工方法。使用扫描电子显微镜 (SEM) 和 X 射线衍射 (XRD) 进行的矿物学表征证实存在浓度为 3.29 wt% 的锂，主要以 LiAl2SiO6 的形式存在。进一步分析表明，锂仍然可以从矿石中提取，并且在最终处理的样品中，锂的含量仍约为 3.29 wt%。这凸显了优化提取技术以最大限度地提高锂回收率的潜力。如果实现最大提取效率，印度可以实现锂生产自给自足，从而消除进口需求。此外，当地的矿山开采可以为该地区的社区提供经济效益和就业机会。采用硫酸消解法将锂浸出到溶液中，然后使用原子吸收光谱（AAS）进行定量分析以确定锂回收效率。考虑到复杂的矿物成分，建议采用替代分离策略，例如使用 DK 膜系列进行纳滤以选择性保留铝离子，以及使用氢氧化钠进行碱消化以沉淀氢氧化铝 (Al(OH)₃)，以进一步优化萃取工艺。随后将提取的锂转化为电池级氢氧化锂和磷酸铁锂，涉及额外的化学精炼步骤，需要硫酸钴、硫酸镍和硫酸镁等前体。这些工艺的优化对于提高提取效率、降低成本和尽量减少对环境的影响至关重要。从Reasi储量中成功提炼锂有可能建立国内供应链，增强印度的能源安全，并为清洁能源技术的进步做出贡献。
