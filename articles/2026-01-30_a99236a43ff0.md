## Abstract
arXiv:2312.15462v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: In the last years in-vivo tractography has assumed an important role in neurosciences, for both research and clinical applications such as non-invasive investigation of brain connectivity and presurgical planning in neurosurgery. In more recent years there has been a growing interest in the applications of diffusion tractography for target identification in functional neurological disorders for an increasingly tailored approach. The growing diffusion of well-established neurosurgical procedures, such as deep brain stimulation or trans-cranial Magnetic Resonance-guided Focused Ultrasound, favored this trend. Tractography can indeed provide more accurate, patient-specific, information about the targeted region if compared to stereotactic atlases. On the other hand, this tractography-based approach is not very physician-friendly, and its heavily time consuming since it needs several hours for Magnetic Resonance Imaging data processing. In this study we propose a novel open-source deep learning framework called DeLTA-BIT (acronym of Deep-learning Local TrActography for BraIn Targeting) for fast target predictions, based on probabilistic tractography. The proposed framework exploits a convolutional neural network (CNN) to predict the location of the Ventral Intermediate Nucleus of the thalamus (VIM). The CNN was trained on the Human Connectome Project (HCP) dataset. The model capability in predicting the VIM location was tested both on the HCP (internal validation) and clinical data (external validation). Results from the internal validation have shown good capability in predicting the VIM region (mean DSC = 0.62+- 0.15, mean sDSC=0.76+- 0.17) by using just T1 images as input, in a time scale of fraction of second per subject. As for the clinical data, results have been compared with an atlas-based method demonstrating similar performance, but within a significantly shorter timeframe.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2312.15462v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：近年来，体内纤维束成像技术在神经科学领域发挥着重要作用，无论是研究还是临床应用，例如脑连接的非侵入性研究和神经外科手术中的术前规划。近年来，人们对扩散纤维束成像技术在功能性神经系统疾病中的靶点识别中的应用越来越感兴趣，以寻求越来越定制化的方法。成熟的神经外科手术的日益普及，例如深部脑刺激或经颅磁共振引导的聚焦超声，有利于这一趋势。与立体定向图谱相比，纤维束成像确实可以提供有关目标区域的更准确、针对患者的信息。另一方面，这种基于纤维束成像的方法对医生来说不太友好，并且非常耗时，因为磁共振成像数据处理需要几个小时。在这项研究中，我们提出了一种名为 DeLTA-BIT（Deep-learning Local TrActography for Brain Targeting 的缩写）的新型开源深度学习框架，用于基于概率纤维束成像的快速目标预测。所提出的框架利用卷积神经网络（CNN）来预测丘脑腹中间核（VIM）的位置。 CNN 在人类连接组计划 (HCP) 数据集上进行了训练。模型预测 VIM 位置的能力在 HCP（内部验证）和临床数据（外部验证）上进行了测试。内部验证的结果显示，仅使用 T1 图像作为输入，在每个受试者几分之一秒的时间尺度内预测 VIM 区域（平均 DSC = 0.62+- 0.15，平均 sDSC=0.76+- 0.17）具有良好的能力。至于临床数据，结果与基于图谱的方法进行了比较，显示出相似的性能，但时间显着缩短。
