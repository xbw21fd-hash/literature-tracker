# Broken neural scaling laws in materials science

## Abstract
arXiv:2602.05702v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: In materials science, data are scarce and expensive to generate, whether computationally or experimentally. Therefore, it is crucial to identify how model performance scales with dataset size and model capacity to distinguish between data- and model-limited regimes. Neural scaling laws provide a framework for quantifying this behavior and guide the design of materials datasets and machine learning architectures. Here, we investigate neural scaling laws for a paradigmatic materials science task: predicting the dielectric function of metals, a high-dimensional response that governs how solids interact with light. Using over 200,000 dielectric functions from high-throughput ab initio calculations, we study two multi-objective graph neural networks trained to predict the frequency-dependent complex interband dielectric function and the Drude frequency. We observe broken neural scaling laws with respect to dataset size, whereas scaling with the number of model parameters follows a simple power law that rapidly saturates.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.05702v1 公告类型：新 摘要：在材料科学中，无论是通过计算还是通过实验，数据的生成都很稀缺且昂贵。因此，确定模型性能如何随数据集大小和模型容量扩展以区分数据限制和模型限制的情况至关重要。神经缩放定律提供了一个量化这种行为的框架，并指导材料数据集和机器学习架构的设计。在这里，我们研究了典型材料科学任务的神经标度定律：预测金属的介电函数，这是一种控制固体与光相互作用的高维响应。我们使用来自高通量从头计算的超过 200,000 个介电函数，研究了两个经过训练来预测频率相关的复杂带间介电函数和德鲁德频率的多目标图神经网络。我们观察到关于数据集大小的神经缩放定律被破坏，而模型参数数量的缩放遵循快速饱和的简单幂律。
