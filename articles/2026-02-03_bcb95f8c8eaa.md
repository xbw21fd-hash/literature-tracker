## Abstract
arXiv:2504.08787v3 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Laser-induced forward transfer (LIFT) printing is a versatile technique to realize micro/nano-scale additive manufacturing of functional materials, including metals and semiconductors. However, the crystallization phenomena during LIFT printing have not been well understood, which is critical to determine the resulting microstructure and properties. In this work, we systematically investigate silicon crystallization during LIFT printing using molecular dynamics (MD) simulations. Specifically, MD simulation with Stillinger-Weber (SW) potential is used to investigate the size effect and surface influence on the crystallization of Si nanoparticles during transportation in air. We found that with a decrease in nanoparticle size, crystallization becomes increasingly rare, even at low cooling rates. The nucleation location of different particles is also analyzed and almost always starts at a sub-surface location (below 5 {\AA}). The evolution of the atomic structure during solidification is also monitored to guide LIFT printing of Si. Our simulation results indicate that nano-confinement induced by the surface layer can lead to single-crystal structure formation, which may shed light on additive manufacturing of single-crystal structures and devices.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2504.08787v3 公告类型：替换 摘要：激光诱导正向转移 (LIFT) 打印是一种实现功能材料（包括金属和半导体）微/纳米级增材制造的通用技术。然而，LIFT 打印过程中的结晶现象尚未得到很好的理解，这对于确定最终的微观结构和性能至关重要。在这项工作中，我们使用分子动力学 (MD) 模拟系统地研究 LIFT 打印过程中的硅结晶。具体来说，采用斯蒂林格-韦伯（SW）势的MD模拟来研究尺寸效应和表面对硅纳米颗粒在空气中运输过程中结晶的影响。我们发现，随着纳米颗粒尺寸的减小，即使在低冷却速率下，结晶也变得越来越罕见。还分析了不同颗粒的成核位置，并且几乎总是从表面下位置（低于 5 {\AA}）开始。凝固过程中原子结构的演变也受到监测，以指导硅的 LIFT 打印。我们的模拟结果表明，表面层引起的纳米约束可以导致单晶结构的形成，这可能有助于单晶结构和器件的增材制造。
