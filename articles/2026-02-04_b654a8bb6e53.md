## Abstract
arXiv:2602.02788v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: We aim to develop physics foundation models for science and engineering that provide real-time solutions to Partial Differential Equations (PDEs) which preserve structure and accuracy under adaptation to unseen geometries. To this end, we introduce General-Geometry Neural Whitney Forms (Geo-NeW): a data-driven finite element method. We jointly learn a differential operator and compatible reduced finite element spaces defined on the underlying geometry. The resulting model is solved to generate predictions, while exactly preserving physical conservation laws through Finite Element Exterior Calculus. Geometry enters the model as a discretized mesh both through a transformer-based encoding and as the basis for the learned finite element spaces. This explicitly connects the underlying geometry and imposed boundary conditions to the solution, providing a powerful inductive bias for learning neural PDEs, which we demonstrate improves generalization to unseen domains. We provide a novel parameterization of the constitutive model ensuring the existence and uniqueness of the solution. Our approach demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on several steady-state PDE benchmarks, and provides a significant improvement over conventional baselines on out-of-distribution geometries.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.02788v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：我们的目标是开发科学和工程的物理基础模型，为偏微分方程 (PDE) 提供实时解决方案，在适应看不见的几何形状的情况下保持结构和准确性。为此，我们引入通用几何神经惠特尼形式（Geo-NeW）：一种数据驱动的有限元方法。我们共同学习微分算子和在基础几何上定义的兼容简化有限元空间。对所得模型进行求解以生成预测，同时通过有限元外微积分准确保留物理守恒定律。几何通过基于变压器的编码作为离散网格进入模型，并作为学习的有限元空间的基础。这明确地将底层几何结构和施加的边界条件与解决方案联系起来，为学习神经偏微分方程提供了强大的归纳偏差，我们证明这可以提高对未知领域的泛化能力。我们提供了一种新颖的本构模型参数化，确保解的存在性和唯一性。我们的方法在几个稳态 PDE 基准上展示了最先进的性能，并且在非分布几何上比传统基准有了显着的改进。
