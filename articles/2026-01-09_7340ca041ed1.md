## Abstract
The functionalization of recalcitrant chemical bonds that require extreme reduction potentials is often thwarted by the absence of a defined reaction field to control potent intermediates. Conventional multiphoton photocatalysis generates super-reductants, whose immense energy dissipates through chaotic, diffusion-controlled pathways. Herein, an "association-gated" strategy is introduced that constructs sequential electron donor–acceptor (EDA) complexes as programmable reaction microenvironments. This system initially captures a single photon through its sulfide/naphthalene monoimide (NMI) complex. The resulting NMI radical anion serves as a scaffold to pre-organize the haloarene substrate into a second, distinct EDA complex, creating a reaction pocket for the final, energy-storing excitation. Such supramolecular confinement generates an extreme reducing potential at the carbon–halogen bond, suppressing both diffusive side-reactions and parasitic hydrogen atom transfer from amine donors. This unlocks a broad scope of transformations under air-tolerant conditions, providing a versatile tool for late-stage diversification.

## 摘要（中文）
需要极端还原电位的顽固化学键的功能化常常因缺乏控制有效中间体的明确反应场而受阻。传统的多光子光催化产生超级还原剂，其巨大的能量通过混乱的、扩散控制的途径消散。在此，引入了“关联门控”策略，该策略构建了顺序电子供体-受体（EDA）复合物作为可编程反应微环境。该系统最初通过其硫化物/萘单酰亚胺 (NMI) 复合物捕获单个光子。由此产生的 NMI 自由基阴离子充当支架，将卤代芳烃底物预组织成第二个不同的 EDA 复合物，为最终的储能激发创建反应袋。这种超分子限制在碳-卤键处产生极端的还原电位，抑制扩散副反应和来自胺供体的寄生氢原子转移。这在耐空气条件下开启了广泛的转化，为后期多样化提供了多功能工具。
