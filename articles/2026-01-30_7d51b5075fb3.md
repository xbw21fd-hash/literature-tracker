## Abstract
arXiv:2411.07233v2 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: Generative diffusion models have emerged as powerful tools for sampling high-dimensional distributions, yet they typically rely on white gaussian noise and noise schedules to destroy and reconstruct information. Here, we demonstrate that driving the generative process out of equilibrium using active, temporally correlated noise sources fundamentally alters the information thermodynamics of the system. We show that coupling the data to an active non-Markovian bath creates a `memory effect' where high-level semantic information (such as class identity or molecular metastability) is stored in the temporal correlations of auxiliary degrees of freedom. Using Fisher information analysis, we prove that this active mechanism significantly retards the rate of information decay compared to passive Brownian motion. Crucially, this memory effect facilitates an earlier and more robust symmetry breaking (speciation) during the reverse generative process, allowing the system to resolve multi-scale structures, reminiscent of metastable states in molecular configurations that are washed out in the typical noising processes. Our results suggest that non-equilibrium protocols, inspired by active matter physics, offer a thermodynamically distinct and potentially advantageous pathway for recovering high-dimensional energy landscapes using generative diffusion.

arXiv:2411.07233v2 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：生成扩散模型已成为对高维分布进行采样的强大工具，但它们通常依赖于白高斯噪声和噪声表来破坏和重建信息。在这里，我们证明，使用主动的、时间相关的噪声源使生成过程失去平衡，从根本上改变了系统的信息热力学。我们表明，将数据耦合到活跃的非马尔可夫浴会产生“记忆效应”，其中高级语义信息（例如类同一性或分子亚稳定性）存储在辅助自由度的时间相关性中。使用费希尔信息分析，我们证明与被动布朗运动相比，这种主动机制显着减缓了信息衰减的速度。至关重要的是，这种记忆效应有助于在反向生成过程中更早、更鲁棒地对称性破缺（形态形成），从而使系统能够解析多尺度结构，让人想起在典型的噪声过程中被冲掉的分子构型中的亚稳态。我们的结果表明，受活性物质物理学启发的非平衡协议为利用生成扩散恢复高维能量景观提供了一条热力学上独特且潜在有利的途径。
