## Abstract
arXiv:2601.17636v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: AI weather models now rival leading numerical weather prediction (NWP) systems in medium-range skill. However, almost all still rely on NWP data assimilation (DA) to provide initial conditions, tying them to expensive infrastructure and limiting the practical speed and accuracy gains of ML. More recently, ML-based DA systems have been proposed, which are often trained and evaluated end-to-end with a forecast model, making it difficult to assess the quality of their analysis fields. We introduce HealDA, a global ML-based DA system that maps a short window of satellite and conventional observations directly to a 1{\deg} atmospheric state on the HEALPix grid, using a smaller sensor suite than operational NWP and no background forecast at runtime. We treat HealDA strictly as a DA module: its analyses are used to initialize off-the-shelf ML forecast models without any fine-tuning of either. For a variety of off-the-shelf ML forecast models, including FourCastNet3 (FCN3), Aurora, and FengWu, HealDA-initialized forecasts lose less than one day of effective lead time when scored against ERA5. HealDA-initialized FCN3 ensembles similarly trail those of the ECMWF IFS ENS system by < 24 h. We find that forecast error growth in these models i unchanged from HealDA initialization, and the skill gap primarily arises from the larger initial error of the HealDA analysis. Spectral analysis reveals that this stems from overfitting to the large scales and upper-tropospheric fields. We also demonstrate that small changes in the verification setup can shift apparent skill by 12--24h, underscoring the need for consistent scoring. Taken together, these results clarify the current performance of ML-based DA systems and show that a relatively simple, background-free network can already provide initial conditions that are usable by state-of-the-art ML forecast models with only modest loss in medium-range skill.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.17636v1 公告类型：新 摘要：人工智能天气模型现在在中程技能方面可与领先的数值天气预报 (NWP) 系统相媲美。然而，几乎所有这些仍然依赖 NWP 数据同化 (DA) 来提供初始条件，将它们与昂贵的基础设施联系在一起，并限制了 ML 的实际速度和精度增益。最近，人们提出了基于机器学习的 DA 系统，这些系统通常使用预测模型进行端到端的训练和评估，因此很难评估其分析领域的质量。我们引入了 HealDA，这是一种基于 ML 的全球 DA 系统，可将卫星和常规观测的短窗口直接映射到 HEALPix 网格上的 1{\deg} 大气状态，使用比可操作 NWP 更小的传感器套件，并且运行时没有背景预报。我们严格将 HealDA 视为 DA 模块：它的分析用于初始化现成的 ML 预测模型，而无需对两者进行任何微调。对于各种现成的 ML 预测模型，包括 FourCastNet3 (FCN3)、Aurora 和 FengWu，与 ERA5 相比，HealDA 初始化的预测损失了不到一天的有效提前时间。 HealDA 初始化的 FCN3 集成同样落后于 ECMWF IFS ENS 系统 < 24 小时。我们发现这些模型中的预测误差增长与 HealDA 初始化相比没有变化，而技能差距主要源于 HealDA 分析的较大初始误差。频谱分析表明，这是由于对大尺度和对流层上层场的过度拟合造成的。我们还证明，验证设置中的微小变化可以将明显的技能改变 12--24 小时，这强调了一致评分的必要性。总而言之，这些结果阐明了基于 ML 的 DA 系统的当前性能，并表明相对简单、无背景的网络已经可以提供最先进的 ML 预测模型可用的初始条件，而中期技能仅略有损失。
