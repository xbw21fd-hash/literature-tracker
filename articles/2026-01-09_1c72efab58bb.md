## Abstract
arXiv:2503.20107v2 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: Artificial intelligence has transformed the perspective of medical imaging, leading to a genuine technological revolution in modern computer-assisted healthcare systems. However, ubiquitously featured deep learning (DL) systems require access to a considerable amount of data, facilitating proper knowledge extraction and generalization. Access to such extensive resources may be hindered due to the time and effort required to convey ethical agreements, set up and carry the acquisition procedures through, and manage the datasets adequately with a particular emphasis on proper anonymization. One of the pivotal challenges in the DL field is data integration from various sources acquired using different hardware vendors, diverse acquisition protocols, experimental setups, and even inter-operator variabilities. In this paper, we review the federated learning (FL) concept that fosters the integration of large-scale heterogeneous datasets from multiple institutions in training DL models. In contrast to a centralized approach, the decentralized FL procedure promotes training DL models while preserving data privacy at each institution involved. We formulate the FL principle and comprehensively review general and specialized medical imaging aggregation and learning algorithms, enabling the generation of a globally generalized model. We meticulously go through the challenges in constructing FL-based systems, such as data and model heterogeneities across the institutions, resilience to potential attacks on data privacy, and the variability in computational and communication resources among the entangled sites that might induce efficiency issues of the entire system. Finally, we explore the up-to-date open frameworks for rapid FL-based algorithm prototyping, comprehensively present real-world implementations of FL systems and shed light on future directions in this intensively growing field.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2503.20107v2 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：人工智能改变了医学成像的视角，引发了现代计算机辅助医疗系统的真正技术革命。然而，无处不在的深度学习（DL）系统需要访问大量数据，以促进正确的知识提取和泛化。由于传达道德协议、建立和执行获取程序以及充分管理数据集（特别强调适当的匿名化）所需的时间和精力，对如此广泛的资源的访问可能会受到阻碍。深度学习领域的关键挑战之一是使用不同硬件供应商、不同采集协议、实验设置甚至操作员间差异获取的各种来源的数据集成。在本文中，我们回顾了联邦学习 (FL) 概念，该概念促进了来自多个机构的大规模异构数据集在训练深度学习模型中的集成。与集中式方法相比，去中心化的 FL 程序促进了深度学习模型的训练，同时保护了每个相关机构的数据隐私。我们制定了 FL 原理，并全面审查了通用和专业的医学影像聚合和学习算法，从而能够生成全球通用的模型。我们仔细研究了构建基于 FL 的系统时面临的挑战，例如机构之间的数据和模型异构性、对数据隐私潜在攻击的恢复能力，以及纠缠站点之间计算和通信资源的可变性，这些可变性可能会导致整个系统的效率问题。最后，我们探索了基于 FL 的快速算法原型设计的最新开放框架，全面展示了 FL 系统的实际实现，并阐明了这个不断发展的领域的未来方向。
