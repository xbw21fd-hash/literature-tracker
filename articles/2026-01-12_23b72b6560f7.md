## Abstract
arXiv:2507.19255v2 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: Parametric surrogate models of electric machines are widely used for efficient design optimization and operational monitoring. Addressing geometry variations, spline-based computer-aided design representations play a pivotal role. In this study, we propose a novel approach that combines isogeometric analysis, proper orthogonal decomposition and deep learning to enable rapid and physically consistent predictions by directly learning spline basis coefficients. The effectiveness of this method is demonstrated using a parametric nonlinear magnetostatic model of a permanent magnet synchronous machine.
