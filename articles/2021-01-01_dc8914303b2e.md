## Abstract
We show that, contrary to popular assumptions, predictions from machine learning potentials built upon atom-density representations almost exclusively occur in an extrapolation regime -- i.e., in regions of the representation space which lie outside the convex hull defined by the training set points. We then propose a perspective to rationalise the domain of robust extrapolation and accurate prediction of atomistic machine learning potentials in terms of the probability density induced by training points in the representation space.
