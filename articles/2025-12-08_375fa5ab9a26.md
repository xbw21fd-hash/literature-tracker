## Abstract
ABSTRACT Physical non-linearities near the Mott transition exhibit substantial potential for neuromorphic computing. The complex computational behaviour stems from their intrinsic local active characteristics. Most studies focus on decay dynamics or regular oscillations, treating Mott devices primarily as simple threshold elements. Challenges remain in connecting measurable material properties to more complex device dynamics and their control methods through a unified theoretical model. Here, we develop a thermodynamic compact model for vanadium oxide devices based on electrical measurements and the local active principle. Utilizing the non-linearities near the Mott transition, we propose an injection-based control method to regulate behaviours of non-linear oscillators, such as frequency division, stochastic oscillations and frequency locking. Finally, a single device operating at the edge of chaos demonstrates exceptional capability in extracting information in the frequency domain within a physical computing framework, achieving performance equivalent to a two-layer convolutional neural network on the same task. This work facilitates a paradigm shift from traditional local passive devices to local active devices, bridging the physical non-linearities, circuit dynamics and computational theory to advance dynamic neuromorphic computing.

## 摘要（中文）
摘要 莫特转变附近的物理非线性表现出神经形态计算的巨大潜力。复杂的计算行为源于其固有的局部活跃特征。大多数研究集中于衰变动力学或规则振荡，将莫特器件主要视为简单的阈值元件。通过统一的理论模型将可测量的材料特性与更复杂的设备动力学及其控制方法联系起来仍然存在挑战。在这里，我们基于电气测量和局部有源原理开发了氧化钒器件的热力学紧凑模型。利用莫特转变附近的非线性，我们提出了一种基于注入的控制方法来调节非线性振荡器的行为，例如分频、随机振荡和锁频。最后，在混沌边缘运行的单个设备表现出了在物理计算框架内提取频域信息的卓越能力，在同一任务上实现了相当于两层卷积神经网络的性能。这项工作促进了从传统本地无源设备到本地有源设备的范式转变，连接了物理非线性、电路动力学和计算理论，以推进动态神经形态计算。
