# What is emergence, after all?

## Abstract
arXiv:2507.04951v4 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: The term emergence is increasingly used across scientific disciplines to describe phenomena that arise from interactions among a system's components but cannot be readily inferred by examining those components in isolation. While often invoked to explain higher-level behaviors, such as flocking, synchronization, or collective intelligence, the term is frequently used without precision, sometimes giving rise to ambiguity or even mystique. In this perspective paper, we clarify the scientific meaning of emergence as a measurable and physically grounded phenomenon. Through concrete examples, such as temperature, magnetism, and herd immunity in social networks, we review how collective behavior can arise from local interactions that are constrained by global boundaries. By refining the concept of emergence, we gain a clearer and more grounded understanding of complex systems. Our goal is to show that emergence, when properly framed, offers not mysticism but insight.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2507.04951v4 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：术语“涌现”越来越多地在科学学科中使用，用于描述系统组件之间相互作用产生的现象，但通过单独检查这些组件无法轻松推断出这些现象。虽然经常被用来解释更高层次的行为，例如聚集、同步或集体智慧，但该术语的使用经常不精确，有时会引起歧义甚至神秘。在这篇透视论文中，我们阐明了涌现作为一种可测量且有物理基础的现象的科学意义。通过社交网络中的温度、磁力和群体免疫等具体例子，我们回顾了受全球边界限制的局部互动如何产生集体行为。通过完善涌现的概念，我们对复杂系统有了更清晰、更扎实的理解。我们的目标是表明，在适当的框架下，出现提供的不是神秘主义，而是洞察力。
