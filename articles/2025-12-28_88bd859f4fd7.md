## Abstract
ADMET property prediction is crucial for drug discovery yet severely hindered by left-censored labels in experimental datasets, which introduce significant prediction bias in standard Graph Neural Networks (GNNs). While relational learning approaches, such as Bilinear Transduction, address this by focusing on property differences, they suffer from substantial computational overhead and deployment complexity due to their reliance on fixed anchor molecules. We propose the Dynamic Anchor Relational Graph Neural Network (DAR-GNN), a novel end-to-end architecture that integrates dynamic anchor generation and relational difference learning. DAR-GNN employs a Dynamic Anchor Generation Module that adaptively synthesizes a context-aware dynamic anchor representation from a learnable virtual anchor pool via an attention mechanism. This dynamic anchor facilitates a difference-aware prediction of the target property. Extensive experiments on diverse ADMET datasets, including challenging temporal splits with varying levels of censoring, demonstrate that DAR-GNN consistently outperforms traditional GNN baselines and achieves comparable or superior predictive performance to Bilinear Transduction, while offering significantly enhanced computational efficiency and improved generalization capabilities. Our work provides a scalable and robust solution for ADMET property prediction, particularly for datasets plagued by censored labels, thereby accelerating early-stage drug discovery.

## 摘要（中文）
ADMET 属性预测对于药物发现至关重要，但受到实验数据集中左删失标签的严重阻碍，这在标准图神经网络 (GNN) 中引入了显着的预测偏差。虽然关系学习方法（例如双线性转换）通过关注属性差异来解决这个问题，但由于它们依赖于固定的锚分子，因此它们会遭受大量的计算开销和部署复杂性。我们提出了动态锚关系图神经网络（DAR-GNN），这是一种集成了动态锚生成和关系差异学习的新颖的端到端架构。 DAR-GNN 采用动态锚生成模块，通过注意力机制从可学习的虚拟锚池中自适应地合成上下文感知的动态锚表示。这种动态锚有助于对目标属性进行差异感知预测。对不同 ADMET 数据集的大量实验（包括具有不同级别审查的挑战性时间分割）表明，DAR-GNN 始终优于传统的 GNN 基线，并实现了与双线性转导相当或更好的预测性能，同时显着提高了计算效率并改进了泛化能力。我们的工作为 ADMET 属性预测提供了一个可扩展且强大的解决方案，特别是对于受审查标签困扰的数据集，从而加速早期药物发现。
