# Mistletoe- and mussel-inspired fabrication of hierarchically structured protein-cellulose scaffolds from biomolecular condensates

## Abstract
Nature's ability to produce hierarchical materials via biomolecular self-assembly can inspire bioinspired avenues to advanced materials using biorenewable components and water as a solvent. Recent advances have shown that biomolecular condensates are important precursor phases for fabricating biological materials like silk, mussel byssus, and velvet worm slime. Here, we leverage recent findings on the role of malleable biomolecular phases from both animal and plant systems to develop a synergistic mussel- and mistletoe-inspired approach for fabricating protein-cellulose composite scaffolds possessing tunable hierarchical structure. We demonstrate that recombinant mussel foot protein-1 (rMfp-1), undergoes controlled phase separation in solution when mixed with surface-functionalized anionic cellulose nanorods, forming condensates with characteristic core-shell morphology. Using a facile approach based on freeze-drying of suspensions, we produce freestanding protein-cellulose composite scaffolds possessing tunable porous structures with potential as scaffolds for tissue engineering. Through a cross-disciplinary approach combining various spectroscopic and imaging modalities, we gain mechanistic insights into the role of intermolecular interactions and physical processes in guiding this process. These findings highlight that hierarchically structured materials can be fabricated simply via multi-component phase separation. This work establishes a framework for understanding and controlling bio-inspired material fabrication, offering a strategy to engineer materials with tunable structure and properties that bridge biomaterials research and emerging directions in synthetic biology

## 摘要（中文）
大自然通过生物分子自组装生产分层材料的能力可以激发利用生物可再生成分和水作为溶剂的先进材料的仿生途径。最近的进展表明，生物分子缩合物是制造丝、贻贝足丝和绒虫粘液等生物材料的重要前体相。在这里，我们利用关于动物和植物系统可延展生物分子相的作用的最新发现，开发了一种受贻贝和槲寄生启发的协同方法，用于制造具有可调层次结构的蛋白质纤维素复合支架。我们证明，当与表面功能化阴离子纤维素纳米棒混合时，重组贻贝足蛋白-1 (rMfp-1) 在溶液中发生受控相分离，形成具有特征性核壳形态的缩合物。使用基于悬浮液冷冻干燥的简便方法，我们生产了独立式蛋白质纤维素复合支架，其具有可调节的多孔结构，具有作为组织工程支架的潜力。通过结合各种光谱和成像方式的跨学科方法，我们获得了对分子间相互作用和物理过程在指导这一过程中的作用的机械见解。这些发现强调，可以通过多组分相分离简单地制造分层结构材料。这项工作建立了一个理解和控制仿生材料制造的框架，提供了一种设计具有可调结构和性能的材料的策略，将生物材料研究和合成生物学的新兴方向联系起来
