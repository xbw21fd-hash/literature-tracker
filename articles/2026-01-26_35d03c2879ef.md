## Abstract
arXiv:2601.16961v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: We introduce a method for engineering discrete local dynamics in globally-driven dual-species neutral atom experiments, allowing us to study emergent digital models through uniform analog controls. Leveraging the new opportunities offered by dual-species systems, such as species-alternated driving, our construction exploits simple Floquet protocols on static atom arrangements, and benefits of generalized blockade regimes (different inter- and intra-species interactions). We focus on discrete dynamical models that are special examples of Quantum Cellular Automata (QCA), and explicitly consider a number of relevant examples, including the kicked-Ising model, the Floquet Kitaev honeycomb model, and the digitization of generic translation-invariant nearest-neighbor Hamiltonians (e.g., for Trotterized evolution). As an application, we study chaotic features of discretized many-body dynamics that can be detected by leveraging only demonstrated capabilities of globally-driven experiments, and benchmark their ability to discriminate chaotic evolution.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.16961v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：我们引入了一种在全局驱动的双物种中性原子实验中设计离散局部动力学的方法，使我们能够通过统一的模拟控制来研究新兴的数字模型。利用双物种系统提供的新机会，例如物种交替驱动，我们的构建利用了静态原子排列的简单 Floquet 协议，以及广义封锁机制（不同的物种间和物种内相互作用）的好处。我们重点关注作为量子元胞自动机 (QCA) 特殊示例的离散动力学模型，并明确考虑许多相关示例，包括 kicked-Ising 模型、Floquet Kitaev 蜂窝模型以及通用平移不变最近邻哈密顿量的数字化（例如，Trotterized 进化）。作为一种应用，我们研究离散多体动力学的混沌特征，这些特征可以通过仅利用全局驱动实验的演示能力来检测，并对其区分混沌演化的能力进行基准测试。
