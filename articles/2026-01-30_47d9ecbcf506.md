## Abstract
arXiv:2601.21881v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Humans can infer accurate mechanical outcomes from only a few observations, a capability known as mechanics intuition. The mechanisms behind such data-efficient learning remain unclear. Here, we propose a reinforcement learning framework in which an agent encodes continuous physical observation parameters into its state and is trained via episodic switching across closely related observations. With merely two or three observations, the agent acquires robust mechanics intuition that generalizes accurately over wide parameter ranges, substantially beyond the training data, as demonstrated on the brachistochrone and a large-deformation elastic plate. We explain this generalization through a unified theoretical view: it emerges when the learned value function enforces Bellman consistency across neighboring task parameters, rendering the Bellman residual stationary with respect to physical variations. This induces a smooth policy that captures a low-dimensional solution manifold underlying the continuum of tasks. Our work establishes episodic switching as a principled route to artificial mechanics intuition and offers a theoretical link to similar generalization abilities in biological learners.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.21881v1 公告类型：新 摘要：人类只需少量观察即可推断出准确的机械结果，这种能力称为机械直觉。这种数据高效学习背后的机制仍不清楚。在这里，我们提出了一个强化学习框架，其中代理将连续的物理观察参数编码为其状态，并通过紧密相关的观察之间的情景切换进行训练。仅通过两到三个观察，智能体就获得了强大的力学直觉，可以在广泛的参数范围内准确地概括，远远超出训练数据，如急速线和大变形弹性板所证明的那样。我们通过统一的理论观点来解释这种概括：当学习到的价值函数在相邻任务参数之间强制实施贝尔曼一致性时，就会出现这种概括，从而使贝尔曼残差相对于物理变化保持平稳。这会产生一个平滑的策略，捕获任务连续体背后的低维解决方案流形。我们的工作将情景切换确立为人工力学直觉的原则途径，并为生物学习者的类似泛化能力提供了理论联系。
