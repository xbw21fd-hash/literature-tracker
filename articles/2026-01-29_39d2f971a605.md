## Abstract
arXiv:2510.18387v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Quantification of protoporphyrin IX (PpIX) fluorescence in human brain tumours has the potential to significantly improve patient outcomes in neuro-oncology, but represents a formidable imaging challenge. Protoporphyrin is a biological molecule which interacts with the tissue micro-environment to form two photochemical states in glioma. Each exhibits markedly different quantum efficiencies, with distinct but overlapping emission spectra that also overlap with tissue autofluorescence. Fluorescence emission is known to be distorted by the intrinsic optical properties of tissue, coupled with marked intra-tumoural heterogeneity as a hallmark of glioma tumours. Existing quantitative fluorescence systems are developed and validated using simplified phantoms that do not simultaneously mimic the complex interactions between fluorophores and tissue optical properties or micro-environment. Consequently, existing systems risk introducing systematic errors into PpIX quantification when used in tissue. In this work, we introduce a novel pipeline for quantification of PpIX in glioma, which robustly differentiates both emission states from background autofluorescence without reliance on a priori spectral information, and accounts for variations in their quantum efficiency. Unmixed PpIX emission forms are then corrected for wavelength-dependent optical distortions and weighted for accurate quantification. Significantly, this pipeline is developed and validated using novel tissue-mimicking phantoms replicating the optical properties of glioma tissues and photochemical variability of PpIX fluorescence in glioma. Our workflow achieves strong correlation with ground-truth PpIX concentrations (R2 = 0.918+-0.002), demonstrating its potential for robust, quantitative PpIX fluorescence imaging in clinical settings.

arXiv:2510.18387v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：人脑肿瘤中原卟啉 IX (PpIX) 荧光的定量有可能显着改善神经肿瘤学患者的治疗结果，但也带来了巨大的成像挑战。原卟啉是一种生物分子，与组织微环境相互作用，在神经胶质瘤中形成两种光化学状态。每种都表现出明显不同的量子效率，具有不同但重叠的发射光谱，也与组织自发荧光重叠。众所周知，荧光发射会因组织的固有光学特性而扭曲，再加上作为神经胶质瘤标志的显着的肿瘤内异质性。现有的定量荧光系统是使用简化的模型来开发和验证的，这些模型不会同时模拟荧光团与组织光学特性或微环境之间的复杂相互作用。因此，现有系统在组织中使用时存在将系统误差引入 PpIX 定量的风险。在这项工作中，我们引入了一种用于定量神经胶质瘤中 PpIX 的新颖管道，该管道可以在不依赖先验光谱信息的情况下将两种发射态与背景自发荧光区分开来，并解释其量子效率的变化。然后，对未混合的 PpIX 发射形式进行与波长相关的光学畸变的校正，并进行加权以进行准确的定量。值得注意的是，该流程是使用新型组织模拟模型来开发和验证的，该模型复制了神经胶质瘤组织的光学特性和神经胶质瘤中 PpIX 荧光的光化学变异性。我们的工作流程与真实 PpIX 浓度 (R2 = 0.918+-0.002) 具有很强的相关性，证明了其在临床环境中进行稳健、定量 PpIX 荧光成像的潜力。
