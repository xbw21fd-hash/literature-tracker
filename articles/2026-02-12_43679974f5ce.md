## Abstract
arXiv:2602.10170v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: Solar vortices are fundamental components of solar atmospheric dynamics, serving as natural laboratories for magnetic field twisting, energy concentration and transport, wave guidance, and plasma coupling across atmospheric layers. Numerical and observational studies show that solar vortices are intimately connected to key physical processes including magnetic reconnection, atmospheric heating, turbulence, and wave generation. This white paper, prepared for the UK Space Frontiers 2035 call, outline five high-priority scientific questions addressing vortex generation mechanisms, cross-layer coupling, magnetic restructuring, collective wave-guidance structures, and their role in triggering explosive events and modulating the solar wind. Key observations and capabilities required to make significant advancements over the coming decade are identified. The UK solar physics community has established world-leading expertise in vortex dynamics, combining strengths in high-resolution observations, MHD turbulence theory, numerical modelling, and space instrumentation. UK researchers have made foundational contributions to Solar Orbiter, delivered critical systems for DKIST, and maintain active involvement in MUSE and SOLAR-C EUVST missions. Our technical approach centres on developing next-generation instrumentation: a multi-band, space-qualified system employing four tunable Fabry-P\'erot Interferometers providing diffraction-limited, high-cadence spectropolarimetric coverage from the deep photosphere to the low corona. This capability will be validated through a staged mission architecture beginning with balloon-borne demonstrators. Continuing this effort over the coming decade is vital to maintain UK leadership in this field and achieve the goals of roadmap for solar system research.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.10170v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：太阳涡旋是太阳大气动力学的基本组成部分，是磁场扭曲、能量集中和传输、波导以及跨大气层等离子体耦合的天然实验室。数值和观测研究表明，太阳涡旋与磁重联、大气加热、湍流和波产生等关键物理过程密切相关。这份为英国太空前沿 2035 号召准备的白皮书概述了五个高度优先的科学问题，涉及涡旋产生机制、跨层耦合、磁重组、集体波导结构及其在触发爆炸事件和调节太阳风中的作用。确定了未来十年取得重大进展所需的关键观察和能力。英国太阳物理学界在涡动力学方面建立了世界领先的专业知识，结合了高分辨率观测、MHD 湍流理论、数值模拟和空间仪器方面的优势。英国研究人员为太阳轨道飞行器做出了基础性贡献，为 DKIST 提供了关键系统，并积极参与 MUSE 和 SOLAR-C EUVST 任务。我们的技术方法集中于开发下一代仪器：一个多波段、符合太空要求的系统，采用四个可调谐法布里-珀罗干涉仪，提供从深光球层到低日冕的衍射极限、高节奏光谱偏振覆盖。这种能力将通过从气球承载演示器开始的分阶段任务架构进行验证。在未来十年继续这项努力对于保持英国在该领域的领导地位并实现太阳系研究路线图的目标至关重要。
