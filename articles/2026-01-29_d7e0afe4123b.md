## Abstract
arXiv:2601.20497v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: The importance of microstructure increases when decreasing the size of an object to the nanoscale, along with the complexity of controlling it. For instance, it is particularly complicated to create nano-object with controlled interfaces. Therefore, progressing towards 1D epitaxial nanostructures poses a challenge, and realization of their full potential is linked to technological issues of achieving large-scale, precise atom stacking of two or more different chemical elements. Achieving such coherent, epitaxial interfaces is a key step toward enabling spintronic phenomena in 1D objects, by minimizing interface scattering and strain-driven defects. Our results demonstrate a successful realization of controlled nanoscale heteroepitaxy in one-dimensional single-crystal structures. We fabricated nanowires composed of alternating magnetic (nickel) and non-magnetic, highly conductive (copper) segments. This periodic stacking modulates electron transport under magnetic stimuli. The epitaxial precision achieved eliminates detrimental electron scattering that has historically limited the magnetotransport properties of such 1D structures and hindered their development. Such materials are crucial for further advancements in the miniaturisation of nanosensors, actuators, and next-generation 3D spintronic devices.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.20497v1 公告类型：新 摘要：当物体的尺寸减小到纳米级时，微观结构的重要性随之增加，控制它的复杂性也随之增加。例如，创建具有受控接口的纳米物体尤其复杂。因此，向一维外延纳米结构发展提出了挑战，而充分发挥其潜力与实现两种或多种不同化学元素的大规模、精确原子堆叠的技术问题有关。通过最大限度地减少界面散射和应变驱动的缺陷，实现这种相干外延界面是实现一维物体中自旋电子现象的关键一步。我们的结果证明在一维单晶结构中成功实现了受控纳米级异质外延。我们制造了由交替磁性（镍）和非磁性、高导电性（铜）段组成的纳米线。这种周期性堆叠在磁刺激下调节电子传输。所达到的外延精度消除了有害的电子散射，这种散射历来限制了此类一维结构的磁输运特性并阻碍了它们的发展。此类材料对于纳米传感器、执行器和下一代 3D 自旋电子器件小型化的进一步发展至关重要。
