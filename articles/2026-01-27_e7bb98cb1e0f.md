## Abstract
arXiv:2601.18297v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Electrochemical devices (batteries, fuel cells, and electrolyzers) are in full development, driven by the green energy transition. Their real-time control requires ms predictions in order to take critical decisions during fast transients or faults. The physics behind include coupled multi-physics phenomena that conventional finite element methods cannot solve so fast with the current CPU technology. This paper evaluates the potential of physics-informed machine learning represented by three frameworks: \ac{pinn}, \ac{pideeponet}, and \ac{pino} by evaluating their training effort, inference speed, and extrapolation capacity. Our analysis reveals valuable performance trade-offs. \acp{pinn} offer simplicity for fixed problem instances but require retraining for parameter changes. \ac{pideeponet} enables operator learning across varying conditions with mesh-free geometric flexibility. \ac{pino} delivers superior performance on regular grids, with the strongest extrapolation capabilities due to spectral derivative computation and resolution invariance. \ac{pideeponet} is particularly suited for irregular, unstructured geometries (e.g., porous electrodes or complex flow fields), while \ac{pino} works best for layered, structured-grid problems (e.g., transport across stacked electrochemical layers) requiring fast inference. Possible future applications include real-time lithium concentration prediction for safe fast-charging and micro short circuit detection, water management in fuel cells, and optimal power management in electrolyzers under intermittent renewable inputs. These findings establish physics-informed operator learning as a transformative approach for next-generation electrochemical device controller technology.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.18297v1 公告类型：新 摘要：在绿色能源转型的推动下，电化学装置（电池、燃料电池和电解槽）正在全面发展。它们的实时控制需要毫秒级的预测，以便在快速瞬态或故障期间做出关键决策。其背后的物理现象包括耦合的多物理现象，传统的有限元方法无法利用当前的 CPU 技术如此快速地求解。本文通过评估训练量、推理速度和外推能力，评估了以 \ac{pinn}、\ac{pideeponet} 和 \ac{pino} 三个框架为代表的基于物理的机器学习的潜力。我们的分析揭示了有价值的性能权衡。 \acp{pinn} 为固定问题实例提供了简单性，但需要重新训练参数更改。 \ac{pideeponet} 使操作员能够在不同条件下进行学习，并具有无网格几何灵活性。 \ac{pino} 在规则网格上提供卓越的性能，由于谱导数计算和分辨率不变性，具有最强的外推能力。 \ac{pideeponet} 特别适合不规则的、非结构化的几何形状（例如，多孔电极或复杂的流场），而 \ac{pino} 最适合需要快速推理的分层、结构化网格问题（例如，跨堆叠电化学层的传输）。未来可能的应用包括用于安全快速充电和微短路检测的实时锂浓度预测、燃料电池中的水管理以及间歇性可再生能源输入下电解槽的最佳电源管理。这些发现将基于物理的操作员学习确立为下一代电化学设备控制器技术的变革性方法。
