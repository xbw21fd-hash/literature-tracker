## Abstract
In this study, I revisit the notion of gauge invariance in thermal transport and show, in the simplest and most general possible terms, why heat conductivity is unaffected by the specific choice of energy density. I provide the minimal and general conditions under which any two energy densities, though differing locally, lead to the same heat conductivity within the Green–Kubo framework. The relevance of gauge invariance in heat-transport simulations performed with machine-trained neural-network potentials is also briefly highlighted.

在这项研究中，我重新审视了热传输中规范不变性的概念，并以最简单和最普遍的可能术语展示了为什么热导率不受能量密度的具体选择的影响。我提供了最小和一般的条件，在该条件下，任何两种能量密度（尽管局部不同）都会在 Green-Kubo 框架内产生相同的导热率。还简要强调了使用机器训练的神经网络势进行的热传输模拟中规范不变性的相关性。
