## Abstract
Addendum to:Nature Machine Intelligencehttps://doi.org/10.1038/s42256-025-01124-5, published online 21 October 2025. Our paper proposes a refined dataset split for PDBbind to reduce data leakage and training dataset redundancy. We write this Addendum to acknowledge a related preprint1that also suggests an improved PDBbind split. To achieve the goal of generating an improved PDBbind split, the two works use complementary methodologies. While our paper refines the existing PDBbind split by reducing data leakage and training dataset redundancy and introduces the GEMS model, a new GNN for binding affinity prediction, the Leak Proof PDBBind preprint1generates new test datasets using additional data curation and a leave-cluster-out splitting of PDBbind. It does not discuss the effect of training data redundancy. The works also employ different similarity metrics in their filtering methods. While our paper combines structure-based methods (TM scores and pocket-aligned ligand RMSD) with count-based fingerprint similarity, the preprint relies on protein sequence identity, binary Morgan fingerprints, and proteochemometric fingerprint similarity. A strength of the preprint is the physicochemical and structural quality filters employed to sanitize the PDBbind dataset. Li, J. et al.
