# Explosive eruption cycles in a rotating Z-pinch

## Abstract
arXiv:2602.06012v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: A transonic shear flow directed along magnetic field lines can linearly stabilize a steep pressure gradient in a confined magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) plasma. In Z-pinch geometry, we show that, like the edge pedestal in tokamak devices, this transport barrier -- which we call the ``MHD pedestal'' -- is metastable, i.e., unstable to finite-amplitude displacements of flux tubes. We simulate the slow formation of an MHD pedestal in a heated and sheared Z-pinch, which collapses on reaching a critical height, expelling an order-unity fraction of the confined thermal energy. The MHD pedestal then rebuilds and the process repeats, in a manner analogous to the ELM cycle seen in fusion experiments. We show that the available energy of the metastable equilibrium, and the most energetically favorable amount of ejected plasma, can be calculated from first principles via combinatorial optimization of flux-tube interchanges.

arXiv:2602.06012v1 公告类型：新 摘要：沿磁场线引导的跨音速剪切流可以线性稳定受限磁流体动力学 (MHD) 等离子体中的陡峭压力梯度。在 Z 箍缩几何中，我们表明，就像托卡马克装置中的边缘基座一样，这种传输势垒（我们称之为“MHD 基座”）是亚稳态的，即对通量管的有限振幅位移不稳定。我们模拟了在加热和剪切的 Z 箍缩中 MHD 基座的缓慢形成，该基座在达到临界高度时塌陷，排出受限热能的有序单位部分。然后 MHD 基座重建并重复该过程，其方式类似于聚变实验中看到的 ELM 循环。我们表明，亚稳态平衡的可用能量以及喷射等离子体的最有利的能量量可以通过通量管互换的组合优化从第一原理计算出来。
