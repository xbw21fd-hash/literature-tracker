## Abstract
arXiv:2506.20577v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Neutron star glitches -- sudden increases in rotational frequency -- are thought to result from angular momentum transfer via quantized vortices in the superfluid core. To investigate the underlying superfluid dynamics, we employ a two-dimensional rotating atomic Bose-Einstein condensate described by a damped Gross-Pitaevskii equation with an imposed pinning potential that serves as a simplified analogue of a crust. Within this minimal framework, we examine the emergence and evolution of turbulent vortex motion following impulsive perturbations reminiscent of glitch-like forcing. Our simulations reveal a transient Kolmogorov-like turbulent cascade ($k^{-5/3}$) that transitions to a Vinen-like scaling ($k^{-1}$). We identify an anomalous secondary injection mechanism driven primarily by quantum pressure, which can sustain turbulent fluctuations in such a system. By tuning the damping coefficient $\gamma$, we determine an optimal regime for energy transfer. While idealized, these findings illustrate how quantum turbulence with multiple scaling regimes can arise in pinned, rotating superfluids, and they suggest possible qualitative connections to vortex-mediated dynamics in neutron stars and other astrophysical superfluid systems.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2506.20577v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：中子星故障（旋转频率突然增加）被认为是由超流体核心中的量子化涡旋的角动量传递造成的。为了研究潜在的超流体动力学，我们采用了二维旋转原子玻色-爱因斯坦凝聚体，该凝聚体由阻尼格罗斯-皮塔耶夫斯基方程描述，并具有强加的钉扎势，作为地壳的简化模拟。在这个最小的框架内，我们研究了脉冲扰动后湍流涡旋运动的出现和演变，让人想起类似故障的强迫。我们的模拟揭示了瞬态 Kolmogorov 式湍流级联 ($k^{-5/3}$)，该级联过渡到 Vinen 式缩放 ($k^{-1}$)。我们发现了一种主要由量子压力驱动的异常二次注入机制，它可以维持此类系统中的湍流波动。通过调整阻尼系数 $\gamma$，我们确定了能量传递的最佳机制。虽然是理想化的，但这些发现说明了具有多种尺度状态的量子湍流如何在固定的旋转超流体中出现，并且它们表明与中子星和其他天体物理超流体系统中涡旋介导的动力学可能存在定性联系。
