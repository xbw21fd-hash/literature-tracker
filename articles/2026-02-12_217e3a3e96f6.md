## Abstract
arXiv:2602.10243v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: The magnetic field of the Sun drives a wide range of eruptive phenomena, from small-scale nanoflares to large flares and coronal mass ejections (CMEs). While direct observations of solar activity cover only the past few decades, indirect evidence indicates that the Sun can occasionally produce events orders of magnitude stronger than any recorded ones in the modern era. Two complementary lines of evidence exist. First, extreme solar particle events (ESPEs) have been inferred from prominent spikes in cosmogenic isotope concentrations preserved in precisely dated natural archives such as tree rings and ice cores over the past 15 millennia. Second, high-precision space-borne photometry has revealed superflares on thousands of stars similar to the Sun. Whether these solar and stellar extremes are physically related remains an open question. We summarise the present state of understanding and discuss physical mechanisms that may link them. Although superflares and ESPEs are both extremely energetic manifestations of magnetic energy storage and release, their relationship does not appear to be one-to-one. Their occurrence and energetics likely depend on how magnetic flux and topology govern the partitioning of released energy between radiation, mass ejection, and particle acceleration.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.10243v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：太阳磁场驱动各种喷发现象，从小规模纳米耀斑到大型耀斑和日冕物质抛射 (CME)。虽然对太阳活动的直接观测仅涵盖过去几十年，但间接证据表明，太阳偶尔会产生比现代有记录的事件强几个数量级的事件。存在两条互补的证据。首先，极端太阳粒子事件（ESPE）是根据过去 15 千年来树木年轮和冰芯等自然档案中保存的宇宙成因同位素浓度的显着峰值推断出来的。其次，高精度星载光度测量揭示了数千颗类似于太阳的恒星上存在超级耀斑。这些太阳和恒星的极端现象在物理上是否相关仍然是一个悬而未决的问题。我们总结了目前的理解状态并讨论了可能将它们联系起来的物理机制。尽管超级耀斑和 ESPE 都是磁能储存和释放的极其高能的表现形式，但它们的关系似乎并不是一一对应的。它们的出现和能量可能取决于磁通量和拓扑如何控制辐射、质量喷射和粒子加速之间释放的能量的分配。
