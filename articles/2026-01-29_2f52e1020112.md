## Abstract
arXiv:2601.20358v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Coupled-wire description has been developed as a powerful framework for providing bosonic descriptions of strongly correlated quantum matter, with early applications to systems such as the cuprates and the integer and fractional quantum Hall states. In this topical review, we discuss recent developments of coupled-wire description in nanoscale systems, where it emerges not only as a theoretical tool but also as a highly tunable physical platform. In these nanoscale realizations, coupled-wire networks are formed by one-dimensional channels embedded in two-dimensional materials, most prominently in moir\'e and twisted structures. Such networks host a broad range of unconventional states of matter, including superconductivity, charge density waves, spin density waves, Mott insulating phases, Anderson insulating phases, quantum spin Hall states, quantum anomalous Hall states, and their fractionalized counterparts. The ability to electrically control interaction strength, confinement, and coupling between wires makes these systems qualitatively different from earlier realizations and allows continuous tuning between competing phases. Notably, recent work has demonstrated that the coupled-wire framework in moir\'e networks completes the trio of quantum Hall phenomena, encompassing quantum Hall, quantum spin Hall, and quantum anomalous Hall states, together with their fractional analogues. This development highlights coupled-wire networks in nanoscale materials as a versatile and experimentally relevant setting for exploring the interplay of topology, strong correlations, and low-dimensional physics.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.20358v1 公告类型：新 摘要：耦合线描述已被开发为一个强大的框架，用于提供强相关量子物质的玻色子描述，并早期应用于铜酸盐、整数和分数量子霍尔态等系统。在这篇专题综述中，我们讨论了纳米级系统中耦合线描述的最新进展，它不仅作为一种理论工具，而且作为一个高度可调的物理平台。在这些纳米级实现中，耦合线网络是由嵌入二维材料中的一维通道形成的，最突出的是摩尔纹和扭曲结构。这种网络承载着广泛的非常规物质态，包括超导性、电荷密度波、自旋密度波、莫特绝缘相、安德森绝缘相、量子自旋霍尔态、量子反常霍尔态及其分段对应物。电气控制导线之间的相互作用强度、限制和耦合的能力使这些系统在质量上不同于早期的实现，并允许在竞争阶段之间进行连续调整。值得注意的是，最近的工作表明，莫尔网络中的耦合线框架完成了量子霍尔现象的三重奏，包括量子霍尔、量子自旋霍尔和量子反常霍尔态，以及它们的分数类似物。这一发展凸显了纳米级材料中的耦合线网络作为探索拓扑、强相关性和低维物理相互作用的多功能且与实验相关的设置。
