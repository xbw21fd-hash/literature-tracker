## Abstract
Author(s): S. G. Wilkins et al. The ionization potential of radium monofluoride (RaF) was experimentally determined in agreement with predictions from theory, showing that its dissociation energy is greater than the energy required to remove an electron. This electronic structure places RaF in a group of molecules that possess long-lived Rydberg states that are stable against predissociation. [Phys. Rev. Research 8, L012012] Published Tue Jan 13, 2026
