## Abstract
arXiv:2602.01006v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Acoustic metamaterials offer exceptional control over wave propagation, but their potential remains unfulfilled due to fabrication constraints. Conventional processes yield mostly rigid, planar structures, whereas soft-matter alternatives have so far been confined to ultrasounds. This work overcomes prior limitations with a fully soft 3D metamaterial operating around 200Hz. The design combines a 3D-printed elastomer lattice with resonant inclusions of liquid metal, injected via a network of mesofluidic channels. Its dynamic response is derived from a hybrid strategy uniting a lumped-element model with finite element analysis. Simulations reveal how the dual-phase design decouples flexural and torsional modes, opening a subwavelength band gap for low-frequency elastic waves. Empirical validation is achieved via a custom camera-based vibrometer. Its high spatiotemporal resolution and full-field capabilities enable direct capture of local modes and evanescent waves underlying the band gap. Accelerometer data corroborate these findings and demonstrate greater attenuation than common silicone elastomers at only half of the density. By combining scalable fabrication, compliance, and operations at frequencies relevant to human tactile perception, this novel metamaterial paves the way for lightweight, high-performance cushioning and handles that protect users from harmful vibration exposure.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.01006v1 公告类型：新 摘要：声学超材料对波传播提供了卓越的控制，但由于制造限制，其潜力仍未得到发挥。传统工艺主要产生刚性的平面结构，而软物质替代品迄今为止仅限于超声波。这项工作克服了先前的限制，采用了工作频率约为 200Hz 的全软 3D 超材料。该设计将 3D 打印的弹性体晶格与通过介观流体通道网络注入的液态金属共振内含物相结合。其动态响应源自集总元模型与有限元分析相结合的混合策略。仿真揭示了双相设计如何解耦弯曲和扭转模式，为低频弹性波打开亚波长带隙。经验验证是通过基于相机的定制振动计实现的。其高时空分辨率和全场能力能够直接捕获带隙下方的局域模式和倏逝波。加速度计数据证实了这些发现，并显示出比普通有机硅弹性体更大的衰减，而密度仅为普通有机硅弹性体的一半。通过将可扩展的制造、合规性和与人类触觉感知相关的频率的操作相结合，这种新型超材料为轻质、高性能的缓冲垫和手柄铺平了道路，保护用户免受有害振动的影响。
