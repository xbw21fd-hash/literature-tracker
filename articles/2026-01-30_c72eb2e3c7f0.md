## Abstract
arXiv:2601.21724v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: This paper summarizes and consolidates fusion power-plant costing work performed in support of ARPA-E from 2017 through 2024, and documents the evolution of the associated analysis framework from early capital-cost-focused studies to a standards-aligned, auditable costing capability. Early efforts applied ARIES-style cost-scaling relations to generate Nth-of-a-kind (NOAK) estimates and were calibrated through a pilot study with Bechtel and Decysive Systems to benchmark balance-of-plant (BOP) costs and validate plant-level reasonableness from an engineering, procurement, and construction (EPC) perspective. Subsequent work, informed by Lucid Catalyst studies of nuclear cost drivers, expanded the methodology to treat indirect costs explicitly and to evaluate cost-reduction pathways for non-fusion-island systems through design-for-cost practices, modularization, centralized manufacturing, and learning. As ARPA-E's fusion portfolio expanded, these methods were applied across BETHE and GAMOW concepts (and select ALPHA revisits), including enhanced treatment of tritium handling and plant integration supported by Princeton/PPPL expertise. In 2023 the capability was refactored to align with the IAEA-GEN-IV EMWG-EPRI code-of-accounts lineage, while key ARIES-derived scaling relations were replaced by bottom-up subsystem models for dominant fusion cost drivers (e.g., magnets, lasers, power supplies, and power-core components) coupled to physics-informed power balances and engineering-constrained radial builds. These developments were implemented in the spreadsheet-based Fusion Economics code (FECONs) and released as an open-source Python framework (pyFECONs), providing a transparent mapping from subsystem estimates to standardized accounts and a consistent computation of LCOE.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.21724v1 公告类型：新 摘要：本文总结并巩固了 2017 年至 2024 年为支持 ARPA-E 所开展的聚变电厂成本核算工作，并记录了相关分析框架从早期以资本成本为中心的研究到符合标准、可审计的成本核算能力的演变。早期的工作应用 ARIES 式的成本缩放关系来生成 Nth-of-a-kind (NOAK) 估算，并通过与 Bechtel 和 Decysive Systems 进行的试点研究进行校准，以对工厂平衡 (BOP) 成本进行基准测试，并从工程、采购和施工 (EPC) 的角度验证工厂级的合理性。随后的工作以 Lucid Catalyst 对核成本驱动因素的研究为基础，扩展了明确处理间接成本的方法，并通过成本设计实践、模块化、集中制造和学习来评估非聚变岛系统的成本降低途径。随着 ARPA-E 核聚变产品组合的扩展，这些方法被应用于 BETHE 和 GAMOW 概念（以及选择的 ALPHA 重新审视），包括在普林斯顿/PPPL 专业知识支持下增强氚处理和工厂集成的处理。 2023 年，该能力进行了重构，以与 IAEA-GEN-IV EMWG-EPRI 账户代码谱系保持一致，而关键的 ARIES 衍生的缩放关系被自下而上的子系统模型取代，用于主要的聚变成本驱动因素（例如磁体、激光器、电源和动力核心组件），与物理信息功率平衡和工程约束的径向构建相结合。这些开发成果在基于电子表格的 Fusion Economics 代码 (FECONs) 中实现，并作为开源 Python 框架 (pyFECONs) 发布，提供从子系统估算到标准化账户的透明映射以及一致的 LCOE 计算。
