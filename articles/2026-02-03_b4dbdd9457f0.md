## Abstract
Author(s): Avdhesh K. Sharma, Bo Tai, Subhajit Roychowdhury, Premakumar Yanda, Ulrich Burkhardt, Xiaolong Feng, Claudia Felser, and Chandra Shekhar Here, the authors experimentally observe the anisotropic anomalous Hall effect (AHE) in GdTi 3 Bi 4 , despite the isotropic magnetization along respective crystallographic directions. This observation contradicts the conventional scaling of AHE with magnetization. In the presence of spin-orbit coupling and broken time-reversal symmetry within the Gd 4 f sublattice, the magnetization direction controls the orbital mixing in the Ti t 2 g bands. This relocates the Berry curvature hot spots and produces orientation-selective AHE. These findings establish GdTi 3 Bi 4 as a platform for novel quantum phenomena. [Phys. Rev. B 113, L060402] Published Tue Feb 03, 2026
