# 3D-printed low-voltage-driven ciliary hydrogel microactuators

## Abstract
Micrometre-sized, densely packed natural cilia that perform non-reciprocal 3D motions with dynamically tunable collective patterns are crucial for biological processes such as microscale locomotion1, nutrient acquisition2, cell trafficking3,4,5and embryonic and neurological development6,7,8. However, replicating these motions in artificial systems remains challenging given the limits of scalable, locally controllable soft-bodied actuation at the micrometre scale. Overcoming this challenge would enhance our understanding of ciliary dynamics, clarify their biological importance and enable new microscale devices and bioinspired technologies. Here we show a previously unrecognized fast electrical response of micrometre-scale hydrogels, induced by voltages down to 1.5 V without hydrolysis, with bending motions driven by ion migration across a nanometre-scale hydrogel network 3D-printed by two-photon polymerization, occurring within milliseconds. On the basis of these findings, we print gel microcilia arrays composed of a soft acrylic acid-co-acrylamide (AAc-co-AAm) hydrogel (modulus of approximately 1,000 Pa) that respond to electrical stimuli within milliseconds. Each microcilium measures 2–10 µm in diameter and 18–90 µm in height, achieving 3D rotational bending motion at up to 40 Hz, mirroring the geometry and dynamics of natural cilia. These gel microcilia maintain functionality after 330,000 continuous actuation cycles with less than 30% performance degradation. The gel microcilia arrays can be integrated on flexible polyimide substrates and fabricated at large scale using conventional lithography techniques. They also offer individual dynamic control by means of microelectrode arrays and enable fluid manipulation and particle transport at the micrometre scale.

## 摘要（中文）
微米大小、密集的天然纤毛以动态可调的集体模式执行非交互 3D 运动，对于微尺度运动 1、营养获取 2、细胞运输 3、4、5 以及胚胎和神经发育 6、7、8 等生物过程至关重要。然而，考虑到微米级可扩展、局部可控软体驱动的局限性，在人工系统中复制这些运动仍然具有挑战性。克服这一挑战将增强我们对纤毛动力学的理解，阐明其生物学重要性，并实现新的微型设备和仿生技术。在这里，我们展示了微米级水凝胶先前未被识别的快速电响应，由低至 1.5 V 的电压诱导而不水解，离子迁移驱动弯曲运动穿过通过双光子聚合 3D 打印的纳米级水凝胶网络，在几毫秒内发生。基于这些发现，我们打印了由柔软的丙烯酸-共-丙烯酰胺 (AAc-co-AAm) 水凝胶（模量约为 1,000 Pa）组成的凝胶微纤毛阵列，可在几毫秒内响应电刺激。每个微纤毛的直径为 2-10 µm，高度为 18-90 µm，可实现高达 40 Hz 的 3D 旋转弯曲运动，反映自然纤毛的几何形状和动力学。这些凝胶微纤毛在 330,000 次连续驱动循环后仍保持功能，性能下降不到 30%。凝胶微纤毛阵列可以集成在柔性聚酰亚胺基底上，并使用传统的光刻技术大规模制造。它们还通过微电极阵列提供单独的动态控制，并实现微米级的流体操纵和颗粒传输。
