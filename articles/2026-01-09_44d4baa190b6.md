## Abstract
arXiv:2601.04364v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: Entangled many-body states enable high-precision quantum sensing beyond the standard quantum limit. We develop interferometric sensing protocols based on quantum critical wavefunctions and compare their performance with Greenberger-Horne-Zeilinger (GHZ) and spin-squeezed states. Building on the idea of symmetries as a metrological resource, we introduce a symmetry-based algorithm to identify optimal measurement strategies. We illustrate this algorithm both for magnetic systems with internal symmetries and Rydberg-atom arrays with spatial symmetries. We study the robustness of criticality for quantum sensing under non-unitary deformations, symmetry-preserving and symmetry-breaking decoherence, and qubit loss -- identifying regimes where critical systems outperform GHZ states and showing that non-unitary deformation can even enhance sensing precision. Combined with recent results on log-depth preparation of critical wavefunctions, interferometric sensing in this setting appears increasingly promising.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.04364v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：纠缠多体态能够实现超越标准量子极限的高精度量子传感。我们开发基于量子临界波函数的干涉传感协议，并将其性能与 Greenberger-Horne-Zeilinger (GHZ) 和自旋压缩态进行比较。基于对称性作为计量资源的理念，我们引入了一种基于对称性的算法来确定最佳测量策略。我们针对具有内部对称性的磁系统和具有空间对称性的里德堡原子阵列来说明该算法。我们研究了非酉变形、对称性保持和对称破缺退相干以及量子位损失下量子传感临界性的鲁棒性，识别出关键系统优于 GHZ 状态的状态，并表明非酉变形甚至可以提高传感精度。结合关键波函数的测井深度准备的最新结果，这种情况下的干涉传感似乎越来越有前途。
