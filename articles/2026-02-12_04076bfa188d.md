## Abstract
arXiv:2512.11341v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: When multiple degrees of freedom share similar energy scales in quantum materials, intertwined electronic orders, which exhibit broken symmetries, are often strongly coupled. Recent studies on kagome superconductors such as CsV$_3$Sb$_5$ report rotational and time-reversal symmetry breaking linked to a charge density wave. Here, we observe a momentum-selective response of the electronic structure of CsV$_3$Sb$_5$ to an external magnetic field. By performing angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy in a tuneable magnetic field, we demonstrate that the response of the electronic structure is compatible with piezomagnetism along with strong orbital selectivity. Our results show that the origin of the time-reversal symmetry breaking is associated with the vanadium Van Hove singularities at the onset of the charge density wave order. We also demonstrate the presence of fluctuations beyond the charge ordering temperature. Our results reveal that magnetic fields can be used as tuning knobs for disentangling intertwined orders in the momentum space for quantum materials.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.11341v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：当量子材料中的多个自由度共享相似的能量尺度时，相互缠绕的电子序（表现出破缺对称性）通常是强耦合的。最近对 Kagome 超导体（例如 CsV$_3$Sb$_5$）的研究报告了与电荷密度波相关的旋转和时间反转对称性破缺。在这里，我们观察到 CsV$_3$Sb$_5$ 电子结构对外部磁场的动量选择性响应。通过在可调谐磁场中进行角分辨光电子能谱，我们证明电子结构的响应与压磁性以及强轨道选择性兼容。我们的结果表明，时间反演对称性破缺的起源与电荷密度波序开始时的钒范霍夫奇点有关。我们还证明了超出电荷排序温度的波动的存在。我们的结果表明，磁场可以用作调谐旋钮，用于解开量子材料动量空间中相互交织的秩序。
