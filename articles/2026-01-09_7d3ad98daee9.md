## Abstract
arXiv:2601.04838v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Metallenes are atomically thin, nonlayered two-dimensional materials. While they have appealing properties, their isotropic metallic bonding makes their stabilization difficult and presents considerable challenges to their synthesis and practical applications. However, their stabilization can still be achieved by suspending them in the pores of two-dimensional template materials, making the properties of lateral interfaces of metallenes scientifically relevant. Here, we combined density-functional theory and universal machine-learning interatomic potentials to study lateral interfaces between graphene and 45 metallenes with various profiles. We optimized the interfaces and analyzed their energies, electronic structures, and stabilities at room temperature, defect formations, and structural deformations. While broad trends were identified using machine-learning analysis of all interfaces, density-functional theory was the main tool for studying the microscopic properties of selected elements. We found that the interfaces are the most stable energetically and with respect to lattice mismatch, defect formation, and lateral strain when their profiles were geometrically smooth. The most stable interfaces are found for transition metals. In addition, we demonstrate how universal machine-learning interatomic potentials now offer the accuracy required for the modeling of graphene-metallene interfaces. By systematically expanding the understanding of metallenes' interface properties, we hope these results guide and accelerate their synthesis to enable future applications and benefit from metallenes' appealing properties.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.04838v1 公告类型：新 摘要：金属烯是原子级薄的非层状二维材料。虽然它们具有吸引人的特性，但它们的各向同性金属键合使其稳定化变得困难，并对其合成和实际应用提出了相当大的挑战。然而，仍然可以通过将它们悬浮在二维模板材料的孔中来实现它们的稳定性，从而使金属烯横向界面的性质具有科学意义。在这里，我们结合密度泛函理论和通用机器学习原子间势来研究石墨烯和具有不同轮廓的 45 种金属之间的横向界面。我们优化了界面并分析了它们的能量、电子结构、室温稳定性、缺陷形成和结构变形。虽然通过对所有界面的机器学习分析确定了广泛的趋势，但密度泛函理论是研究所选元素微观特性的主要工具。我们发现，当界面轮廓几何光滑时，界面在能量上以及晶格失配、缺陷形成和横向应变方面是最稳定的。最稳定的界面是过渡金属。此外，我们还演示了通用机器学习原子间势现在如何提供石墨烯-金属界面建模所需的准确性。通过系统地扩展对金属烯界面特性的理解，我们希望这些结果能够指导和加速其合成，以实现未来的应用并受益于金属烯的吸引人的特性。
