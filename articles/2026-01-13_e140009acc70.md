# Polypharmacology Browser PPB3: A Web-based Deep Learning Tool for Target Prediction Using ChEMBL Data

## Abstract
Drug-like molecules often interact with multiple biological targets. Assessing this polypharmacology is essential for drug development. Here we trained deep neural networks to associate bioactive molecules up to 80 non-hydrogen atoms in size reported in ChEMBL 34, represented as binary substructure fingerprints, with lists of targets on which the molecules are 50% active at ≤10 µM. We included 2,496,555 interactions between 1,187,089 molecules and 7,546 targets having at least five reported active molecules, including single proteins, protein complexes, protein families, cell lines, organisms, and further target types. This represents a much larger dataset than previously reported models, which were mostly limited to protein targets. Our DNNs achieve good performance in terms of recall and precision per molecule and per target, as illustrated by overall statistics and by a case study in comparison with other online prediction tools. PPB3 predictions can be performed online at https://ppb3.gdb.tools/.

## 摘要（中文）
药物样分子通常与多个生物靶点相互作用。评估这种多药理学对于药物开发至关重要。在这里，我们训练深度神经网络将 ChEMBL 34 中报告的大小高达 80 个非氢原子的生物活性分子（表示为二元子结构指纹）与分子在 ≤10 µM 时具有 50% 活性的目标列表相关联。我们纳入了 1,187,089 个分子和 7,546 个靶标之间的 2,496,555 种相互作用，其中至少有 5 个已报告的活性分子，包括单个蛋白质、蛋白质复合物、蛋白质家族、细胞系、生物体和其他靶标类型。这代表了比之前报道的模型大得多的数据集，之前报道的模型大多仅限于蛋白质目标。总体统计数据以及与其他在线预测工具相比的案例研究表明，我们的 DNN 在每个分子和每个目标的召回率和精度方面取得了良好的性能。 PPB3 预测可以在 https://ppb3.gdb.tools/ 上在线执行。
