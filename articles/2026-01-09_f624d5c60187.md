## Abstract
arXiv:2508.15592v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Molecular modeling of thermally activated chemistry in condensed phases is essential to understand polymerization, depolymerization, and other processing steps of molecular materials. Current methods typically combine molecular dynamics (MD) simulations to describe short-time relaxation with a stochastic description of predetermined chemical reactions. Possible reactions are often selected on the basis of geometric criteria, such as a capture distance between reactive atoms. Although these simulations have provided valuable insight, the approximations used to determine possible reactions often lead to significant molecular strain and unrealistic structures. We show that the local molecular environment surrounding the reactive site plays a crucial role in determining the resulting molecular strain energy and, in turn, the associated reaction rates. We develop a graph neural network capable of predicting the strain energy associated with a cyclization reaction from the pre-reaction, local, molecular environment surrounding the reactive site. The model is trained on a large dataset of condensed-phase reactions during the activation of polyacrylonitrile (PAN) obtained from MD simulations and can be used to adjust relative reaction rates in condensed systems and advance our understanding of thermally activated chemical processes in complex materials

arXiv:2508.15592v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：凝聚相热活化化学的分子建模对于理解分子材料的聚合、解聚和其他加工步骤至关重要。目前的方法通常将分子动力学 (MD) 模拟与预定化学反应的随机描述相结合来描述短时弛豫。可能的反应通常是根据几何标准来选择的，例如反应原子之间的捕获距离。尽管这些模拟提供了有价值的见解，但用于确定可能反应的近似值通常会导致显着的分子应变和不切实际的结构。我们表明，反应位点周围的局部分子环境在确定所得分子应变能以及相关反应速率方面起着至关重要的作用。我们开发了一种图神经网络，能够根据反应位点周围的预反应、局部分子环境预测与环化反应相关的应变能。该模型在通过 MD 模拟获得的聚丙烯腈 (PAN) 活化过程中的凝聚相反应的大型数据集上进行训练，可用于调整凝聚系统中的相对反应速率，并加深我们对复杂材料中热活化化学过程的理解
