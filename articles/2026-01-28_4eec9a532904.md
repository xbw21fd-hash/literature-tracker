## Abstract
arXiv:2601.19076v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Generative models for materials have achieved strong performance on periodic bulk crystals, yet their ability to generalize across scale transitions to finite nanostructures remains largely untested. We introduce Crystal-to-Nanoparticle (C2NP), a systematic benchmark for evaluating generative models when moving between infinite crystalline unit cells and finite nanoparticles, where surface effects and size-dependent distortions dominate. C2NP defines two complementary tasks: (i) generating nanoparticles of specified radii from periodic unit cells, testing whether models capture surface truncation and geometric constraints; and (ii) recovering bulk lattice parameters and space-group symmetry from finite particle configurations, assessing whether models can infer underlying crystallographic order despite surface perturbations. Using diverse materials as a structurally consistent testbed, we construct over 170,000 nanoparticle configurations by carving particles from supercells derived from DFT-relaxed crystal unit cells, and introduce size-based splits that separate interpolation from extrapolation regimes. Experiments with state-of-the-art approaches, including diffusion, flow-matching, and variational models, show that even when losses are low, models often fail geometrically under distribution shift, yielding large lattice-recovery errors and near-zero joint accuracy on structure and symmetry. Overall, our results suggest that current methods rely on template memorization rather than scalable physical generalization. C2NP offers a controlled, reproducible framework for diagnosing these failures, with immediate applications to nanoparticle catalyst design, nanostructured hydrides for hydrogen storage, and materials discovery. Dataset and code are available at https://github.com/KurbanIntelligenceLab/C2NP.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.19076v1 公告类型：新 摘要：材料生成模型在周期性块体晶体上取得了强大的性能，但它们将跨尺度过渡推广到有限纳米结构的能力在很大程度上尚未经过测试。我们引入了晶体到纳米粒子（C2NP），这是一个系统基准，用于评估在无限晶体晶胞和有限纳米粒子之间移动时的生成模型，其中表面效应和尺寸相关的扭曲占主导地位。 C2NP 定义了两个互补的任务：（i）从周期性晶胞生成指定半径的纳米颗粒，测试模型是否捕获表面截断和几何约束； (ii) 从有限粒子配置中恢复体晶格参数和空间群对称性，评估模型是否可以在表面扰动的情况下推断出潜在的晶体顺序。我们使用不同的材料作为结构一致的测试平台，通过从 DFT 松弛晶体晶胞衍生的超级晶胞中雕刻粒子，构建了超过 170,000 个纳米粒子配置，并引入了基于尺寸的分割，将插值与外推机制分开。采用最先进方法（包括扩散、流匹配和变分模型）进行的实验表明，即使损耗很低，模型在分布偏移下也常常会出现几何故障，从而产生较大的晶格恢复误差以及结构和对称性上接近于零的联合精度。总的来说，我们的结果表明当前的方法依赖于模板记忆而不是可扩展的物理概括。 C2NP 提供了一个受控的、可重复的框架来诊断这些故障，并可立即应用于纳米颗粒催化剂设计、用于储氢的纳米结构氢化物和材料发现。数据集和代码可在 https://github.com/KurbanIntelligenceLab/C2NP 获取。
