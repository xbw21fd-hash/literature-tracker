## Abstract
arXiv:2601.14754v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: We study anomalous localization in a one-dimensional non-Hermitian quasicrystal with a spatially disordered imaginary gauge field. The system is a generalized Aubry-Andr\'{e}-Harper (AAH) chain with asymmetric nearest- and next-nearest-neighbor hoppings generated by a Bernoulli imaginary gauge field and a quasiperiodic onsite potential. In the standard non-Hermitian AAH limit, the system undergoes a transition from a fully erratic non-Hermitian skin effect (ENHSE) phase to a fully localized phase. We show that the fractal dimension cannot distinguish these phases, whereas the Lyapunov exponent and center-of-mass fluctuations provide sharp diagnostics. This transition is accompanied by a complex-to-real spectral change under periodic boundary conditions and a topological change of the spectral winding number. With next-nearest-neighbor hopping, we uncover an anomalous mobility edge separating Anderson-localized states from ENHSE states, rather than extended states. This mobility edge is captured by an energy-dependent winding number that vanishes in the localized regime. Finally, we propose a dynamical probe based on wave-packet expansion: for typical disorder realizations, the dynamics shows winding-controlled drift and disorder-selected pinning or boundary-wrapping recurrence, while disorder averaging restores Hermitian-like transport. These results offer practical spectral, topological, and dynamical diagnostics of anomalous localization and mobility edges in non-Hermitian quasicrystals.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.14754v1 公告类型：新 摘要：我们研究具有空间无序虚规范场的一维非厄米特准晶体中的异常局域化。该系统是一个广义的 Aubry-Andr\'{e}-Harper (AAH) 链，具有由伯努利虚规范场和准周期现场电势生成的不对称最近邻和次近邻跳跃。在标准非厄米 AAH 极限中，系统经历从完全不稳定的非厄米趋肤效应 (ENHSE) 阶段到完全局部化阶段的转变。我们证明分形维数无法区分这些相，而李亚普诺夫指数和质心涨落提供了敏锐的诊断。这种转变伴随着周期性边界条件下的复杂到真实的光谱变化以及光谱缠绕数的拓扑变化。通过下一个最近邻跳跃，我们发现了将安德森局部状态与 ENHSE 状态（而不是扩展状态）分开的异常移动边缘。这种迁移率边缘由能量相关的绕数捕获，该绕数在局部区域中消失。最后，我们提出了一种基于波包展开的动力学探针：对于典型的无序实现，动力学显示了缠绕控制的漂移和无序选择的钉扎或边界包裹复发，而无序平均恢复了类厄米特输运。这些结果为非厄米准晶体中的异常局域化和迁移率边缘提供了实用的光谱、拓扑和动力学诊断。
