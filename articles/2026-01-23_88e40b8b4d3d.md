## Abstract
arXiv:2510.19146v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: The Hopfield associative memory model stores random patterns in synaptic couplings according to Hebb's rule and retrieves them through gradient descent on an energy function. This conventional setting, where neurons are assumed to have monotonic transfer functions, has been central to understanding associative memory. Morita (1993, Neural Netw. 6 115), however, showed that introducing non-monotonic transfer functions can dramatically enhance retrieval performance. While this phenomenon has been qualitatively examined, a full quantitative theory remains elusive due to the difficulty of analysis in the absence of an underlying energy function. In this work, we apply dynamical mean-field theory to the discrete-time synchronous retrieval dynamics of the non-monotonic model, which succeeds in accurately characterizing its macroscopic dynamical properties. We also derive conditions for retrieval states, and clarify their relation to previous studies. Our results provide new insights into the non-equilibrium retrieval dynamics of associative memory models.

arXiv:2510.19146v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：Hopfield 联想记忆模型根据 Hebb 规则存储突触耦合中的随机模式，并通过能量函数的梯度下降来检索它们。这种传统的设置，假设神经元具有单调的传递函数，一直是理解联想记忆的核心。然而，Morita (1993, Neural Netw. 6 115) 表明，引入非单调传递函数可以显着提高检索性能。尽管已经对这种现象进行了定性研究，但由于缺乏潜在的能量函数而难以进行分析，因此完整的定量理论仍然难以捉摸。在这项工作中，我们将动态平均场理论应用于非单调模型的离散时间同步检索动力学，成功地准确地表征了其宏观动力学特性。我们还推导了检索状态的条件，并阐明了它们与先前研究的关系。我们的结果为联想记忆模型的非平衡检索动力学提供了新的见解。
