## Abstract
arXiv:2602.03187v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Understanding the mechanism of turbulent skin-friction drag (TSD) generation is of fundamental and practical importance for designing effective drag reduction strategies. However, many previous studies adopted correlation analysis to reveal the causal map between turbulent motions and TSD generation, an approach that is potentially risky as correlation does not necessarily imply causation. In this study, a novel causal inference method called Liang-Kleeman information flow (LKIF) is utilized for the first time to identify the velocity-induced causal structures related to TSD generation in a turbulent channel flow. The statistical properties of the causal structures are comprehensively investigated. The positive and negative causal structures, defined by their signs and respectively associated with an increase and decrease in TSD information entropy, promote and suppress the generation of extreme TSD. Particularly, we find that the underlying physics of causal structures is essentially associated with the processes of streamwise streaks and rolls approaching or receding from the extreme events. Results indicate that the physics-informed LKIF framework can reveal a more explicit and interpretable causal relationship than correlation analysis.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.03187v1 公告类型：新 摘要：了解湍流表面摩擦阻力 (TSD) 产生的机制对于设计有效的减阻策略具有基础和实际意义。然而，之前的许多研究采用相关性分析来揭示湍流运动与 TSD 生成之间的因果图，这种方法存在潜在风险，因为相关性并不一定意味着因果关系。在这项研究中，首次利用一种称为梁-克利曼信息流（LKIF）的新颖因果推理方法来识别与湍流通道流中 TSD 生成相关的速度引起的因果结构。因果结构的统计特性得到了全面的研究。正负因果结构由其符号定义，分别与 TSD 信息熵的增加和减少相关，促进和抑制极端 TSD 的产生。特别是，我们发现因果结构的基础物理本质上与流向条纹和滚动接近或远离极端事件的过程相关。结果表明，基于物理的 LKIF 框架可以揭示比相关分析更明确和可解释的因果关系。
