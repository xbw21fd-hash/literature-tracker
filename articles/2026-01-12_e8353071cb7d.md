## Abstract
arXiv:2601.05660v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: The response of the Fermi energy to external perturbations governs key physical observables at metallic interfaces. Although this response admits a local formulation in terms of the Fukui function, its evaluation has traditionally been limited by inherent approximations, fundamentally rooted in the difficulty of adding a finite charge in a periodic system. We present an exact resolution to this problem that leverages the screening properties of electronic conductors to compute Fukui functions via a finite electric field. The resulting linear-response theory yields strictly quadratic error scaling of Fermi-level shifts across representative platinum surfaces, achieving sub-meV accuracy up to fields of 0.1 V/\AA. The approach is further validated by reproducing work-function changes under molecular perturbations, and by providing mean-field estimates of electrode potentials that yield capacitance--voltage curves consistent with experiment. Our findings establish a rigorous foundation for a local theory relating electrostatic screening and Fermi-energy variations at metallic interfaces.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.05660v1 公告类型：新 摘要：费米能量对外部扰动的响应控制着金属界面处的关键物理可观测值。尽管该响应承认福井函数的局部公式，但其评估传统上受到固有近似的限制，从根本上根源于在周期系统中添加有限电荷的困难。我们针对这个问题提出了一个精确的解决方案，利用电子导体的屏蔽特性通过有限电场计算福井函数。由此产生的线性响应理论产生了代表性铂表面上费米能级位移的严格二次误差缩放，实现了高达 0.1 V/\AA 场的亚兆伏精度。通过再现分子扰动下的功函数变化，并提供电极电势的平均场估计，产生与实验一致的电容-电压曲线，进一步验证了该方法。我们的研究结果为有关金属界面静电屏蔽和费米能量变化的局部理论奠定了严格的基础。
