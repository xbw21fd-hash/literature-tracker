## Abstract
arXiv:2602.11097v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: We study the training and performance of physics-informed learning for initial and boundary value problems (IBVP) with physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) from a statistical learning perspective. Specifically, we restrict ourselves to parameterizations with hard initial and boundary condition constraints and reformulate the problem of estimating PINN parameters as a statistical learning problem. From this perspective, the physics penalty on the IBVP residuals can be better understood not as a regularizing term bus as an infinite source of indirect data, and the learning process as fitting the PINN distribution of residuals $p(y \mid x, t, w) q(x, t) $ to the true data-generating distribution $\delta(0) q(x, t)$ by minimizing the Kullback-Leibler divergence between the true and PINN distributions. Furthermore, this analysis show that physics-informed learning with PINNs is a singular learning problem, and we employ singular learning theory tools, namely the so-called Local Learning Coefficient (Lau et al., 2025) to analyze the estimates of PINN parameters obtained via stochastic optimization for a heat equation IBVP. Finally, we discuss implications of this analysis on the quantification of predictive uncertainty of PINNs and the extrapolation capacity of PINNs.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.11097v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：我们从统计学习的角度研究了物理信息神经网络（PINN）对初始和边界值问题（IBVP）的物理信息学习的训练和性能。具体来说，我们将自己限制在具有硬初始和边界条件约束的参数化，并将估计 PINN 参数的问题重新表述为统计学习问题。从这个角度来看，IBVP 残差上的物理惩罚可以更好地理解为不将正则项总线视为间接数据的无限源，并且将学习过程理解为通过最小化真实分布和 PINN 分布之间的 Kullback-Leibler 散度，将残差 $p(y \mid x, t, w) q(x, t) $ 的 PINN 分布拟合到真实数据生成分布 $\delta(0) q(x, t)$ 。此外，该分析表明，PINN 的物理信息学习是一个奇异学习问题，我们采用奇异学习理论工具，即所谓的局部学习系数（Lau et al., 2025）来分析通过热方程 IBVP 的随机优化获得的 PINN 参数的估计。最后，我们讨论了该分析对 PINN 预测不确定性量化和 PINN 外推能力的影响。
