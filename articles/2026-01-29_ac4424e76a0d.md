## Abstract
Neural quantum states (NQS) have emerged as powerful tools for simulating many-body quantum systems, but their practical use is often hindered by limitations of current sampling techniques. Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods suffer from slow mixing and require manual tuning, while autoregressive NQS impose restrictive architectural constraints that complicate the enforcement of symmetries and the construction of determinant-based multi-state wave functions. In this work, we introduce neural importance resampling (NIR), a new sampling algorithm that combines importance resampling with a separately trained autoregressive proposal network. This approach enables efficient and unbiased sampling without constraining the NQS architecture. We demonstrate that NIR supports stable and scalable training, including for multi-state NQS, and mitigates issues faced by MCMC and autoregressive approaches. Numerical experiments on the 2D transverse-field Ising and J1–J2 Heisenberg models show that NIR outperforms MCMC in challenging regimes and yields results competitive with state of the art methods. Our results establish NIR as a robust alternative for sampling in variational NQS algorithms.

## 摘要（中文）
神经量子态（NQS）已成为模拟多体量子系统的强大工具，但其实际应用往往受到当前采样技术的限制。马尔可夫链蒙特卡罗 (MCMC) 方法混合缓慢，需要手动调整，而自回归 NQS 施加了限制性的架构约束，使对称性的执行和基于行列式的多状态波函数的构造变得复杂。在这项工作中，我们引入了神经重要性重采样（NIR），这是一种新的采样算法，它将重要性重采样与单独训练的自回归提议网络相结合。这种方法可以在不限制 NQS 架构的情况下实现高效且无偏差的采样。我们证明 NIR 支持稳定且可扩展的训练，包括多状态 NQS，并缓解 MCMC 和自回归方法面临的问题。二维横向场 Ising 和 J1-J2 海森堡模型的数值实验表明，NIR 在具有挑战性的情况下优于 MCMC，并且产生的结果可与最先进的方法相媲美。我们的结果表明 NIR 是变分 NQS 算法中采样的可靠替代方案。
