## Abstract
arXiv:2602.04960v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: Although in general boundary conditions do not affect the bulk properties of a system, some of them are special and defy such expectation. This is the case, for instance, of those inducing geometrical frustration in a classical magnet. Recently, the study of such settings in quantum systems (dubbed topological frustration) has uncovered peculiar features, interesting both from a fundamental and technological point of view. In this work, we present and discuss the behavior of several quantum resources in presence of TF, namely the (disconnected) entanglement entropy and the non-stabilizerness Renyi entropy. We will show that, compared to their non-frustrated counterparts, TF adds a distinct contribution to these resources, due to a stable, delocalized, topological excitation. Remarkably, this contribution can be calculated analytically, due to its similarities with that of a W-state.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.04960v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：虽然一般情况下边界条件不会影响系统的整体属性，但其中一些边界条件很特殊并且违背了这种期望。例如，在经典磁铁中引起几何挫败的情况就是这种情况。最近，对量子系统中此类设置（称为拓扑挫败）的研究发现了一些奇特的特征，从基础和技术的角度来看，这些特征都很有趣。在这项工作中，我们提出并讨论了存在 TF 的几种量子资源的行为，即（断开的）纠缠熵和非稳定 Renyi 熵。我们将证明，与非受挫对应物相比，TF 由于稳定的、离域的拓扑激励而对这些资源做出了明显的贡献。值得注意的是，由于其与 W 状态的贡献相似，因此可以通过分析计算该贡献。
