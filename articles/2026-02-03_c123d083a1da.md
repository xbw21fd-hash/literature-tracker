## Abstract
arXiv:2602.00363v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Studying the electrical signals generated by living cells is key to understanding numerous biological phenomena. Electrochromic optical recording (ECORE) uses the electrochromism exhibited by certain materials to noninvasively measure these signals in real time. In this work, we report on the development of ECORE based on a high-NA microscope objective. We demonstrate the recording of extracellular action potentials from cardiomyocytes with single-cell resolution and a high sensitivity of 3 {\mu}V, which compares favorably to the previous record for any ECORE setup. Combining ECORE with microscopy simplifies the optical setup, allows for the simultaneous imaging of specimens, and makes ECORE accessible to a broader community of researchers, allowing for a better understanding of the biological processes that are integral to life.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.00363v1 公告类型：新 摘要：研究活细胞产生的电信号是理解众多生物现象的关键。电致变色光学记录（ECORE）利用某些材料表现出的电致变色来实时无创地测量这些信号。在这项工作中，我们报告了基于高数值孔径显微镜物镜的 ECORE 的开发。我们展示了以单细胞分辨率和 3 μV 的高灵敏度记录心肌细胞的细胞外动作电位，这与任何 ECORE 设置的先前记录相比都是有利的。 ECORE 与显微镜的结合简化了光学设置，允许同时对样本进行成像，并使 ECORE 可供更广泛的研究人员使用，从而更好地了解生命不可或缺的生物过程。
