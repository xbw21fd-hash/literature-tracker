## Abstract
arXiv:2601.20963v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Geometrically frustrated triangular-lattice magnets provide fertile ground for realizing intriguing quantum phases such as spin supersolids. A common expectation is that spin-orbit coupling (SOC), which breaks continuous spin rotational symmetry, destabilizes these phases by gapping their low-energy modes. Revisiting this assumption, we map out the SOC-field phase diagram of a frustrated triangular-lattice magnet using spin-wave theory and infinite density-matrix renormalization group (iDMRG) simulations. We find that while infinitesimally weak SOC indeed drives a zero-temperature instability of the supersolid by opening a gap, certain supersolid states remain thermodynamically stable at non-zero temperatures. This reveals a previously unrecognized mechanism in which thermal fluctuations counteract SOC to stabilize supersolidity. The resulting finite-temperature supersolids retain key responses, including a giant magnetocaloric effect, highlighting their potential relevance to real materials. At larger SOC, the system transitions into distinct magnetic orders, including a skyrmion lattice, completing a unified phase diagram.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.20963v1 公告类型：新 摘要：几何受挫三角晶格磁体为实现自旋超固体等有趣的量子相提供了肥沃的土壤。人们普遍的期望是，自旋轨道耦合（SOC）打破了连续自旋旋转对称性，通过间隙低能模式来破坏这些相的稳定。重新审视这一假设，我们使用自旋波理论和无限密度矩阵重整化群 (iDMRG) 模拟绘制了受挫三角晶格磁体的 SOC 场相图。我们发现，虽然无限弱的 SOC 确实通过打开间隙驱动了超固体的零温度不稳定性，但某些超固体状态在非零温度下仍保持热力学稳定。这揭示了一种以前未被认识的机制，其中热波动抵消 SOC 以稳定超固体。由此产生的有限温度超固体保留了关键响应，包括巨大的磁热效应，突出了它们与真实材料的潜在相关性。在较大的 SOC 下，系统转变为不同的磁序，包括斯格明子晶格，从而完成统一的相图。
