## Abstract
Despite the transformative potential of nanomaterials in drug delivery, imaging, and regenerative medicine, their clinical success remains bottlenecked by the challenge of achieving predictive biocompatibility. This six-year (2019–2025) critical review synthesizes the translational landscape, moving beyond simple descriptions of preclinical assessments to analyze the factors that govern clinical trial eligibility and outcome. We argue that while in vitro efficacy is often achieved, the primary barriers to clinical adoption are systematically rooted in manufacturing irreproducibility, regulatory misalignment, and unpredictable long-term immunotoxicity—often in that order of importance. Focusing on clinically relevant platforms—including lipid, polymeric, metal-based, and carbon systems—we analyze how conventional preclinical models fail to accurately predict human responses, particularly regarding hemocompatibility and genotoxicity. The review highlights emerging strategies—such as biomimetic design, green synthesis, and AI-driven toxicity modeling —that offer pathways to de-risk translation. Case studies, from successful COVID-19 lipid nanoparticles to stalled metal-based cancer agents, illustrate that clinical adoption is dictated not by initial efficacy, but by the material’s capacity for sustainable, scalable, and safe production. We conclude that accelerating the clinical utility of nanomaterials demands a paradigm shift: the focus must move from optimizing biological effect to prioritizing predictive translational design and coordinated interdisciplinary quality control.

## 摘要（中文）
尽管纳米材料在药物输送、成像和再生医学方面具有变革潜力，但其临床成功仍然受到实现预测生物相容性挑战的瓶颈。这项为期六年（2019-2025）的严格审查综合了转化前景，超越了对临床前评估的简单描述，分析了控制临床试验资格和结果的因素。我们认为，虽然体外疗效通常可以实现，但临床采用的主要障碍系统性地根源于制造的不可重复性、监管失调和不可预测的长期免疫毒性——通常按重要性顺序排列。我们专注于临床相关平台（包括脂质、聚合物、金属基和碳系统），分析传统的临床前模型如何无法准确预测人类反应，特别是在血液相容性和遗传毒性方面。该评论强调了新兴策略，例如仿生设计、绿色合成和人工智能驱动的毒性模型，这些策略提供了降低翻译风险的途径。从成功的 COVID-19 脂质纳米粒子到停滞不前的金属抗癌药物的案例研究表明，临床采用并非取决于最初的疗效，而是取决于材料的可持续、可扩展和安全生产的能力。我们的结论是，加速纳米材料的临床应用需要范式转变：重点必须从优化生物效应转向优先考虑预测性转化设计和协调的跨学科质量控制。
