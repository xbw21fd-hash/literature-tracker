## Abstract
Asymmetric catalysts exert control over the reactivity and enantioselectivity of chemical reactions, often through their large size and highly flexible geometries. Current quantum chemical calculations provide a powerful means to predict enantioselectivity through exhaustive conformational sampling at the selectivity-determining transition state (TS). However, this approach still relies on a predetermined selectivity-determining step and on the assumption that selectivity is governed by the Boltzmann distribution of TS conformers. To address this issue, constructing a reaction path network that captures the kinetically accessible regions of the potential energy surface is essential. In this study, we applied a delta-learning neural network potential (ΔNNP) to an asymmetric organocatalyst belonging to the imidodiphosphorimidates (IDPi) family, comprising over 200 atoms. The ΔNNP demonstrated accuracy comparable to that of density functional theory (DFT) using GFN2-xTB as the baseline method and learning the DFT–GFN2-xTB energy differences through the neural network model. Using the ΔNNP-based single component - artificial force induced reaction (denoted by NNP/AFIR) method, we constructed a reaction path network containing 48463 paths. Kinetic simulations based on this network were then performed to predict the reaction mechanism and enantioselectivity. Within the network, neumerous paths, including asynchronous concerted and stepwise mechanisms, were found to be energetically competitive and thus to contribute to the overall kinetics. Traffic volume analysis further revealed that intermediates with negligible yields can still play important kinetic roles. Overall, the NNP/AFIR approach would provide a powerful framework not only for deepening mechanistic understanding but also for accelerating the rational design of asymmetric catalytic systems with high reactivity and enantioselectivity.

## 摘要（中文）
不对称催化剂通常通过其大尺寸和高度灵活的几何形状来控制化学反应的反应性和对映选择性。当前的量子化学计算提供了一种强大的方法，通过在选择性决定的过渡态 (TS) 处进行详尽的构象采样来预测对映选择性。然而，这种方法仍然依赖于预定的选择性确定步骤，并假设选择性由 TS 构象异构体的玻尔兹曼分布控制。为了解决这个问题，构建一个反应路径网络来捕获势能表面的动力学可及区域至关重要。在这项研究中，我们将 Delta 学习神经网络势 (ΔNNP) 应用于属于亚胺二磷酸酯 (IDPi) 家族（由 200 多个原子组成）的不对称有机催化剂。使用 GFN2-xTB 作为基线方法并通过神经网络模型学习 DFT-GFN2-xTB 能量差异，ΔNNP 表现出与密度泛函理论 (DFT) 相当的精度。采用基于ΔNNP的单分量-人工力诱导反应（记为NNP/AFIR）方法，构建了包含48463条路径的反应路径网络。然后进行基于该网络的动力学模拟以预测反应机理和对映选择性。在网络内，许多路径，包括异步协调和逐步机制，被发现具有积极的竞争性，从而有助于整体动力学。流量分析进一步表明，产量可忽略不计的中间体仍然可以发挥重要的动力学作用。总体而言，NNP/AFIR方法不仅可以为加深机理理解提供强大的框架，而且可以加速具有高反应活性和对映选择性的不对称催化系统的合理设计。
