## Abstract
Author(s): Hui Zhou, Qilong Hu, Yuquan Chen, Tianyun Wang, Fangzhou Jin, Yunlan Ji, Jianpei Geng, and Xinhua Peng Controlled conversion between distinct classes of multipartite entangled states is crucial for quantum technologies, but direct interconversion is impossible, due to the states’ inequivalence under local operations. More sophisticated dynamical protocols are required; unfortunately, conventional adiabatic methods face a trade-off between fidelity and speed. This study employs inverse engineering to design accelerated adiabatic passages in a spin-chain system, enabling rapid W -to-GHZ conversion, as experimentally verified on an NMR quantum processor. The work highlights the potential of inverse engineering for efficient quantum state manipulation in many-body systems. [Phys. Rev. Applied 25, 014056] Published Fri Jan 23, 2026
