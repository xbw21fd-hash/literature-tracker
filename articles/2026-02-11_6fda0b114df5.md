# Multi-centennial internal variability in the North Atlantic could drive additional warming over Europe

## Abstract
Europe has experienced abnormal warming over the recent decades. Model-based studies highlight that multi-centennial internal variability of the North Atlantic can strongly affect European temperatures. However, the limited availability of high-resolution proxy records has hindered observational assessment of the existence and amplitude of such variability in the real climate system. Here, we compile annual-to-decadal proxy-based Holocene reconstructions, instrumental observations, and climate model simulations to demonstrate the existence of this multi-centennial variability mode and quantify its amplitude. We show that this mode is closely tied to the internal variability of the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation. Its temporal evolution explains part of the observed 20thcentury variability, and a shift towards a positive phase in the late 1990s can explain the recent amplified warming over Europe. When its amplitude is constrained by observations, this internal variability may enhance anthropogenic warming in Northern Europe by up to 30% over 2000–2035.

## 摘要（中文）
近几十年来，欧洲经历了异常变暖。基于模型的研究强调，北大西洋数百年来的内部变化会强烈影响欧洲的气温。然而，高分辨率代理记录的有限可用性阻碍了对真实气候系统中这种变化的存在和幅度的观测评估。在这里，我们编译了基于年度到十年代理的全新世重建、仪器观测和气候模型模拟，以证明这种数百年变率模式的存在并量化其幅度。我们表明，这种模式与大西洋经向翻转环流的内部变化密切相关。它的时间演变解释了所观察到的 20 世纪变化的部分原因，而 20 世纪 90 年代末向正阶段的转变可以解释最近欧洲变暖的加剧。当其幅度受到观测的限制时，这种内部变化可能会使北欧的人为变暖在 2000 年至 2035 年期间增加高达 30%。
