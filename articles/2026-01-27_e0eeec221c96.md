## Abstract
arXiv:2405.10360v2 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: Despite their ever more widespread deployment throughout society, machine learning algorithms remain critically vulnerable to being spoofed by subtle adversarial tampering with their input data. The prospect of near-term quantum computers being capable of running {quantum machine learning} (QML) algorithms has therefore generated intense interest in their adversarial vulnerability. Here we show that quantum properties of QML algorithms can confer fundamental protections against such attacks, in certain scenarios guaranteeing robustness against classically-armed adversaries. We leverage tools from many-body physics to identify the quantum sources of this protection. Our results offer a theoretical underpinning of recent evidence which suggest quantum advantages in the search for adversarial robustness. In particular, we prove that quantum classifiers are: (i) protected against weak perturbations of data drawn from the trained distribution, (ii) protected against local attacks if they are insufficiently scrambling, and (iii) show evidence that they are protected against universal adversarial attacks if they are sufficiently chaotic. Our analytic results are supported by numerical evidence demonstrating the applicability of our theorems and the resulting robustness of a quantum classifier in practice. This line of inquiry constitutes a concrete pathway to advantage in QML, orthogonal to the usually sought improvements in model speed or accuracy.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2405.10360v2 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：尽管机器学习算法在整个社会中得到了越来越广泛的部署，但机器学习算法仍然非常容易受到对其输入数据的微妙对抗性篡改的欺骗。因此，近期量子计算机能够运行量子机器学习（QML）算法的前景引起了人们对其对抗脆弱性的强烈兴趣。在这里，我们展示了 QML 算法的量子特性可以提供针对此类攻击的基本保护，在某些情况下可以保证针对传统武装对手的鲁棒性。我们利用多体物理学的工具来识别这种保护的量子来源。我们的结果为最近的证据提供了理论基础，这些证据表明在寻找对抗鲁棒性方面具有量子优势。特别是，我们证明量子分类器：（i）免受从训练分布中提取的数据的弱扰动，（ii）如果它们不充分扰乱，则可以免受局部攻击，并且（iii）有证据表明，如果它们足够混乱，它们可以免受普遍的对抗性攻击。我们的分析结果得到了数值证据的支持，证明了我们的定理的适用性以及量子分类器在实践中的鲁棒性。这一探究路线构成了在 QML 中取得优势的具体途径，与通常寻求的模型速度或准确性的改进正交。
