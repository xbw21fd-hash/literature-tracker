## Abstract
Machine-learned interatomic potentials can offer near first-principles accuracy but are computationally expensive, limiting their application to large-scale molecular dynamics simulations. Inspired by quantum mechanics/molecular mechanics methods, we present ML-MIX, a CPU- and GPU-compatibleLAMMPSpackage to accelerate simulations by spatially mixing interatomic potentials of different complexities, allowing deployment of modern MLIPs even under restricted computational budgets. We demonstrate our method for ACE, UF3, SNAP and MACE potential architectures and demonstrate how linear ‘cheap’ potentials can be distilled from a given ‘expensive’ potential, allowing close matching in relevant regions of configuration space. The functionality of ML-MIX is demonstrated through tests on point defects in Si, Fe and W-He, in which speedups of up to 11× over ~8000 atoms are demonstrated, without sacrificing accuracy. The scientific potential of ML-MIX is demonstrated via two case studies in W, measuring the mobility of\(b=\frac{1}{2}\langle 111\rangle\)screw dislocations with ACE/ACE mixing and the implantation of He with MACE/SNAP mixing. The latter returns He reflection coefficients which (for the first time) match experimental observations up to an He incident energy of 80 eV—demonstrating the benefits of deploying state-of-the-art models on large, realistic systems.

## 摘要（中文）
机器学习的原子间势可以提供接近第一原理的精度，但计算成本高昂，限制了它们在大规模分子动力学模拟中的应用。受量子力学/分子力学方法的启发，我们推出了 ML-MIX，这是一种与 CPU 和 GPU 兼容的 LAMMPS 软件包，通过空间混合不同复杂度的原子间势来加速模拟，即使在计算预算有限的情况下也允许部署现代 MLIP。我们演示了针对 ACE、UF3、SNAP 和 MACE 势能架构的方法，并演示了如何从给定的“昂贵”势能中提取线性“廉价”势能，从而允许在配置空间的相关区域中进行紧密匹配。 ML-MIX 的功能通过对 Si、Fe 和 W-He 中的点缺陷进行测试得到了证明，其中超过约 8000 个原子的加速速度高达 11 倍，且不牺牲精度。 ML-MIX 的科学潜力通过 W 中的两个案例研究得到了证明，测量了 ACE/ACE 混合下的\(b=\frac{1}{2}\langle 111\rangle\)螺旋位错的迁移率以及 MACE/SNAP 混合下的 He 注入。后者返回的 He 反射系数（首次）与高达 80 eV 的 He 入射能量的实验观测结果相匹配，这证明了在大型现实系统上部署最先进模型的好处。
