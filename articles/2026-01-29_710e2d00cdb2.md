## Abstract
arXiv:2601.20450v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: We study the disequilibrium dynamics of a stylised model of production networks in which firms use perishable and non-substitutable intermediate inputs, so that adverse idiosyncratic productivity shocks can trigger downstream shortages and output losses. To protect against such disruptions, firms hold precautionary inventories that act as buffer stocks. We show that, for a given dispersion of firm-level productivity shocks, there exists a critical level of inventories above which the economy remains in a stable stochastic steady state. Below this critical level, the system becomes fragile, i.e., it becomes prone to system-wide crises. As this resilience-fragility boundary is approached from above, aggregate output volatility rises sharply and diverges, even though shocks are purely idiosyncratic. Because inventories are costly, competitive pressures induce firms to economize on buffers. Although we do not explicitly model such costs, we argue that the resulting behaviour of individual firms drives the system close to criticality, generating persistent excess macroeconomic volatility -- in other words, ``small shocks, large cycles'' -- in line with other settings where efficiency and resilience are in tension with each other. In the language of phase transitions, the resilient-to-fragile transition is continuous (supercritical): the economy exhibits a well-defined stochastic equilibrium with finite volatility on one side of the boundary, while beyond it the probability of a collapse in finite time tends to one. We characterize this transition primarily through numerical simulations and derive an analytical description in a high-perishability, high-connectivity limit.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.20450v1 公告类型：新 摘要：我们研究了生产网络程式化模型的不平衡动态，其中企业使用易腐烂且不可替代的中间投入，因此不利的特殊生产力冲击可能引发下游短缺和产出损失。为了防止此类干扰，公司持有预防性库存作为缓冲库存。我们表明，对于给定的企业层面生产率冲击分散度，存在一个临界库存水平，高于该水平经济将保持稳定的随机稳定状态。低于这个临界水平，系统就会变得脆弱，即容易发生系统范围的危机。当自上而下逼近弹性-脆弱性边界时，总产出波动性急剧上升并出现分化，尽管冲击纯粹是特殊的。由于库存成本高昂，竞争压力促使企业节省缓冲时间。尽管我们没有明确地对此类成本进行建模，但我们认为，个别企业的行为导致系统接近临界点，产生持续的过度宏观经济波动——换句话说，“小冲击，大周期”——这与效率和弹性相互紧张的其他环境是一致的。用相变的语言来说，弹性到脆弱的转变是连续的（超临界的）：经济表现出一种明确的随机平衡，边界一侧的波动性有限，而在边界之外，有限时间内崩溃的概率趋于1。我们主要通过数值模拟来描述这种转变，并在高易腐性、高连通性极限下得出分析描述。
