## Abstract
arXiv:2601.15080v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: We report on the experimental observation of stimulated cooling in the non-equilibrium Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) of weakly interacting exciton-polaritons from approximately room temperature down to 20K. By resolving the condensate in energy-momentum space and performing interferometric measurements, we distinguish the condensate from thermalized particles yet occupying excited states macroscopically. In contrast to the analytical quantum theories of non-equilibrium BEC [Shishkov et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 128, 065301 (2022)], we observe segmentation of the particle density along the excited states into two fractions both following Bose-Einstein distribution, albeit with different effective temperatures and chemical potentials. Our results indicate that the temperature of the weakly interacting Bose gas is universally set by the density-dependent chemical potential, revealing a defining property of non-equilibrium BECs. Finally, we demonstrate that the stimulated nature of the cooling process directly governs the emergence of quantum coherence of the condensate and shapes the dissipative properties of the excited states.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.15080v1 公告类型：新 摘要：我们报告了弱相互作用激子-极化子的非平衡玻色-爱因斯坦凝聚体 (BEC) 受激冷却从大约室温到 20K 的实验观察。通过解析能量动量空间中的凝聚态并进行干涉测量，我们将凝聚态与宏观上占据激发态的热化粒子区分开来。与非平衡 BEC 的分析量子理论相反 [Shishkov et al., Phys.莱特牧师。 128, 065301 (2022)]，我们观察到粒子密度沿着激发态分割成两个部分，尽管有效温度和化学势不同，但都遵循玻色-爱因斯坦分布。我们的结果表明，弱相互作用玻色气体的温度普遍由密度相关的化学势决定，揭示了非平衡 BEC 的一个决定性特性。最后，我们证明冷却过程的受激性质直接控制凝聚态量子相干性的出现并塑造激发态的耗散特性。
