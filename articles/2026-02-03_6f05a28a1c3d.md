## Abstract
arXiv:2504.07976v3 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Multiscale problems are ubiquitous in physics. Numerical simulations of such problems by solving partial differential equations (PDEs) at high resolution are computationally too expensive for many-query scenarios, such as uncertainty quantification, remeshing applications, and topology optimization. This limitation has motivated the development of data-driven surrogate models, where microscale computations are substituted by black-box mappings between macroscale quantities. While these approaches offer significant speedups, they typically struggle to incorporate microscale physical constraints, such as the balance of linear momentum. In this contribution, we propose the Equilibrium Neural Operator (EquiNO), a physics-informed PDE surrogate in which equilibrium is hard-enforced by construction. EquiNO achieves this by projecting the solution onto a set of divergence-free basis functions obtained via proper orthogonal decomposition (POD), thereby ensuring satisfaction of equilibrium without relying on penalty terms or multi-objective loss functions. We compare EquiNO with variational physics-informed neural and operator networks that enforce physical constraints only weakly through the loss function, as well as with purely data-driven operator-learning baselines. Our framework, applicable to multiscale FE$^{\,2}$ computations, introduces a finite element-operator learning (FE-OL) approach that integrates the finite element (FE) method with operator learning (OL). We apply the proposed methodology to quasi-static problems in solid mechanics and demonstrate that FE-OL yields accurate solutions even when trained on restricted datasets. The results show that EquiNO achieves speedup factors exceeding 8000-fold compared to traditional methods and offers a robust and physically consistent alternative to existing data-driven surrogate models.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2504.07976v3 公告类型：替换 摘要：多尺度问题在物理学中普遍存在。通过以高分辨率求解偏微分方程 (PDE) 来对此类问题进行数值模拟对于许多查询场景（例如不确定性量化、重新划分应用程序和拓扑优化）来说计算成本太高。这种限制促进了数据驱动替代模型的发展，其中微观尺度计算被宏观尺度量之间的黑盒映射所取代。虽然这些方法提供了显着的加速，但它们通常难以纳入微观物理约束，例如线性动量的平衡。在这篇文章中，我们提出了平衡神经算子（EquiNO），这是一种基于物理的偏微分方程替代项，其中平衡是通过构造来强制执行的。 EquiNO 通过将解投影到一组通过适当的正交分解 (POD) 获得的无散基函数上来实现这一点，从而确保满足平衡而不依赖于惩罚项或多目标损失函数。我们将 EquiNO 与变分物理通知的神经网络和算子网络进行比较，这些网络仅通过损失函数微弱地施加物理约束，以及纯粹的数据驱动的算子学习基线。我们的框架适用于多尺度 FE$^{\,2}$ 计算，引入了有限元算子学习（FE-OL）方法，该方法将有限元（FE）方法与算子学习（OL）相结合。我们将所提出的方法应用于固体力学中的准静态问题，并证明即使在受限数据集上进行训练，FE-OL 也能产生准确的解决方案。结果表明，与传统方法相比，EquiNO 的加速因子超过 8000 倍，并为现有的数据驱动替代模型提供了强大且物理一致的替代方案。
