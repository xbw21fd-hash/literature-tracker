## Abstract
Controlling quantum matter with light offers a promising route to dynamically tune its many-body properties, ranging from band topology1,2to superconductivity3. However, achieving such optical control for strongly correlated electron systems in the steady state has remained elusive. Here we demonstrate optical switching of the spin–valley degree of freedom of itinerant ferromagnets in twisted MoTe2(t-MoTe2) homobilayers. This system uniquely features flat valley-contrasting Chern bands and exhibits a range of strongly correlated phases at various moiré lattice fillings, including Chern insulators and ferromagnetic metals4,5,6,7. We show that the spin–valley orientation of all of these phases can be dynamically reversed by resonantly exciting the exciton–polaron8transitions with circularly polarized light. These findings not only provide direct evidence for non-thermal optical switching of a ferromagnetic spin state at zero magnetic field but also demonstrate the possibility of dynamical control over a topological order parameter, paving the way for optical generation of chiral edge modes and topological quantum circuits.

## 摘要（中文）
用光控制量子物质提供了一条动态调整其多体特性（从能带拓扑1,2到超导性3）的有前途的途径。然而，在稳态下实现强相关电子系统的这种光学控制仍然难以实现。在这里，我们演示了扭曲 MoTe2(t-MoTe2) 同双层中流动铁磁体自旋谷自由度的光学切换。该系统独特地具有平谷对比陈氏带，并在各种莫尔晶格填充处表现出一系列强相关相位，包括陈氏绝缘体和铁磁金属4,5,6,7。我们表明，通过用圆偏振光共振激发激子-极化子8跃迁，所有这些相的自旋谷方向都可以动态反转。这些发现不仅为零磁场下铁磁自旋态的非热光学开关提供了直接证据，而且证明了动态控制拓扑序参数的可能性，为手性边缘模式和拓扑量子电路的光学生成铺平了道路。
