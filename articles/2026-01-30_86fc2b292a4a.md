## Abstract
arXiv:2601.21175v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Understanding the three-dimensional motion of bubbles is essential for interpreting transport and mixing in multiphase flows, especially when bubbles deform under shear or move rapidly through the flow field. In many laboratory setups, only a single high-speed camera is available, which limits measurements to two dimensions. Traditional image-processing tools can identify bubbles only when they appear circular and isolated, but they struggle with irregularly shaped bubbles, shear-induced deformations, strong blurring, and partial overlaps. Multi-camera systems could overcome these issues, but require significant hardware additions and calibration effort. In this work, we introduce a new machine-learning framework that can detect bubbles and estimate their depth using only a single 20 kHz high-speed camera with 3 \textmu m resolution. The method first uses a large unlabeled dataset and clusters the bubbles with an unsupervised algorithm to reveal their underlying structure. These clusters provide pseudo labels, which are combined with a small set of true in-plane bubble labels to train a semi-supervised model that generalizes across different bubble appearances. These components produce a continuous depth-proxy score that indicates how close each bubble is to the imaging plane, even when bubbles are distorted or irregularly shaped. In parallel, we perform robust bubble identification using instance segmentation, which separates touching, overlapping, and elongated bubbles generated by high-velocity shear. Quantitatively, the in-plane segmentation baseline achieves strong held-out performance with Average Precision (AP) = 0.818, implying stable detection across thresholds, clutter, bubble detection Precision of 0.901, and a False-Positive Rate (FPR) near 6.1\%, hence low spurious bubbles and cleaner statistics under the tested acquisition conditions.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.21175v1 公告类型：新 摘要：了解气泡的三维运动对于解释多相流中的传输和混合至关重要，特别是当气泡在剪切作用下变形或在流场中快速移动时。在许多实验室设置中，只有一台高速相机可用，这将测量限制在二维范围内。传统的图像处理工具只能识别圆形且孤立的气泡，但它们很难识别形状不规则的气泡、剪切引起的变形、强烈模糊和部分重叠。多摄像头系统可以克服这些问题，但需要大量的硬件添加和校准工作。在这项工作中，我们引入了一种新的机器学习框架，该框架可以仅使用分辨率为 3 μm 的单个 20 kHz 高速相机来检测气泡并估计其深度。该方法首先使用大型未标记数据集，并使用无监督算法对气泡进行聚类，以揭示其底层结构。这些簇提供伪标签，与一小组真实的平面内气泡标签相结合，训练一个半监督模型，该模型可以泛化不同的气泡外观。这些组件会产生连续的深度代理分数，指示每个气泡与成像平面的接近程度，即使气泡扭曲或形状不规则也是如此。同时，我们使用实例分割来执行稳健的气泡识别，该实例分割分离由高速剪切产生的接触、重叠和拉长的气泡。从数量上讲，面内分割基线实现了强大的保留性能，平均精度 (AP) = 0.818，这意味着跨阈值、杂波、气泡检测精度为 0.901 的稳定检测，以及接近 6.1\% 的误报率 (FPR)，因此在测试的采集条件下，杂散气泡较低且统计数据更清晰。
