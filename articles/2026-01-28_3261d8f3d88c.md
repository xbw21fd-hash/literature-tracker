## Abstract
arXiv:2509.03743v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Dislocations are line defects in crystals that multiply and self-organize into a complex network during strain hardening. The length of dislocation links, connecting neighboring nodes within this network, contains crucial information about the evolving dislocation microstructure. By analyzing data from Discrete Dislocation Dynamics (DDD) simulations in face-centered cubic (fcc) Cu, we characterize the statistical distribution of link lengths of dislocation networks during strain hardening on individual slip systems. Our analysis reveals that link lengths on active slip systems follow a double-exponential distribution, while those on inactive slip systems conform to a single-exponential distribution. The distinctive long tail observed in the double-exponential distribution is attributed to the stress-induced bowing out of long links on active slip systems, a feature that disappears upon removal of the applied stress. We further demonstrate that both observed link length distributions can be explained by extending a one-dimensional Poisson process to include different growth functions. Specifically, the double-exponential distribution emerges when the growth rate for links exceeding a critical length becomes super-linear, which aligns with the physical phenomenon of long links bowing out under stress. This work advances our understanding of dislocation microstructure evolution during strain hardening and elucidates the underlying physical mechanisms governing its formation.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2509.03743v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：位错是晶体中的线缺陷，在应变硬化过程中会倍增并自组织成复杂的网络。连接该网络内相邻节点的位错链的长度包含有关不断演变的位错微观结构的重要信息。通过分析面心立方 (fcc) Cu 中的离散位错动力学 (DDD) 模拟数据，我们表征了各个滑移系统应变硬化期间位错网络链接长度的统计分布。我们的分析表明，主动滑移系统上的连杆长度遵循双指数分布，而非主动滑移系统上的连杆长度遵循单指数分布。在双指数分布中观察到的独特的长尾归因于主动滑动系统上长连杆的应力引起的弯曲，该特征在去除施加的应力后消失。我们进一步证明，观察到的链路长度分布可以通过扩展一维泊松过程以包括不同的增长函数来解释。具体来说，当超过临界长度的链接的增长率变得超线性时，就会出现双指数分布，这与长链接在压力下弯曲的物理现象一致。这项工作增进了我们对应变硬化过程中位错微观结构演化的理解，并阐明了控制其形成的潜在物理机制。
