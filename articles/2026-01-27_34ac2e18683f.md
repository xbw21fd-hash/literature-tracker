## Abstract
arXiv:2512.05790v3 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: We develop a theoretical framework that explains how gating mechanisms determine the learnability window $\mathcal{H}_N$ of recurrent neural networks, defined as the largest temporal horizon over which gradient information remains statistically recoverable. While classical analyses emphasize numerical stability of Jacobian products, we show that stability alone is insufficient: learnability is governed instead by the effective learning rates $\mu_{t,\ell}$, per-lag and per-neuron quantities obtained from first-order expansions of gate-induced Jacobian products in Backpropagation Through Time. These effective learning rates act as multiplicative filters that control both the magnitude and anisotropy of gradient transport. Under heavy-tailed ($\alpha$-stable) gradient noise, we prove that the minimal sample size required to detect a dependency at lag~$\ell$ scales as $N(\ell)\propto f(\ell)^{-\kappa_\alpha}$, where $f(\ell)=\|\mu_{t,\ell}\|_1$ is the effective learning rate envelope and $\kappa_\alpha=\alpha/(\alpha-1)$ is the concentration exponent governing empirical averages. This yields an explicit characterization of $\mathcal{H}_N$ and closed-form scaling laws for logarithmic, polynomial, and exponential decay of $f(\ell)$. The theory shows that the time-scale spectra induced by the effective learning rates are the dominant determinants of learnability: broader or more heterogeneous spectra slow the decay of $f(\ell)$, enlarging the learnability window, while heavy-tailed noise uniformly compresses $\mathcal{H}_N$ by slowing statistical concentration to $N^{-1/\kappa_{\alpha}}$. By integrating gate-induced time-scale geometry with gradient noise and sample complexity, the framework identifies effective learning rates as the primary objects that determine whether, when, and over what horizons recurrent networks can learn long-range temporal dependencies.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.05790v3 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：我们开发了一个理论框架，解释门控机制如何确定循环神经网络的可学习性窗口 $\mathcal{H}_N$，定义为梯度信息在统计上可恢复的最大时间范围。虽然经典分析强调雅可比积的数值稳定性，但我们表明仅稳定性是不够的：可学习性而是由有效学习率 $\mu_{t,\ell}$ 以及从随时间反向传播中门诱导雅可比积的一阶展开获得的每滞后和每神经元数量来控制。这些有效的学习率充当乘法滤波器，控制梯度传输的幅度和各向异性。在重尾（$\alpha$-稳定）梯度噪声下，我们证明检测 lag~$\ell$ 处的依赖性所需的最小样本大小为 $N(\ell)\propto f(\ell)^{-\kappa_\alpha}$，其中 $f(\ell)=\|\mu_{t,\ell}\|_1$ 是有效学习率包络，$\kappa_\alpha=\alpha/(\alpha-1)$是控制经验平均值的浓度指数。这产生了 $\mathcal{H}_N$ 的显式表征以及 $f(\ell)$ 的对数、多项式和指数衰减的封闭形式标度律。该理论表明，有效学习率引起的时间尺度谱是可学习性的主要决定因素：更宽或更异质的谱减缓了$f(\ell)$的衰减，扩大了可学习性窗口，而重尾噪声通过减慢统计浓度到$N^{-1/\kappa_{\alpha}}$来均匀压缩$\mathcal{H}_N$。通过将门引起的时间尺度几何与梯度噪声和样本复杂性相结合，该框架将有效学习率确定为主要目标，确定循环网络是否、何时以及在什么范围内可以学习长程时间依赖性。
