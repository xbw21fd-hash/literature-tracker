## Abstract
Author(s): Andrea Grimaldi, Davi R. Rodrigues, Eleonora Raimondo, Vito Puliafito, Vincenza Crupi, Vitoantonio Bevilacqua, Mario Carpentieri, Francesca Garesci, and Giovanni Finocchio The traveling-salesman problem has important applications in logistics and path optimization. For autonomous devices like drones, edge computing provides key advantages compared to cloud computing, but has strict energy-consumption and memory limitations. Ising machines would be suitable here, but they lack compatibility with generalized variants of route-optimization problems and struggle with constraint-based problems. The authors present strategies to overcome these limitations, impacting future engineering solutions that employ Ising machines for edge-computing applications. [Phys. Rev. Applied 25, 024023] Published Fri Feb 06, 2026
