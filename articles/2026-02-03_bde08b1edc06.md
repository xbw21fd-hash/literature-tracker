## Abstract
arXiv:2602.01358v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Mechanical and thermodynamic properties, including the influence of crystal defects, are critical for evaluating materials in engineering applications. Molecular dynamics simulations provide valuable insight into these mechanisms at the atomic scale. However, current practice often relies on fragmented scripts with inconsistent metadata and limited provenance, which hinders reproducibility, interoperability, and reuse. FAIR data principles and workflow-based approaches offer a path to address these limitations. We present reusable atomistic workflows that incorporate metadata annotation aligned with application ontologies, enabling automatic provenance capture and FAIR-compliant data outputs. The workflows cover key mechanical and thermodynamic quantities, including equation of state, elastic tensors, mechanical loading, thermal properties, defect formation energies, and nanoindentation. We demonstrate validation of structure-property relations such as the Hall-Petch effect and show that the workflows can be reused across different interatomic potentials and materials within a coherent semantic framework. The approach provides AI-ready simulation data, supports emerging agentic AI workflows, and establishes a generalizable blueprint for knowledge-based mechanical and thermodynamic simulations.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.01358v1 公告类型：新 摘要：机械和热力学性能（包括晶体缺陷的影响）对于评估工程应用中的材料至关重要。分子动力学模拟为原子尺度上的这些机制提供了有价值的见解。然而，当前的实践通常依赖于元数据不一致且出处有限的碎片脚本，这阻碍了可重复性、互操作性和重用性。 FAIR 数据原则和基于工作流程的方法提供了解决这些限制的途径。我们提出了可重用的原子工作流程，其中包含与应用程序本体一致的元数据注释，从而实现自动来源捕获和符合 FAIR 的数据输出。该工作流程涵盖关键的机械和热力学量，包括状态方程、弹性张量、机械载荷、热性能、缺陷形成能和纳米压痕。我们演示了结构-属性关系（例如 Hall-Petch 效应）的验证，并表明工作流程可以在连贯的语义框架内跨不同原子间势和材料重用。该方法提供人工智能就绪的模拟数据，支持新兴的代理人工智能工作流程，并为基于知识的机械和热力学模拟建立通用蓝图。
