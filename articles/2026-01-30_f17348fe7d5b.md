## Abstract
The fractional quantum anomalous Hall effect has recently been experimentally observed in fractional Chern insulators at zero magnetic field. However, an outstanding challenge is the presence of substantial longitudinal resistance, even though the anomalous Hall resistance is quantized. This dissipation is probably linked to imperfect sample quality. Here we demonstrate a twisted MoTe2bilayer device that exhibits quantized anomalous Hall resistance and vanishing longitudinal resistance for the fractional state, such that it is a dissipationless fractional Chern insulator. Unlike fractional quantum Hall states, where the energy gap increases with magnetic field, the thermal activation gap of the fractional state decreases rapidly with magnetic field and then plateaus above a few teslas. This behaviour reflects the coexistence of two distinct excitation channels: spinful quasiparticles dominate transport at low magnetic fields whereas spinless quasiparticles govern transport at high fields, where Zeeman splitting suppresses spin-flip processes. Our results provide insights into the energy scale of fractional Chern insulators and indicate a pathway to the quantum engineering of exotic correlated topological states.

## 摘要（中文）
最近在零磁场下的分数陈绝缘体中实验观察到了分数量子反常霍尔效应。然而，一个突出的挑战是存在大量的纵向电阻，即使异常霍尔电阻是量子化的。这种耗散可能与样品质量不完美有关。在这里，我们展示了一种扭曲的 MoTe2 双层器件，该器件表现出量子化的反常霍尔电阻和分数态的消失纵向电阻，因此它是一种无耗散的分数陈绝缘体。与能隙随磁场增加的分数量子霍尔态不同，分数态的热激活能隙随磁场迅速减小，然后稳定在几个特斯拉以上。这种行为反映了两种不同激发通道的共存：自旋准粒子主导低磁场下的传输，而无自旋准粒子控制高场下的传输，其中塞曼分裂抑制自旋翻转过程。我们的结果提供了对分数陈绝缘体能量尺度的见解，并指出了奇异相关拓扑态量子工程的途径。
