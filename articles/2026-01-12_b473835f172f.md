## Abstract
arXiv:2601.05284v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: In the brain, neural activity undergoes directed flows between states, thus breaking time-reversal symmetry. At the same time, animals also exhibit irreversible flows between behavioral states. Yet it remains unclear whether -- and how -- irreversibility in the brain relates to irreversibility in behavior. Here, we explore this connection in the hippocampus, where neural activity encodes physical location. We show that hippocampal irreversibility can be quantified using the time-delayed cross-correlations between neurons. As a mouse moves along a virtual track, we find that physical flows through the animal's environment generate neural flows through its cognitive map. Strikingly, this neural irreversibility is explained by a minimal model with only three parameters: the average velocity of the mouse, the variance in this velocity, and the resolution of the neural encoding. Together, these results provide a mechanistic understanding of irreversibility in the hippocampus and shed light on the links between symmetry breaking in the brain and behavior.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.05284v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：在大脑中，神经活动在状态之间进行定向流动，从而打破了时间反转对称性。同时，动物也表现出行为状态之间不可逆转的流动。然而，目前尚不清楚大脑中的不可逆性是否以及如何与行为的不可逆性相关。在这里，我们探索海马体中的这种联系，其中神经活动编码物理位置。我们证明，海马的不可逆性可以使用神经元之间的时滞互相关性来量化。当老鼠沿着虚拟轨道移动时，我们发现动物环境中的物理流会通过其认知图产生神经流。引人注目的是，这种神经不可逆性可以通过只有三个参数的最小模型来解释：鼠标的平均速度、该速度的方差以及神经编码的分辨率。总之，这些结果提供了对海马体不可逆性的机械理解，并揭示了大脑对称性破坏与行为之间的联系。
