## Abstract
arXiv:2602.03721v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: High-purity germanium (HPGe) crystals underpin some of the most sensitive detectors used in fundamental physics and other high-resolution radiation-sensing applications. Despite their importance, the supply of detector-grade HPGe remains limited because achieving high yield in Czochralski growth (CZ) depends on tightly coupled, nonlinear processes, impurity incorporation, thermal gradients, and dynamic control settings that are largely mastered by only a handful of companies with decades of experience. Here we present a data-driven prediction framework based on a Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (BiLSTM) neural network with multi-head attention, trained on time-resolved growth parameters (e.g., heater power, pull rate, and impurity indicators) from 48 independent crystal runs. The model predicts the final detector-grade fraction for each growth and, using SHAP feature-importance analysis, identifies impurity concentration and growth rate as the dominant factors governing yield, consistent with empirical understanding. By providing a quantitative, interpretable link between in-process signals and post-growth detector quality, this framework offers a practical path toward improving yield, reducing dependence on trial-and-error tuning, and scaling HPGe production for next-generation rare-event detectors.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.03721v1 公告类型：新 摘要：高纯度锗 (HPGe) 晶体支撑着基础物理和其他高分辨率辐射传感应用中使用的一些最灵敏的探测器。尽管它们很重要，但探测器级 HPGe 的供应仍然有限，因为直拉生长 (CZ) 的高产量取决于紧密耦合的非线性过程、杂质掺入、热梯度和动态控制设置，而这些设置在很大程度上只有少数拥有数十年经验的公司掌握。在这里，我们提出了一个数据驱动的预测框架，基于具有多头注意力的双向长短期记忆 (BiLSTM) 神经网络，并根据来自 48 个独立晶体运行的时间分辨生长参数（例如加热器功率、提拉速率和杂质指标）进行训练。该模型预测每次生长的最终检测器级分数，并使用 SHAP 特征重要性分析，将杂质浓度和生长速率识别为控制产量的主导因素，这与经验理解一致。通过在过程中信号和生长后探测器质量之间提供定量、可解释的联系，该框架为提高产量、减少对试错调整的依赖以及扩大下一代稀有事件探测器的 HPGe 生产提供了一条实用途径。
