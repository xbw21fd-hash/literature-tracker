# A physics-informed long-range polarizable potential based on deep learning

## Abstract
Machine-learning-based interatomic potentials are widely employed in atomistic simulations, but they struggle to capture long-range electrostatic correlations, which are ubiquitous in polar and biomolecular systems. We present a physics-informed machine-learning interatomic potential that incorporates long-range electrostatic interactions through a polarizable framework. Our model combines two equivariant message-passing neural networks: one for short-range interactions and the other for environment-dependent atomic dipoles. The model is trained not only on energies and forces but also on Born effective-charge tensors, enabling accurate predictions of field-induced properties such as infrared absorption spectra and LO–TO phonon splittings. We validate the method on ionic solids (NaCl), liquid water, and halide perovskites (MAPbI 3 ), demonstrating improved modeling of long-range polarization effects while maintaining competitive accuracy in energy and force predictions. Our results highlight the necessity of explicit long-range electrostatics for capturing collective phenomena in insulating and polar materials.

## 摘要（中文）
基于机器学习的原子间势被广泛应用于原子模拟中，但它们很难捕获在极地和生物分子系统中普遍存在的长程静电相关性。我们提出了一种基于物理的机器学习原子间势，它通过可极化框架结合了长程静电相互作用。我们的模型结合了两个等变的消息传递神经网络：一个用于短程相互作用，另一个用于依赖于环境的原子偶极子。该模型不仅接受能量和力的训练，还接受玻恩有效电荷张量的训练，从而能够准确预测场引起的特性，例如红外吸收光谱和 LO-TO 声子分裂。我们在离子固体 (NaCl)、液态水和卤化物钙钛矿 (MAPbI 3 ) 上验证了该方法，证明了改进的长程极化效应建模，同时保持了能量和力预测的竞争准确性。我们的结果强调了明确的长程静电对于捕获绝缘和极性材料中集体现象的必要性。
