## Abstract
Bromodomain PHD finger Transcription Factor (BPTF) is an epigenetic regulator implicated in cancer progression. However, despite its oncogenic significance, highly selective and potent inhibitors of the BPTF bromodomain (BRD) with suitable physicochemical properties are lacking. Previously, we reported BZ1, a submicromolar BPTF inhibitor which has off-target activities. Here, we applied comparative structural biology and molecular modeling to design BZ2, a regioisomer of BZ1 with enhanced selectivity for BPTF over other class I bromodomains and class-IV BRDs such as BRD7 and BRD9. Co-crystal structures and computational analyses reveal that differential engagement of water networks and polar interactions drives this selectivity. Functional studies demonstrate that genetic disruption of the BPTF BRD or treatment with BZ2 suppresses neuroblastoma cell growth. With high potency and improved physicochemical properties, BZ2 provides a valuable tool for probing the biology of BPTF and represents a promising starting point for advancing BPTF-targeted drug development.

Bromodomain PHD Finger 转录因子 (BPTF) 是一种与癌症进展有关的表观遗传调节因子。然而，尽管具有致癌意义，但具有合适理化性质的 BPTF 溴结构域 (BRD) 的高选择性和有效抑制剂仍然缺乏。此前，我们报道了 BZ1，一种具有脱靶活性的亚微摩尔 BPTF 抑制剂。在这里，我们应用比较结构生物学和分子建模来设计 BZ2，这是 BZ1 的一种区域异构体，与其他 I 类溴结构域和 IV 类 BRD（例如 BRD7 和 BRD9）相比，其对 BPTF 的选择性增强。共晶结构和计算分析表明，水网络的差异参与和极性相互作用驱动了这种选择性。功能研究表明，BPTF BRD 的基因破坏或 BZ2 治疗可抑制神经母细胞瘤细胞的生长。 BZ2 具有高效力和改进的理化性质，为探索 BPTF 生物学提供了有价值的工具，并为推进 BPTF 靶向药物开发提供了一个有希望的起点。
