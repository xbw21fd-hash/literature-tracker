## Abstract
arXiv:2601.12665v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Scientific attention is unevenly distributed, creating inequities in recognition and distorting access to opportunities. Using citations as a proxy, we quantify disparities in attention by gender and institutional prestige. We find that women receive systematically fewer citations than men, and that attention is increasingly concentrated among authors from elite institutions -- patterns not fully explained by underrepresentation alone. To explain these dynamics, we introduce a model of citation network growth that incorporates homophily (tendency to cite similar authors), preferential attachment (favoring highly cited authors) and group size (underrepresentation). The model shows that disparities arise not only from group size imbalances but also from cumulative advantage amplifying biased citation preferences. Importantly, increasing representation alone is often insufficient to reduce disparities. Effective strategies should also include reducing homophily, amplifying the visibility of underrepresented groups, and supporting equitable integration of newcomers. Our findings highlight the challenges of mitigating inequities in asymmetric networks like citations, where recognition flows in one direction. By making visible the mechanisms through which attention is distributed, we contribute to efforts toward a more responsible web of science that is fairer, more transparent, and more inclusive, and that better sustains innovation and knowledge production.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.12665v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：科学注意力分布不均，造成认可上的不平等并扭曲了获得机会的机会。使用引用作为代理，我们量化了性别和机构声望的关注差异。我们发现，女性获得的引用普遍少于男性，而且注意力越来越集中在来自精英机构的作者身上——这种模式并不能仅用代表性不足来完全解释。为了解释这些动态，我们引入了一个引用网络增长模型，该模型结合了同质性（引用相似作者的倾向）、优先依恋（偏爱高被引作者）和群体规模（代表性不足）。该模型表明，差异不仅源于群体规模的不平衡，还源于累积优势放大了有偏见的引用偏好。重要的是，仅增加代表性往往不足以减少差距。有效的策略还应包括减少同质性、扩大代表性不足群体的知名度以及支持新移民的公平融入。我们的研究结果强调了减轻非对称网络（如引文）中的不平等所面临的挑战，在非对称网络中，认可流向一个方向。通过公开分配注意力的机制，我们致力于建立一个更负责任的科学网络，该网络更公平、更透明、更具包容性，并能更好地维持创新和知识生产。
