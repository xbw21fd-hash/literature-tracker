## Abstract
arXiv:2601.17727v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Understanding skyrmion-skyrmion interactions is crucial for effectively manipulating the motion of multiple skyrmions in racetrack and logic devices. However, the fundamental nature and microscopic origins of these interactions remain poorly understood. In this study, we investigate skyrmion-skyrmion interactions in chiral magnetic films and reveal that they possess intrinsic, anisotropic, and oscillatory characteristics. Specifically, we demonstrate that the attractive and repulsive forces between skyrmions oscillate with a well-defined period, akin to the Ruderman-Kittel-Kasuya-Yosida (RKKY) coupling observed between two magnetic moments in metals. Our analysis uncovers the essential physics behind a previously unrecognized universal wavy tail in the skyrmion spin texture. Notably, the resulting RKKY-like interaction between skyrmions is universal for all tilted skyrmions, irrespective of whether the titled easy-axis is from an external field or a crystalline magnetic anisotropy. These findings introduce a novel physical principle for the design of skyrmion molecules or skyrmion superstructures, which hold significant potential for applications in skyrmion-based spintronics and neuromorphic computing.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.17727v1 公告类型：新 摘要：理解斯格明子-斯格明子相互作用对于有效操纵跑道和逻辑设备中多个斯格明子的运动至关重要。然而，这些相互作用的基本性质和微观起源仍然知之甚少。在这项研究中，我们研究了手性磁性薄膜中的斯格明子-斯格明子相互作用，并揭示了它们具有固有的、各向异性和振荡特性。具体来说，我们证明了斯格明子之间的吸引力和排斥力以明确的周期振荡，类似于金属中两个磁矩之间观察到的 Ruderman-Kittel-Kasuya-Yosida (RKKY) 耦合。我们的分析揭示了斯格明子自旋纹理中先前未被识别的普遍波尾背后的基本物理原理。值得注意的是，斯格明子之间产生的类似 RKKY 的相互作用对于所有倾斜的斯格明子都是通用的，无论标题中的易磁化轴是来自外场还是晶体磁各向异性。这些发现为斯格明子分子或斯格明子超结构的设计引入了一种新颖的物理原理，在基于斯格明子的自旋电子学和神经形态计算中具有巨大的应用潜力。
