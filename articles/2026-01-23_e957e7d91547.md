## Abstract
arXiv:2601.15662v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Chemically fueled supramolecular systems can exhibit complex, time-dependent behaviors reminiscent of living matter when maintained far from equilibrium by continuous energy or fuel consumption. Here, we introduce a minimal reaction-diffusion model that captures the essential dynamics of a cooperative supramolecular polymerization network driven by monomer activation and deactivation. We show that a balance between autocatalytic growth and inhibitory decay sustains a nonequilibrium steady state in the model that undergoes a Hopf bifurcation, giving rise to autonomous oscillations. When spatial transport is introduced through diffusion, the system displays rich spatiotemporal phenomena, such as traveling wavefronts and transient polygonal patterns. Our results demonstrate that the interplay between reaction kinetics and diffusion can spontaneously generate self-organized, life-like dynamics in synthetic supramolecular polymer systems. This theoretical framework not only bridges molecular self-assembly and active matter dynamics but also provides design principles for creating adaptive, oscillatory, and self-patterning materials powered by chemical fuels.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.15662v1 公告类型：新 摘要：化学燃料超分子系统在通过持续的能量或燃料消耗而远离平衡时，可以表现出复杂的、依赖时间的行为，让人想起生命物质。在这里，我们引入了一个最小反应扩散模型，该模型捕获了由单体激活和失活驱动的协作超分子聚合网络的基本动力学。我们表明，自催化生长和抑制衰变之间的平衡在经历 Hopf 分岔的模型中维持非平衡稳态，从而引起自主振荡。当通过扩散引入空间传输时，系统显示出丰富的时空现象，例如行进波前和瞬态多边形图案。我们的结果表明，反应动力学和扩散之间的相互作用可以在合成超分子聚合物系统中自发地产生自组织、逼真的动力学。该理论框架不仅连接了分子自组装和活性物质动力学，而且还提供了创建由化学燃料驱动的自适应、振荡和自图案化材料的设计原理。
