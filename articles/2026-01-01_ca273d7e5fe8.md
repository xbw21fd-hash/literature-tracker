## Abstract
arXiv:2512.23930v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: The accurate estimation of free energy differences between two states is a long-standing challenge in molecular simulations. Traditional approaches generally rely on sampling multiple intermediate states to ensure sufficient overlap in phase space and are, consequently, computationally expensive. Several generative-model-based methods have recently addressed this challenge by learning a direct bridge between distributions, bypassing the need for intermediate states. However, it remains unclear which approaches provide the best trade-off between efficiency, accuracy, and scalability. In this work, we systematically review these methods and benchmark selected approaches with a focus on condensed-matter systems. In particular, we investigate the performance of discrete and continuous normalizing flows in the context of targeted free energy perturbation as well as FEAT (Free energy Estimators with Adaptive Transport) together with the escorted Jarzynski equality, using coarse-grained monatomic ice and Lennard-Jones solids as benchmark systems. We evaluate accuracy, data efficiency, computational cost, and scalability with system size. Our results provide a quantitative framework for selecting effective free energy estimation strategies in condensed-phase systems.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.23930v1 公告类型：新 摘要：准确估计两种状态之间的自由能差异是分子模拟中长期存在的挑战。传统方法通常依赖于对多个中间状态进行采样来确保相空间中有足够的重叠，因此计算成本很高。最近，几种基于生成模型的方法通过学习分布之间的直接桥梁来解决这一挑战，绕过中间状态的需要。然而，目前尚不清楚哪种方法可以在效率、准确性和可扩展性之间提供最佳权衡。在这项工作中，我们系统地回顾了这些方法并对选定的方法进行了基准测试，重点关注凝聚态物质系统。特别是，我们使用粗粒单原子冰和 Lennard-Jones 固体作为基准系统，研究了目标自由能扰动以及 FEAT（具有自适应传输的自由能估计器）以及 Jarzynski 等式的背景下离散和连续归一化流的性能。我们根据系统规模评估准确性、数据效率、计算成本和可扩展性。我们的结果为在凝聚相系统中选择有效的自由能估计策略提供了一个定量框架。
