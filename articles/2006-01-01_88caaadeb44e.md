## Abstract
The study of multiferroic and magnetoelectric materials is undergoing something of a revival. Multiferroic materials combine the stable and switchable electrical polarization of ferroelectric crystals with stable, switchable magnetization arising through the quantum mechanical phenomenon of exchange in ferromagnetic crystals. Few truly 'multiferroic' materials exist, but the 'magnetoelectric' coupling of magnetic and electrical properties is more widespread. With the prospect of technological applications as the spur, some remarkable new compounds of this type are being developed.
