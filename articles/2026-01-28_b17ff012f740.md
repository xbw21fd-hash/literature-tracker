## Abstract
Developing a unified algorithm that can learn from and generate across modalities such as text, images and video has been a fundamental challenge in artificial intelligence. Although next-token prediction has driven major advances in large language models1, its extension to multimodal domains has remained limited, and diffusion models for image and video synthesis2,3and compositional frameworks that integrate vision encoders with language models4still dominate. Here we introduce Emu3, a family of multimodal models trained solely with next-token prediction. Emu3 equals the performance of well-established task-specific models across both perception and generation, matching flagship systems while removing the need for diffusion or compositional architectures. It further demonstrates coherent, high-fidelity video generation, interleaved vision–language generation and vision–language–action modelling for robotic manipulation. By reducing multimodal learning to unified token prediction, Emu3 establishes a robust foundation for large-scale multimodal modelling and offers a promising route towards unified multimodal intelligence.

## 摘要（中文）
开发一种可以跨文本、图像和视频等模式学习和生成的统一算法一直是人工智能领域的一项基本挑战。尽管下一个标记预测推动了大型语言模型1的重大进步，但其对多模态领域的扩展仍然有限，图像和视频合成的扩散模型2、3以及将视觉编码器与语言模型集成的组合框架4仍然占主导地位。在这里，我们介绍 Emu3，这是一系列仅通过下一个令牌预测进行训练的多模态模型。 Emu3 在感知和生成方面与成熟的特定任务模型的性能相当，匹配旗舰系统，同时消除了对扩散或组合架构的需求。它进一步展示了用于机器人操作的连贯、高保真视频生成、交错视觉-语言生成和视觉-语言-动作建模。通过将多模态学习简化为统一的标记预测，Emu3 为大规模多模态建模奠定了坚实的基础，并为统一多模态智能提供了一条有前途的途径。
