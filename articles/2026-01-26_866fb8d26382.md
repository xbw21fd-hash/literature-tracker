## Abstract
arXiv:2512.13448v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: $\Lambda$-doubling of diatomic molecules is a subtle microscopic phenomenon that has long attracted the attention of experimental groups, insofar as rotation of molecular $\textit{nuclei}$ induces small energetic changes in the (degenerate) $\textit{electronic}$ state. A direct description of such a phenomenon clearly requires going beyond the Born-Oppenheimer approximation. Here we show that a phase space theory previously developed to capture electronic momentum and model vibrational circular dichroism -- and which we have postulated should also describe the Einstein-de Haas effect, a macroscopic manifestation of angular momentum conservation -- is also able to recover the $\Lambda$-doubling energy splitting (or $\Lambda$-splitting) of the NO molecule nearly quantitatively. The key observation is that, by parameterizing the electronic Hamiltonian in terms of both nuclear position ($\mathbf{X}$) and nuclear momentum ($\mathbf{P}$), a phase space method yields potential energy surfaces that explicitly include the electron-rotation coupling and correctly conserve angular momentum (which we show is essential to capture $\Lambda-$doubling). The data presented in this manuscript offers another small glimpse into the rich physics that one can learn from investigating phase space potential energy surfaces $E_{PS}(\mathbf{X},\mathbf{P})$ as a function of both nuclear position and momentum, all at a computational cost comparable to standard Born-Oppenheimer electronic structure calculations.

arXiv:2512.13448v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：$\Lambda$-双原子分子的倍增是一种微妙的微观现象，长期以来一直吸引着实验组的关注，因为分子 $\textit{nuclei}$ 的旋转会引起（简并）$\textit{电子}$ 状态的微小能量变化。对这种现象的直接描述显然需要超越玻恩-奥本海默近似。在这里，我们表明，先前开发的用于捕获电子动量和模拟振动圆二色性的相空间理论（我们假设该理论也应该描述爱因斯坦-德哈斯效应，角动量守恒的宏观表现）也能够近乎定量地恢复 NO 分子的 $\Lambda$ 倍能分裂（或 $\Lambda$ 分裂）。关键的观察结果是，通过根据核位置 ($\mathbf{X}$) 和核动量 ($\mathbf{P}$) 参数化电子哈密顿量，相空间方法产生明确包含电子旋转耦合并正确守恒角动量的势能面（我们证明这对于捕获 $\Lambda-$ 倍增至关重要）。本手稿中提供的数据提供了对丰富物理的另一个小小的了解，人们可以通过研究相空间势能表面 $E_{PS}(\mathbf{X},\mathbf{P})$ 作为核位置和动量的函数来学习，所有这些计算成本都与标准玻恩-奥本海默电子结构计算相当。
