## Abstract
arXiv:2601.16634v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: We present a comprehensive first-principles study on the origin of the piezomagnetic effect in the non-collinear antiferromagnet Mn$_3$Sn, a material known for exhibiting a large anomalous Hall effect. We investigate strain-induced variations of electronic and magnetic states and elucidate the mechanism of the piezomagnetic effect from both real-space and momentum-space perspectives. In real space, the emergence of piezomagnetism is understood to arise from rotations of the magnetic moments at specific Mn sites, which directly couple to the strain. Through detailed electronic structure analysis, we identify the Fermi surfaces that play a crucial role in the emergence of piezomagnetism. Our results reveal that specific Fermi surface features undergo pseudo-degeneracy lifting under applied strain, which significantly contributes to the induced net magnetization. By combining these complementary real-space and momentum-space pictures, our dual-space analysis provides deep insight into the microscopic origins of strain-driven magnetization in Mn$_3$Sn.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.16634v1 公告类型：新 摘要：我们对非共线反铁磁体 Mn$_3$Sn 中压磁效应的起源进行了全面的第一性原理研究，Mn$_3$Sn 是一种以表现出巨大的反常霍尔效应而闻名的材料。我们研究了应变引起的电子和磁态的变化，并从真实空间和动量空间的角度阐明了压磁效应的机制。在真实空间中，压磁性的出现被认为是由特定 Mn 位点磁矩的旋转引起的，该旋转直接与应变耦合。通过详细的电子结构分析，我们确定了在压磁性的出现中发挥关键作用的费米面。我们的结果表明，特定的费米表面特征在施加应变下经历伪简并提升，这对感应净磁化强度有显着贡献。通过结合这些互补的实空间和动量空间图片，我们的双空间分析可以深入了解 Mn$_3$Sn 中应变驱动磁化的微观起源。
