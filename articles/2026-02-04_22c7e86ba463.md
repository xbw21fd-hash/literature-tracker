## Abstract
arXiv:2602.03005v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Optical tweezers which utilize structured light fields enable the rotation of trapped nanoparticles through the transfer of orbital angular momentum (OAM) from holographically generated Laguerre-Gaussian (LG) modes. In this research we use OAM transfer to demonstrate controlled rotation of bright fluorescent nanodiamond clusters assembled in a focused higher-order LG beam. We find that the assemblies can be effectively rotated in a two-dimensional optical trap with orbital frequencies of up to 5 Hz. We use video tracking to explore the Brownian dynamics of such a trapping arrangement and look at the impact of orientation stability on measurements of optically detected magnetic resonance (ODMR) with an applied weak external magnetic field. By collecting ODMR spectra at multiple points along the orbit, we show that the constrained two-dimensional motion can provide additional insights for vector magnetic field reconstruction.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.03005v1 公告类型：新 摘要：利用结构光场的光镊能够通过全息生成的拉盖尔高斯 (LG) 模式的轨道角动量 (OAM) 转移来旋转被捕获的纳米颗粒。在这项研究中，我们使用 OAM 转移来演示在聚焦的高阶 LG 光束中组装的明亮荧光纳米金刚石簇的受控旋转。我们发现这些组件可以在轨道频率高达 5 Hz 的二维光阱中有效旋转。我们使用视频跟踪来探索这种捕获装置的布朗动力学，并研究方向稳定性对施加弱外部磁场的光学检测磁共振（ODMR）测量的影响。通过收集沿轨道多个点的 ODMR 谱，我们表明受限二维运动可以为矢量磁场重建提供额外的见解。
