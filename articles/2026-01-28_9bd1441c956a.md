## Abstract
arXiv:2509.21778v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Crystal property prediction, governed by quantum mechanical principles, is computationally prohibitive to solve exactly for large many-body systems using traditional density functional theory. While machine learning models have emerged as efficient approximations for large-scale applications, their performance is strongly influenced by the choice of atomic representation. Although modern graph-based approaches have progressively incorporated more structural information, they often fail to capture long-range atomic interactions due to finite receptive fields and local encoding schemes. This limitation leads to distinct crystals being mapped to identical representations, hindering accurate property prediction. To address this, we introduce PRDNet that leverages unique reciprocal-space diffraction besides graph representations. To enhance sensitivity to elemental and environmental variations, we employ a data-driven pseudo-particle to generate a synthetic diffraction pattern. PRDNet ensures full invariance to crystallographic symmetries. Extensive experiments are conducted on Materials Project, JARVIS-DFT, and MatBench, demonstrating that the proposed model achieves state-of-the-art performance. The code is openly available at https://github.com/Bin-Cao/PRDNet.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2509.21778v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：由量子力学原理控制的晶体特性预测在计算上难以使用传统的密度泛函理论精确求解大型多体系统。虽然机器学习模型已经成为大规模应用的有效近似，但它们的性能很大程度上受到原子表示的选择的影响。尽管现代基于图的方法已经逐渐融入了更多的结构信息，但由于有限的感受野和局部编码方案，它们通常无法捕获远程原子相互作用。这种限制导致不同的晶体被映射到相同的表示，从而阻碍了准确的属性预测。为了解决这个问题，我们引入了 PRDNet，除了图形表示之外，它还利用了独特的倒易空间衍射。为了增强对元素和环境变化的敏感性，我们采用数据驱动的伪粒子来生成合成衍射图案。 PRDNet 确保晶体对称性的完全不变性。在 Materials Project、JARVIS-DFT 和 MatBench 上进行了大量实验，证明所提出的模型实现了最先进的性能。该代码可在 https://github.com/Bin-Cao/PRDNet 上公开获取。
