# Higher-order adaptive behaviors outperform pairwise strategies in mitigating contagion dynamics

## Abstract
arXiv:2602.05915v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: When exposed to a contagion phenomenon, individuals may respond to the perceived risk of infection by adopting behavioral changes, aiming to reduce their exposure or their risk of infecting others. The social cost of such adaptive behaviors and their impact on the contagion dynamics have been investigated in pairwise networks, with binary interactions driving both contagion and risk perception. However, contagion and adaptive mechanisms can also be driven by group (higher-order) interactions. Here, we consider several adaptive behaviors triggered by awareness of risk perceived through higher-order and pairwise interactions, and we compare their impact on pairwise and higher-order contagion processes. By numerical simulations and a mean-field analytic approach, we show that adaptive behaviors driven by higher-order information are more effective in limiting the spread of a contagion, than similar mechanisms based on pairwise information. Meanwhile, they also entail a lower social cost, measured as the reduction of the intensity of interactions in the population. Indeed, adaptive mechanisms based on higher-order information lead to a heterogeneous risk perception within the population, producing a higher alert on nodes with large hyperdegree (i.e., participating in many groups), on their neighborhoods, and on large groups. This in turn prevents the spreading process to exploit the properties of these nodes and groups, which tend to drive and sustain the dynamics in the absence of adaptive behaviors.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.05915v1 公告类型：新 摘要：当接触到传染现象时，个人可能会通过行为改变来应对感知到的感染风险，旨在减少接触或感染他人的风险。这种适应性行为的社会成本及其对传染动态的影响已经在成对网络中进行了研究，二元相互作用驱动传染和风险感知。然而，传染和适应机制也可以由群体（高阶）相互作用驱动。在这里，我们考虑通过高阶和成对交互感知的风险意识所触发的几种适应性行为，并比较它们对成对和高阶传染过程的影响。通过数值模拟和平均场分析方法，我们表明，与基于成对信息的类似机制相比，由高阶信息驱动的自适应行为在限制传染病的传播方面更有效。同时，它们还带来较低的社会成本，以人口互动强度的减少来衡量。事实上，基于高阶信息的自适应机制会导致群体内的异质风险感知，对具有大超度（即参与许多群体）的节点、其邻居和大群体产生更高的警报。这反过来又阻止了传播过程利用这些节点和组的属性，这些属性往往会在缺乏适应性行为的情况下驱动和维持动态。
