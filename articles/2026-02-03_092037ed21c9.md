## Abstract
arXiv:2511.04067v2 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: The Moon has been long regarded as a natural resonator of gravitational waves (GWs) since 1960, showing great potential to fill the frequency gap left behind GW detections by ground- or space-based laser interferometry. However, the spatial variation of this amplification capacity on the Moon remains unclear. Here, we numerically simulate the lunar response to GWs by fully considering the fluctuant topography and laterally heterogeneous interior structures. Our results show that most regions on the Moon can amplify GWs with a ratio over 2, a finding significantly higher than previous estimations. Particularly, the amplification ratio can even reach factors of tens at the resonant frequency of ~0.015 Hz on the highlands surrounding the South Pole-Aitken (SPA) basin, where the regional crust is the thickest. Our findings establish the thick-crust regions as critical zones of GW amplification, which is essential for future landing site selection and instrumental setting for GW detection on the Moon.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2511.04067v2 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：自 1960 年以来，月球一直被认为是引力波 (GW) 的天然谐振器，显示出填补地基或空基激光干涉测量引力波探测留下的频率间隙的巨大潜力。然而，月球上这种放大能力的空间变化仍不清楚。在这里，我们充分考虑起伏的地形和横向不均匀的内部结构，对月球对引力波的响应进行了数值模拟。我们的结果表明，月球上的大多数区域都能以超过 2 的比率放大引力波，这一发现明显高于之前的估计。特别是在南极-艾特肯（SPA）盆地周围的高地上，该地区地壳最厚，在~0.015 Hz的共振频率下，放大率甚至可以达到数十倍。我们的研究结果将厚地壳区域确定为引力波放大的关键区域，这对于未来着陆点的选择和月球引力波探测的仪器设置至关重要。
