## Abstract
arXiv:2602.00169v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: The convergence of artificial intelligence and materials science presents a transformative opportunity, but achieving true acceleration in discovery requires moving beyond task-isolated, fine-tuned models toward agentic systems that plan, act, and learn across the full discovery loop. This survey advances a unique pipeline-centric view that spans from corpus curation and pretraining, through domain adaptation and instruction tuning, to goal-conditioned agents interfacing with simulation and experimental platforms. Unlike prior reviews, we treat the entire process as an end-to-end system to be optimized for tangible discovery outcomes rather than proxy benchmarks. This perspective allows us to trace how upstream design choices-such as data curation and training objectives-can be aligned with downstream experimental success through effective credit assignment. To bridge communities and establish a shared frame of reference, we first present an integrated lens that aligns terminology, evaluation, and workflow stages across AI and materials science. We then analyze the field through two focused lenses: From the AI perspective, the survey details LLM strengths in pattern recognition, predictive analytics, and natural language processing for literature mining, materials characterization, and property prediction; from the materials science perspective, it highlights applications in materials design, process optimization, and the acceleration of computational workflows via integration with external tools (e.g., DFT, robotic labs). Finally, we contrast passive, reactive approaches with agentic design, cataloging current contributions while motivating systems that pursue long-horizon goals with autonomy, memory, and tool use. This survey charts a practical roadmap towards autonomous, safety-aware LLM agents aimed at discovering novel and useful materials.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.00169v1 公告类型：新 摘要：人工智能和材料科学的融合带来了变革机遇，但要实现真正的加速发现，需要超越任务隔离、微调的模型，转向在整个发现循环中规划、行动和学习的代理系统。这项调查提出了一种以管道为中心的独特观点，涵盖从语料库管理和预训练，到领域适应和指令调整，再到与模拟和实验平台连接的目标条件代理。与之前的审查不同，我们将整个过程视为一个端到端系统，以针对切实的发现结果而不是代理基准进行优化。这种视角使我们能够追踪上游设计选择（例如数据管理和培训目标）如何通过有效的学分分配与下游实验成功保持一致。为了弥合社区并建立共享的参考框架，我们首先提出一个集成的镜头，该镜头可以协调人工智能和材料科学的术语、评估和工作流程阶段。然后，我们通过两个重点镜头来分析该领域：从人工智能的角度来看，调查详细介绍了法学硕士在模式识别、预测分析和用于文献挖掘、材料表征和属性预测的自然语言处理方面的优势；从材料科学的角度来看，它重点介绍了在材料设计、工艺优化以及通过与外部工具（例如 DFT、机器人实验室）集成来加速计算工作流程中的应用。最后，我们将被动、反应性方法与代理设计进行对比，对当前的贡献进行分类，同时激励通过自主性、记忆和工具使用来追求长期目标的系统。这项调查为自主、具有安全意识的法学硕士代理人绘制了一个实用路线图，旨在发现新颖且有用的材料。
