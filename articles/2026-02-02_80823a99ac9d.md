## Abstract
Rate performance of several applications, such as batteries, fuel cells, and electrochemical sensors, is exponentially dependent on the ionic migration barrier (Em) within solids, a difficult-to-estimate quantity. Previous approaches to identify materials with lowEmhave often relied on imprecise descriptors or rules-of-thumb. Here, we present a graph-neural-network-based architecture that leverages principles of transfer learning to efficiently and accurately predictEmacross a variety of materials. We use a model (labeled MPT) that has been simultaneously pre-trained on seven bulk properties, introduce architectural modifications to build inductive bias on different migration pathways in a structure, and subsequently fine-tune (FT) on a manually-curated, literature-derived, first-principles computational dataset of 619Emvalues. Importantly, our best-performing FT model (labeled MODEL-3, based on test set scores) demonstrates substantially better accuracy compared to classical machine learning methods, graph models trained from scratch, and a universal machine learned interatomic potential, with aR2score and a mean absolute error of 0.703 ± 0.109 and 0.261 ± 0.034 eV, respectively, on the test set and is able to classify ‘good’ ionic conductors with an 80% accuracy. Thus, our work demonstrates the effective use of FT strategies and MPT architectural modifications to predictEm, and can be extended to make predictions on other data-scarce material properties.

## 摘要（中文）
电池、燃料电池和电化学传感器等多种应用的倍率性能与固体内的离子迁移势垒 (Em) 成指数关系，而这是一个难以估计的量。以前识别低电磁值材料的方法通常依赖于不精确的描述符或经验法则。在这里，我们提出了一种基于图神经网络的架构，它利用迁移学习的原理来有效、准确地预测各种材料的 Ema。我们使用一个模型（标记为 MPT），该模型已同时针对七个批量属性进行了预训练，引入架构修改以在结构中的不同迁移路径上建立归纳偏差，然后对手动策划的、源自文献的 619Em 值的第一原理计算数据集进行微调（FT）。重要的是，与经典机器学习方法、从头开始训练的图模型和通用机器学习原子间势相比，我们表现最好的 FT 模型（标记为 MODEL-3，基于测试集分数）表现出明显更好的准确性，在测试集上的 aR2score 和平均绝对误差分别为 0.703 ± 0.109 和 0.261 ± 0.034 eV，并且能够使用80% 的准确率。因此，我们的工作展示了 FT 策略和 MPT 架构修改的有效使用来预测 Em，并且可以扩展到对其他数据稀缺材料属性进行预测。
