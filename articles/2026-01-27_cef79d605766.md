# Geometry-Induced Skin Effect in Electron Hydrodynamics

## Abstract
arXiv:2601.17229v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: In ultra-clean 2d materials electron viscosity is as important as Ohmic dissipation and electron transport exhibits hydrodynamic features. Using a simple framework of Brinkman equations we find that hydrodynamic electron flows exhibit a geometric skin effect: sharp obstacles locally enhance the current suppressing it far from the edges where the flow is unobstructed. This effect arises within hydrodynamic transport with finite momentum relaxation and does not rely on ballistic dynamics. Our results provide a natural hydrodynamic interpretation of edge-enhanced and double-bump current profiles observed in constricted geometries. By comparing with recent scanning NV magnetometry experiments on gated graphene, we demonstrate that such flow patterns are consistent with viscous hydrodynamics shaped by geometry, clarifying the role of geometric effects in the interpretation of electronic flow experiments.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.17229v1 公告类型：新 摘要：在超净二维材料中，电子粘度与欧姆耗散一样重要，并且电子传输表现出流体动力学特征。使用布林克曼方程的简单框架，我们发现流体动力电子流表现出几何集肤效应：尖锐的障碍物局部增强电流，将电流抑制在远离流动无障碍的边缘处。这种效应出现在具有有限动量弛豫的流体动力传输中，并且不依赖于弹道动力学。我们的结果为在收缩几何形状中观察到的边缘增强和双凸点电流剖面提供了自然的流体动力学解释。通过与最近对门控石墨烯进行的扫描 NV 磁力测量实验进行比较，我们证明这种流动模式与几何形状形成的粘性流体动力学一致，阐明了几何效应在电子流实验解释中的作用。
