## Abstract
The low-energy electron (LEE) attachment to biological molecules can result in irreversible damage. The attachment of LEEs to DNA, amino acids, peptides, and proteins has a significant impact due to their diverse biological applications. This study focuses on the effect of the electron attachment to the conformers of alanyl glycine and glycyl alanine dipeptide molecules. This study used a quantum mechanical approach to extract the low-lying conformers of the dipeptide molecules and performed atom-centered density matrix propagation simulation to assess the impact of the electron attachment at 2.0 eV. We observed a dipole-bound state to a π * valence-bound state transition based on the singly occupied molecular orbitals’ analysis. It was observed that different conformers have distinct impacts on electron attachment to the system, and strong intramolecular H-bonds give extra stability to the system toward the dissociative electron attachment (DEA). The latter part of this work focuses on identifying two low-lying π * resonance states, as the DEA process involves a π * to σ * transition, which was studied using the complex absorbing potential approach. Our results align reasonably well with experimental observations for the alanyl glycine and glycyl alanine molecules in the gas phase.

## 摘要（中文）
低能电子 (LEE) 附着在生物分子上可能会导致不可逆的损伤。 LEE 与 DNA、氨基酸、肽和蛋白质的附着因其多样化的生物学应用而具有重大影响。本研究重点关注电子附着对丙氨酰甘氨酸和甘氨酰丙氨酸二肽分子构象异构体的影响。本研究使用量子力学方法提取二肽分子的低位构象异构体，并进行以原子为中心的密度矩阵传播模拟，以评估电子附着在 2.0 eV 下的影响。基于单占据分子轨道的分析，我们观察到偶极束缚态到 π * 价键束缚态的转变。据观察，不同的构象异构体对系统的电子附着有不同的影响，并且强的分子内氢键为系统的离解电子附着（DEA）提供了额外的稳定性。这项工作的后一部分重点是识别两个低位 π * 共振态，因为 DEA 过程涉及 π * 到 σ * 转变，这是使用复吸收势方法进行研究的。我们的结果与气相中丙氨酰甘氨酸和甘氨酰丙氨酸分子的实验观察结果相当吻合。
