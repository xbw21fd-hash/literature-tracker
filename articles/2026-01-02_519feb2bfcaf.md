## Abstract
Activated escape from metastable states is a foundational concept in rate theory, underpinning diverse phenomena from chemical reactions to protein folding. A long-standing paradigm posits that potential energy landscape roughness invariably impedes kinetics by creating a multitude of local traps that suppress escape rates. Challenging this established paradigm, we investigate particles driven by discrete, non-equilibrium shot noise and reveal a striking inversion of this role. We demonstrate that, contrary to expectation, roughness can transform from a kinetic impediment into a potent catalyst, dramatically accelerating escape. The escape rate exhibits a striking non-monotonic dependence on the roughness amplitude, peaking at an optimal value. We attribute this counter-intuitive effect to a non-equilibrium mechanism we term slide inhibition , where local minima act as transient anchors. These anchors arrest dissipative relaxation between stochastic kicks, enabling a cumulative, ratchet-like ascent over the main energy barrier. The intrinsically non-equilibrium character of this synergy is powerfully underscored by the finding that thermal noise becomes destructive, destabilizing these crucial footholds. Our work unveils a constructive synergy between spatial disorder and non-equilibrium fluctuations, fundamentally recasting landscape roughness from a passive obstacle into a functional element for manipulating activated transport far from equilibrium.

## 摘要（中文）
亚稳态的激活逃逸是速率理论的基本概念，支撑着从化学反应到蛋白质折叠的各种现象。一个长期存在的范式认为，势能景观的粗糙度总是会通过产生大量抑制逃逸率的局部陷阱来阻碍动力学。为了挑战这一既定范式，我们研究了由离散、非平衡散粒噪声驱动的粒子，并揭示了这一角色的惊人反转。我们证明，与预期相反，粗糙度可以从动力障碍转变为有效的催化剂，极大地加速逃逸。逃逸率对粗糙度幅度表现出惊人的非单调依赖性，在最佳值处达到峰值。我们将这种反直觉的效应归因于一种非平衡机制，我们称之为滑动抑制，其中局部最小值充当瞬态锚。这些锚阻止了随机踢动之间的耗散弛豫，从而能够在主能量屏障上实现累积的、棘轮式的上升。热噪声具有破坏性，破坏了这些关键立足点的稳定性，这一发现有力地强调了这种协同作用本质上的非平衡特征。我们的工作揭示了空间无序和非平衡波动之间的建设性协同作用，从根本上将景观粗糙度从被动障碍物重塑为用于操纵远离平衡的激活运输的功能元素。
