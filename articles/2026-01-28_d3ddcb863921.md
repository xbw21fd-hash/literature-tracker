## Abstract
arXiv:2601.18972v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: Realizing high-throughput aberration-corrected Scanning Transmission Electron Microscopy (STEM) exploration of atomic structures requires rapid tuning of multipole probe correctors while compensating for the inevitable drift of the optical column. While automated alignment routines exist, conventional approaches rely on serial, gradient-free searches (e.g., Nelder-Mead) that are sample-inefficient and struggle to correct multiple interacting parameters simultaneously. Conversely, emerging deep learning methods offer speed but often lack the flexibility to adapt to varying sample conditions without extensive retraining. Here, we introduce a Multi-Objective Bayesian Optimization (MOBO) framework for rapid, data-efficient aberration correction. Importantly, this framework does not prescribe a single notion of image quality; instead, it enables user-defined, physically motivated reward formulations (e.g., symmetry-induced objectives) and uses Pareto fronts to expose the resulting trade-offs between competing experimental priorities. By using Gaussian Process regression to model the aberration landscape probabilistically, our workflow actively selects the most informative lens settings to evaluate next, rather than performing an exhaustive blind search. We demonstrate that this active learning loop is more robust than traditional optimization algorithms and effectively tunes focus, astigmatism, and higher-order aberrations. By balancing competing objectives, this approach enables "self-optimizing" microscopy by dynamically sustaining optimal performance during experiments.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.18972v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：实现原子结构的高通量像差校正扫描透射电子显微镜 (STEM) 探索需要快速调谐多极探针校正器，同时补偿光学柱不可避免的漂移。虽然存在自动对齐例程，但传统方法依赖于串行、无梯度搜索（例如 Nelder-Mead），这些搜索的样本效率低下，并且很难同时纠正多个相互作用的参数。相反，新兴的深度学习方法速度快，但通常缺乏灵活性，无法在不进行大量再训练的情况下适应不同的样本条件。在这里，我们引入了多目标贝叶斯优化 (MOBO) 框架，用于快速、数据高效的像差校正。重要的是，这个框架没有规定图像质量的单一概念；相反，它支持用户定义的、物理激励的奖励公式（例如，对称诱导的目标），并使用帕累托前沿来揭示竞争实验优先级之间的权衡。通过使用高斯过程回归对像差景观进行概率建模，我们的工作流程会主动选择信息最丰富的镜头设置来进行下一步评估，而不是执行详尽的盲目搜索。我们证明了这种主动学习循环比传统的优化算法更强大，并且可以有效地调整焦点、散光和高阶像差。通过平衡相互竞争的目标，该方法通过在实验过程中动态维持最佳性能来实现“自我优化”显微镜。
