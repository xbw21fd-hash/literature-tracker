## Abstract
arXiv:2512.19936v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: We develop a data-driven framework for discovering constitutive relations in models of fluid flow and scalar transport. Under the assumption that velocity and/or scalar fields are measured, our approach infers unknown closure terms in the governing equations as neural networks. The target to be discovered is the constitutive relations only, while the temporal derivative, convective transport terms, and pressure-gradient term in the governing equations are prescribed. The formulation is rooted in a variational principle from non-equilibrium thermodynamics, where the dynamics is defined by a free-energy functional and a dissipation functional. The unknown constitutive terms arise as functional derivatives of these functionals with respect to the state variables. To enable a flexible and structured model discovery, the free-energy and dissipation functionals are parameterized using neural networks, while their functional derivatives are obtained via automatic differentiation. This construction enforces thermodynamic consistency by design, guaranteeing monotonic decay of the total free energy and non-negative entropy production. The resulting method, termed GIMLET (Generalizable and Interpretable Model Learning through Embedded Thermodynamics), avoids reliance on a predefined library of candidate functions, unlike sparse regression or symbolic identification approaches. The learned models are generalizable in that functionals identified from one dataset can be transferred to distinct datasets governed by the same underlying equations. Moreover, the inferred free-energy and dissipation functions provide direct physical interpretability of the learned dynamics. The framework is demonstrated on several benchmark systems, including the viscous Burgers equation, the Kuramoto--Sivashinsky equation, and the incompressible Navier--Stokes equations for both Newtonian and non-Newtonian fluids.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2512.19936v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：我们开发了一个数据驱动的框架，用于发现流体流动和标量传输模型中的本构关系。在测量速度和/或标量场的假设下，我们的方法将控制方程中的未知闭包项推断为神经网络。待发现的目标仅是本构关系，而规定了控制方程中的时间导数、对流输运项和压力梯度项。该公式植根于非平衡热力学的变分原理，其中动力学由自由能泛函和耗散泛函定义。未知本构项作为这些泛函关于状态变量的泛函导数而出现。为了实现灵活且结构化的模型发现，使用神经网络对自由能和耗散泛函进行参数化，同时通过自动微分获得它们的泛函导数。这种结构通过设计强化热力学一致性，保证总自由能的单调衰减和非负熵产生。由此产生的方法称为 GIMLET（通过嵌入式热力学进行可推广和可解释的模型学习），与稀疏回归或符号识别方法不同，它避免了对预定义候选函数库的依赖。学习到的模型是可推广的，因为从一个数据集中识别的函数可以转移到由相同基础方程控制的不同数据集。此外，推断的自由能和耗散函数提供了所学动力学的直接物理解释。该框架在几个基准系统上进行了演示，包括牛顿和非牛顿流体的粘性 Burgers 方程、Kuramoto-Sivashinsky 方程以及不可压缩的 Navier-Stokes 方程。
