## Abstract
arXiv:2601.21385v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: We provide a theoretical framework to describe the dynamics of a free-electron beam interacting with quantized bound systems in arbitrary electromagnetic environments. This expands the quantum optics toolbox to incorporate free-electron beams for applications in highly tunable quantum control, imaging, and spectroscopy at the nanoscale. The framework recovers previously studied results and shows that electromagnetic environments can amplify the intrinsically weak coupling between a free-electron and a bound electron to reach previously inaccessible interaction regimes. We leverage this enhanced coupling for experimentally feasible protocols in coherent qubit control and towards the nondestructive readout and projective control of the electron beam's quantum-number statistics. Our framework is broadly applicable to microwave-frequency qubits, optical nanophotonics, cavity quantum electrodynamics, and emerging platforms at the interface of electron microscopy and quantum information.

arXiv:2601.21385v1 公告类型：cross 摘要：我们提供了一个理论框架来描述任意电磁环境中自由电子束与量子化束缚系统相互作用的动力学。这扩展了量子光学工具箱，将自由电子束纳入纳米尺度的高度可调量子控制、成像和光谱学应用。该框架恢复了先前研究的结果，并表明电磁环境可以放大自由电子和束缚电子之间本质上的弱耦合，以达到以前无法达到的相互作用状态。我们利用这种增强的耦合来实现相干量子位控制中实验上可行的协议以及电子束量子数统计的无损读出和投影控制。我们的框架广泛适用于微波频率量子位、光学纳米光子学、腔量子电动力学以及电子显微镜和量子信息界面的新兴平台。
