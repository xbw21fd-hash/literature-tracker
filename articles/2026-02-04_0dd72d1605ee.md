## Abstract
arXiv:2502.18380v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: When a fluid flows past a solid surface, its macroscopic motion arises from a subtle interplay between microscopic hydrodynamic and thermodynamic effects at the fluid-solid interface. Classical hydrodynamic models often rely on an unphysical no-slip boundary condition or an arbitrarily prescribed slip length, yet both approaches lack a rigorous physical foundation. This work introduces the concept of an Adsorption Layer (AL), an interfacial region of thickness delta l, where fluid-solid molecular interactions regulate both surface adsorption/depletion and interfacial slip. By applying the energy minimization principle, we derive balance equations within the AL that couple fluid-solid friction, viscous stresses, and surface adsorption dynamics. This framework establishes a self-consistent thermodynamic coupling between the AL and the bulk fluid, unlike conventional sharp-interface models. A key finding is the often-overlooked role and coupling of pressure and chemical potential gradients in the direction normal to the interface. This theoretical advance successfully explains the confinement-induced enhancement of water slippage in carbon nanotubes, quantitatively agreeing with molecular dynamics and experimental data -- an effect classical slip models fail to reproduce. Furthermore, when extended to binary liquids, the theory captures spatial variations in slip velocity near moving contact lines, highlighting the role of interfacial friction in shaping local flow. Our results demonstrate that the slip length is not a fixed material constant but rather an emergent, geometry- and composition-dependent property arising from coupled interfacial thermodynamics and hydrodynamics. This framework provides a physically grounded description of interfacial momentum transfer, with significant implications for microfluidics and surface engineering.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2502.18380v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：当流体流过固体表面时，其宏观运动源于流固界面处微观流体力学和热力学效应之间的微妙相互作用。经典的流体动力学模型通常依赖于非物理的无滑移边界条件或任意规定的滑移长度，但这两种方法都缺乏严格的物理基础。这项工作引入了吸附层 (AL) 的概念，这是一个厚度为 delta l 的界面区域，其中流体-固体分子相互作用调节表面吸附/耗尽和界面滑移。通过应用能量最小化原理，我们推导出 AL 内耦合流体-固体摩擦、粘性应力和表面吸附动力学的平衡方程。与传统的锐界面模型不同，该框架在 AL 和本体流体之间建立了自洽的热力学耦合。一个关键的发现是在垂直于界面的方向上压力和化学势梯度的作用和耦合经常被忽视。这一理论进展成功地解释了碳纳米管中限制引起的水滑移增强，在数量上与分子动力学和实验数据一致——这是经典滑移模型无法重现的效应。此外，当扩展到二元液体时，该理论捕获了移动接触线附近滑移速度的空间变化，突出了界面摩擦在塑造局部流动中的作用。我们的结果表明，滑移长度不是一个固定的材料常数，而是由界面热力学和流体力学耦合产生的一种新兴的、依赖于几何形状和成分的特性。该框架提供了界面动量传递的物理基础描述，对微流体和表面工程具有重要意义。
