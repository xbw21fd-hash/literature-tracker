## Abstract
arXiv:2510.21054v2 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Predictive dosimetry is central to enabling personalized radiopharmaceutical therapy (RPT), particularly in prostate specific membrane antigen (PSMA) targeted theranostics. In this work, we develop a three layer computational framework that integrates physiologically based pharmacokinetic (PBPK) modeling with machine learning (ML) to predict both physical (AUC, absorbed dose) and biological (BED, EQD2) dosimetric endpoints in tumors and major organs. In the first layer, we generated 640 virtual patients using PBPK simulations of F-18, Ga-68, and Cu-64 labeled PSMA PET tracers paired with Lu-177 PSMA therapy, producing 15360 tumor and organ time activity curves (TACs) under realistic biological variability and PET-like noise. In the second layer, TACs were transformed into quantitative kinetic features and mapped to physical and biological dose metrics. In the third layer, ML models (Random Forest, Extra Trees, Ridge, Gradient Boosting, and XGBoost) were trained to predict RPT doses from PET derived features, with performance evaluated using mean absolute percentage error (MAPE) and R2. Cu-64 PSMA-617 based PET yielded the most robust predictions, achieving tumor dose MAPE as low as 8 percent and 10 to 20 percent for normal organs, while F-18 DCFPyL showed volume dependent performance and Ga-68 PSMA-11 exhibited higher variability. SHAP analysis revealed that peak uptake, clearance, and early kinetic features dominated predictive performance across organs and endpoints. This PBPK ML framework enables scalable, physiology informed predictive dosimetry and provides a foundation for trial design and patient specific treatment planning in PSMA targeted RPT. These results demonstrate that pre therapy PET can serve as a reliable surrogate for post therapy dosimetry, enabling scalable personalization of PSMA targeted RPT.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2510.21054v2 公告类型：替换 摘要：预测剂量测定对于实现个性化放射性药物治疗 (RPT) 至关重要，特别是在前列腺特异性膜抗原 (PSMA) 靶向治疗诊断中。在这项工作中，我们开发了一个三层计算框架，将基于生理学的药代动力学 (PBPK) 建模与机器学习 (ML) 相结合，以预测肿瘤和主要器官的物理（AUC、吸收剂量）和生物（BED、EQD2）剂量学终点。在第一层中，我们使用 F-18、Ga-68 和 Cu-64 标记的 PSMA PET 示踪剂的 PBPK 模拟与 Lu-177 PSMA 治疗相结合，生成了 640 名虚拟患者，在真实的生物变异性和类 PET 噪声下生成了 15360 条肿瘤和器官时间活动曲线 (TAC)。在第二层，TAC 被转化为定量动力学特征，并映射到物理和生物剂量指标。在第三层中，机器学习模型（随机森林、额外树、岭、梯度提升和 XGBoost）经过训练，可根据 PET 派生特征预测 RPT 剂量，并使用平均绝对百分比误差 (MAPE) 和 R2 评估性能。基于 Cu-64 PSMA-617 的 PET 产生了最可靠的预测，肿瘤剂量 MAPE 低至 8%，正常器官的 MAPE 低至 10% 至 20%，而 F-18 DCFPyL 显示出体积依赖性性能，Ga-68 PSMA-11 显示出更高的变异性。 SHAP 分析显示，峰值摄取、清除和早期动力学特征主导了器官和终点的预测性能。该 PBPK ML 框架可实现可扩展、基于生理学的预测剂量测定，并为 PSMA 靶向 RPT 中的试验设计和患者特定治疗计划奠定基础。这些结果表明，治疗前 PET 可以作为治疗后剂量测定的可靠替代品，从而实现 PSMA 靶向 RPT 的可扩展个性化。
