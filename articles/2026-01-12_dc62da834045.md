## Abstract
arXiv:2601.05436v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: In applications such as atmospheric monitoring of greenhouse gases and pollutants, the detection and identification of trace concentrations of harmful gases is commonly achieved using non-dispersive infrared (NDIR) sensors. These devices employ a broadband infrared emitter, thermopile detector, and a spectrally selective bandpass filter tuned to the vibrational resonance of the target analyte. However, the fabrication of these filters is costly and limited to a single frequency. This limitation introduces a fundamental tradeoff, as broadening the optical passband width enhances sensitivity but compromises selectivity, whereas narrowing improves selectivity at the expense of sensitivity. In this work, we validate a filterless NDIR approach using a multi-peak thermal emitter developed through inverse design. This emitter enhances detection sensitivity by targeting multiple absorption bands, demonstrated through the creation of a sensor designed for the C-H vibrational modes of propane. Additionally, a set of single-peak emitters were developed to showcase the capability of designing highly selective sensors operating within close spectral proximity. These emitters, targeting the stretching modes of carbon monoxide and carbon dioxide, exhibit Q-factors above 50 and minimal crosstalk, enabling accurate detection of the target gas without interference from gases with spectrally adjacent absorption bands. This is enabled by the implementation of an aperiodic distributed Bragg reflectors, which allows for higher Q-factors with fewer layers than a periodic Bragg reflector using the same materials and number of layers, thereby reducing fabrication complexity and cost. Experimental results validate that this approach breaks the tradeoff between sensitivity and selectivity. This work highlights the potential of optimized thermal emitters for more efficient and compact gas sensing applications.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.05436v1 发布类型：新 摘要：在温室气体和污染物的大气监测等应用中，有害气体痕量浓度的检测和识别通常使用非色散红外（NDIR）传感器来实现。这些设备采用宽带红外发射器、热电堆检测器和光谱选择性带通滤波器，可调谐到目标分析物的振动共振。然而，这些滤波器的制造成本昂贵并且仅限于单一频率。这种限制引入了一个基本的权衡，因为加宽光通带宽度可以增强灵敏度，但会损害选择性，而变窄光通带宽度则以牺牲灵敏度为代价来提高选择性。在这项工作中，我们使用通过逆向设计开发的多峰热发射器来验证无滤波器 NDIR 方法。该发射器通过瞄准多个吸收带来提高检测灵敏度，这一点通过创建专为丙烷的 C-H 振动模式设计的传感器来证明。此外，还开发了一组单峰发射器，以展示设计在紧密光谱附近运行的高选择性传感器的能力。这些发射器针对一氧化碳和二氧化碳的拉伸模式，具有高于 50 的 Q 因子和最小的串扰，从而能够准确检测目标气体，而不会受到光谱相邻吸收带气体的干扰。这是通过非周期性分布式布拉格反射器的实现来实现的，与使用相同材料和层数的周期性布拉格反射器相比，非周期性分布式布拉格反射器可以用更少的层实现更高的品质因数，从而降低制造复杂性和成本。实验结果验证该方法打破了灵敏度和选择性之间的权衡。这项工作凸显了优化热发射器在更高效、更紧凑的气体传感应用中的潜力。
