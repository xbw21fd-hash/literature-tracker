## Abstract
arXiv:2602.11098v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Many biological processes involve numerous coupled degrees of freedom, yet free-energy estimation is often restricted to one-dimensional profiles to mitigate the high computational cost of multidimensional sampling. In this work, we extend Fokker--Planck Score Learning (FPSL) to efficiently reconstruct two-dimensional free-energy landscapes from non-equilibrium molecular dynamics simulations using different types of collective variables. We show that explicitly modeling orthogonal degrees of freedom reveals insights hidden in one-dimensional projections at negligible computational overhead. Additionally, exploiting symmetries in the underlying landscape enhances reconstruction accuracy, while regularization techniques ensure numerical robustness in sparsely sampled regions. We validate our approach on three distinct systems: the conformational dynamics of alanine dipeptide, as well as coarse-grained and all-atom models of solute permeation through lipid bilayers. We demonstrate that, because FPSL learns a smooth score function rather than histogram-based densities, it overcomes the exponential scaling of grid-based methods, establishing it as a data-efficient and scalable tool for multidimensional free-energy estimation.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.11098v1 公告类型：新 摘要：许多生物过程涉及大量耦合自由度，但自由能估计通常仅限于一维剖面，以减轻多维采样的高计算成本。在这项工作中，我们扩展了福克-普朗克分数学习（FPSL），以使用不同类型的集体变量从非平衡分子动力学模拟中有效地重建二维自由能景观。我们表明，显式建模正交自由度揭示了隐藏在一维投影中的见解，而计算开销可以忽略不计。此外，利用底层景观的对称性可以提高重建精度，而正则化技术可以确保稀疏采样区域的数值鲁棒性。我们在三个不同的系统上验证了我们的方法：丙氨酸二肽的构象动力学，以及溶质渗透通过脂质双层的粗粒度和全原子模型。我们证明，由于 FPSL 学习的是平滑得分函数而不是基于直方图的密度，因此它克服了基于网格的方法的指数缩放，将其确立为用于多维自由能估计的数据高效且可扩展的工具。
