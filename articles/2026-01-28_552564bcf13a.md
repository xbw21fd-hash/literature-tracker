## Abstract
arXiv:2511.11742v3 Announce Type: replace Abstract: Generalized friendship paradoxes occur when, on average, our friends have more of some attribute than us. These paradoxes are relevant to many aspects of human interaction, notably in social science and epidemiology. Here, we derive new theoretical results concerning the inevitability of a paradox arising, using a linear algebra perspective. Following the seminal 1991 work of Scott L. Feld, we consider two distinct ways to measure and compare averages, which may be regarded as global and local. For global averaging, we show that a generalized friendship paradox holds for a large family of walk-based centralities, including Katz centrality and total subgraph communicability, and also for nonbacktracking eigenvector centrality. However, we also find counterexamples for centralities based on walks of even length. For local averaging we establish a paradox for nonbacktracking eigenvector centrality and we characterize the cases where the paradox holds with equality for the walk-based case. Defining loneliness as the reciprocal of the number of friends, we show that for this attribute the generalized and local friendship paradoxes always hold in reverse. In this sense, we are always more lonely, on average, than our friends. We also derive global and local averaging paradoxes for the case where the arithmetic mean is replaced by the geometric mean. As well as unifying and adding to the literature in this area, we highlight some open questions.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2511.11742v3 公告类型：替换 摘要：当我们的朋友平均拥有比我们更多的某些属性时，就会出现广义友谊悖论。这些悖论与人类互动的许多方面有关，特别是在社会科学和流行病学方面。在这里，我们利用线性代数的视角得出了关于悖论出现的必然性的新理论结果。继 Scott L. Feld 1991 年的开创性工作之后，我们考虑了两种不同的方法来衡量和比较平均值，这可以被视为全局平均值和局部平均值。对于全局平均，我们证明了广义友谊悖论适用于基于步行的中心性大家族，包括 Katz 中心性和总子图可传播性，也适用于非回溯特征向量中心性。然而，我们也找到了基于偶数长度游走的中心性的反例。对于局部平均，我们建立了非回溯特征向量中心性的悖论，并且我们描述了该悖论与基于步行的情况相等的情况。将孤独定义为朋友数量的倒数，我们表明对于这个属性，广义和局部友谊悖论总是相反。从这个意义上说，平均而言，我们总是比我们的朋友更孤独。我们还针对算术平均值被几何平均值代替的情况导出了全局和局部平均悖论。除了统一和补充该领域的文献之外，我们还强调了一些悬而未决的问题。
