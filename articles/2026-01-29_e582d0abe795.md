## Abstract
Author(s): T. Parry, A. Gade, B. A. Brown, D. Weisshaar, S. A. Gillespie, D. Bazin, T. Beck, P. C. Bender, J. Belarge, C. M. Campbell, B. A. Elman, K. W. Kemper, B. Longfellow, E. M. Lunderberg, and A. O. Macchiavelli This work is a new and impressive example for the potential of γ -ray-tagged neutron-adding transfer reactions at high beam velocities. By tuning the desired angular momentum transfer, this reaction allows selecting specific neutron configurations and complements spectroscopic information from the more common neutron-removal and low-energy ( d , p ) transfer reactions. The experiment selectively populated excited states in 47 Ca—a neighbor of the doubly magic 48 Ca nucleus—based on ℓ ≥ 3 neutron configurations following one-neutron pickup by 46 Ca from a 12 C target, and recorded their decay with the GRETINA detector array. The experimental results demonstrate that the ℓ = 3 spectroscopic strength is rather fragmented, in contrast to shell-model calculations, which predict it to be strongly concentrated in only a few states. The work not only suggests opportunities for a modern ( d , p ) measurement to complement the present selective high- ℓ data but more generally introduces γ -ray-tagged neutron-adding transfer reactions as a complementary tool for exploring neutron single-particle excitations. [Phys. Rev. C 113, 014328] Published Thu Jan 29, 2026
