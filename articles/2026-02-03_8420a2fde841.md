## Abstract
arXiv:2602.00336v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: We extend physical differential fuzz testing as an anti-tamper method for radiation detectors [Vavrek et al., Science and Global Security 2025] to comparisons across multiple detector units. The method was previously introduced as a tamper detection method for authenticating a single radiation detector in nuclear safeguards and treaty verification scenarios, and works by randomly sampling detector configuration parameters to produce a sequence of spectra that form a baseline signature of an untampered system. At a later date, after potential tampering, the same random sequence of parameters is used to generate another series of spectra that can be compared against the baseline. Anomalies in the series of comparisons indicate changes in detector behavior, which may be due to tampering. One limitation of this original method is that once the detector has `gone downrange' and may have been tampered with, the original baseline is fixed, and a new trusted baseline can never be established if tests at new parameters are required. In this work, we extend our anti-tamper fuzz testing concept to multiple detector units, such that the downrange detector can be compared against a trusted or `golden copy' detector, even despite normal inter-detector manufacturing variations. We show using three NaI detectors that this inter-detector differential fuzz testing can detect a representative attack, even when the tested and golden copy detectors are from different manufacturers and have different performances. Here, detecting tampering requires visualizing the comparison metric vs. the parameter values and not just the sample number; moreover this baseline is non-linear and may require anomaly detection methods more complex than a simple threshold. Overall, this extension to multiple detectors improves prospects for operationalizing the technique in real-world treaty verification and safeguards contexts.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.00336v1 公告类型：新 摘要：我们将物理差分模糊测试作为辐射探测器的防篡改方法 [Vavrek 等人，科学与全球安全 2025] 扩展到多个探测器单元之间的比较。该方法之前被引入作为一种篡改检测方法，用于在核保障和条约核查场景中验证单个辐射探测器，并通过随机采样探测器配置参数来产生一系列光谱，形成未篡改系统的基线签名。稍后，在可能的篡改之后，使用相同的随机参数序列来生成另一系列可以与基线进行比较的光谱。系列比较中的异常表明检测器行为发生变化，这可能是由于篡改造成的。这种原始方法的一个局限性是，一旦探测器“下降范围”并且可能已被篡改，原始基线就被固定，并且如果需要在新参数下进行测试，则永远无法建立新的可信基线。在这项工作中，我们将防篡改模糊测试概念扩展到多个探测器单元，这样即使探测器之间存在正常的制造差异，也可以将下行探测器与可信或“黄金副本”探测器进行比较。我们使用三个 NaI 检测器证明，这种检测器间差分模糊测试可以检测代表性攻击，即使测试的检测器和黄金副本检测器来自不同的制造商并且具有不同的性能。在这里，检测篡改需要可视化比较指标与参数值，而不仅仅是样本数；此外，该基线是非线性的，可能需要比简单阈值更复杂的异常检测方法。总体而言，这种对多个探测器的扩展改善了该技术在现实世界条约核查和保障背景下的应用前景。
