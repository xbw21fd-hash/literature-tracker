## Abstract
arXiv:2602.05372v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Discovering heterogeneous catalysts tailored for specific reaction intermediates remains a fundamental bottleneck in materials science. While traditional trial-and-error methods and recent generative models have shown promise, they struggle to capture the intrinsic coupling between surface geometry and adsorbate interactions. To address this limitation, we propose CatFlow, a flow matching-based framework for de novo design and structure prediction of heterogeneous catalysts. Our model operates on a primitive cell-based factorized representation of the slab-adsorbate complex, reducing the number of learnable variables by an average of 9.2x while explicitly encoding the surface orientation of the slab-adsorbate interface. Experiments on the Open Catalyst 2020 dataset demonstrate that CatFlow significantly improves the structural fidelity of generated catalysts compared to autoregressive and sequential baselines. Further experiments show that the generated structures accurately capture the adsorption energy distributions of physically plausible interfaces and lie closer to thermodynamic local minima.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.05372v1 公告类型：新 摘要：发现针对特定反应中间体定制的多相催化剂仍然是材料科学的基本瓶颈。虽然传统的试错方法和最近的生成模型已显示出希望，但它们难以捕获表面几何形状和吸附物相互作用之间的内在耦合。为了解决这一限制，我们提出了 CatFlow，一种基于流动匹配的框架，用于多相催化剂的从头设计和结构预测。我们的模型基于平板吸附物复合体的原始细胞分解表示，将可学习变量的数量平均减少了 9.2 倍，同时显式编码平板吸附物界面的表面方向。 Open Catalyst 2020 数据集上的实验表明，与自回归和顺序基线相比，CatFlow 显着提高了生成的催化剂的结构保真度。进一步的实验表明，生成的结构准确地捕获了物理上合理的界面的吸附能分布，并且更接近热力学局部最小值。
