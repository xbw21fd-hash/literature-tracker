## Abstract
arXiv:2602.15176v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Nonwoven fibre networks underpin filtration, insulation and geotextiles, where liquid uptake, redistribution and release govern performance. In needle-punched felts, barbed needles mechanically entangle fibres and partially reorient them toward the thickness direction ($z$), creating out-of-plane "pillars" and heterogeneity. While mechanical and structural consequences of needling are well documented, dynamic $z$-direction transport in partly saturated networks remains difficult to access due to opacity and sub-second timescales. Here we combine micro-CT ($\mu$CT) of dry structure with time-resolved X-ray radiography during droplet addition to quantify through-thickness transport as a function of saturation and needling intensity, using a compact Washburn-type descriptor for dynamics. Results show an exponential dependence of $z$-directional liquid transport on saturation, consistent with previous models for in-plane relative permeability of nonwoven networks. Additionally, increased needle-punch intensity reorients fibres toward the $z$-direction, forming preferential flow pathways that enhance through-thickness transport, even as single-phase permeability decreases. These findings underscore needle-punch as a key design parameter for tuning liquid transport in nonwoven fibre networks. The approach provides an experimental and modelling framework for dynamic, capillarity-driven transport in opaque fibrous materials.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.15176v1 公告类型：新 摘要：非织造纤维网络支撑着过滤、隔热和土工织物，其中液体的吸收、再分配和释放控制着性能。在针刺毡中，带刺的针机械地缠结纤维，并将其部分地重新定向到厚度方向（$z$），从而产生面外“柱”和异质性。虽然针刺的机械和结构后果已有充分记录，但由于不透明和亚秒级时间尺度，部分饱和网络中的动态 $z$ 方向传输仍然难以访问。在这里，我们将干结构的显微 CT ($\mu$CT) 与液滴添加过程中的时间分辨 X 射线照相相结合，使用紧凑的 Washburn 型动力学描述符来量化厚度方向传输作为饱和度和针刺强度的函数。结果显示，$z$ 方向的液体传输对饱和度呈指数依赖性，与之前的非织造网络面内相对渗透率模型一致。此外，增加的针刺强度可将纤维重新定向到$z$方向，形成优先流动路径，即使单相渗透性降低，也能增强全厚度传输。这些发现强调了针刺是调节非织造纤维网络中液体传输的关键设计参数。该方法为不透明纤维材料中动态、毛细管驱动的传输提供了实验和建模框架。
