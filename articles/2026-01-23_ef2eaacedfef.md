## Abstract
arXiv:2601.15317v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Most models in game theory and network formation implicitly assume that relations between agents are feasible whenever incentives are aligned or interaction opportunities exist. Under this premise analytical attention is directed toward equilibrium efficiency or probabilistic link formation while the possibility that a relation may be structurally infeasible is rarely examined. This paper develops a static axiomatic framework in which relation maintenance is treated as a problem of structural compatibility rather than strategic choice or stochastic realization. Agents occupy positions in an abstract space and relations are subject to minimum conditions defined over these positions. A bifurcation event such as a vote declaration or institutional assignment fixes agents positions and thereby determines which relations are compatible. We identify position dependent gain axes as the key source of structural selectivity and prove an impossibility result under any non degenerate positional constraint no bifurcation event can preserve all relations. Instead the post event network necessarily exhibits either the simultaneous emergence of fragmentation and cohesion or a degenerate trivial case in which constraints are position independent. The result is purely structural and does not rely on preferences beliefs incentives or dynamic adjustment. It establishes a fundamental limit on universally cohesive outcomes and reframes division not as a failure of design or coordination but as a logical consequence of positional constraints.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.15317v1 公告类型：新 摘要：博弈论和网络形成中的大多数模型都隐含地假设，只要激励一致或存在互动机会，主体之间的关系都是可行的。在此前提下，分析注意力集中在均衡效率或概率链接形成上，而很少检查关系在结构上不可行的可能性。本文开发了一个静态公理框架，其中关系维护被视为结构兼容性问题，而不是策略选择或随机实现问题。代理在抽象空间中占据位置，并且关系受到针对这些位置定义的最低条件的约束。诸如投票声明或机构分配之类的分叉事件固定了代理的位置，从而确定哪些关系是兼容的。我们将位置相关的增益轴确定为结构选择性的关键来源，并证明了在任何非简并位置约束下的不可能结果，没有分叉事件可以保留所有关系。相反，事后网络必然表现出分裂和凝聚力同时出现，或者约束与位置无关的退化琐碎情况。结果纯粹是结构性的，不依赖偏好信念激励或动态调整。它对普遍具有凝聚力的结果建立了基本限制，并将分裂重新定义为位置约束的逻辑结果，而不是设计或协调的失败。
