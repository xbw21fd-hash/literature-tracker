## Abstract
arXiv:2602.15217v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: The rapid growth of quantum information science and technology (QIST) in the 21st century has created both excitement and uncertainty about the field's trajectory. This qualitative study presents perspectives from leading quantum researchers, who are educators, on fundamental questions frequently posed by students, the public, and the media regarding QIST. Through in-depth interviews, we explored several issues related to QIST including the following key areas: the current state of quantum computing in the noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) era and timelines for fault-tolerant quantum computers, the feasibility of personal quantum computers in our pockets, and promising qubit architectures for future development. Our findings reveal diverse yet convergent perspectives on these issues. While experts agree that the current machines with physical qubits that are being built currently should be called quantum computers, most estimated that it will take a decade to build a small fault-tolerant quantum computer, and several decades to achieve scalable systems capable of running Shor's factoring algorithm with quantum advantage. Regarding carrying a quantum computer in the pocket, experts viewed quantum computers as specialized tools that will remain in central locations such as data centers and can be accessed remotely for applications for which they are particularly effective compared to classical computers. Quantum researchers suggested that multiple platforms show promise, with no clear winner emerging. These insights provide valuable guidance for educators, policymakers, and the broader community in establishing realistic expectations for developments in this exciting field. Our findings can provide valuable information for educators to clarify student doubts about these important yet confusing issues related to quantum technologies.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.15217v1 公告类型：新 摘要：21 世纪量子信息科学与技术 (QIST) 的快速发展给该领域的发展轨迹带来了兴奋和不确定性。这项定性研究针对学生、公众和媒体经常提出的有关 QIST 的基本问题，提出了作为教育工作者的领先量子研究人员的观点。通过深入采访，我们探讨了与 QIST 相关的几个问题，包括以下关键领域：嘈杂的中尺度量子 (NISQ) 时代量子计算的现状和容错量子计算机的时间表、我们口袋里的个人量子计算机的可行性以及未来发展的有前途的量子比特架构。我们的研究结果揭示了对这些问题的不同但趋同的观点。虽然专家们一致认为，目前正在建造的具有物理量子位的机器应该被称为量子计算机，但大多数人估计需要十年的时间才能建造出一台小型容错量子计算机，并且需要几十年的时间才能实现能够运行具有量子优势的肖尔因子分解算法的可扩展系统。关于将量子计算机放在口袋里，专家们将量子计算机视为专用工具，将保留在数据中心等中心位置，并且可以远程访问它们比传统计算机特别有效的应用程序。量子研究人员表示，多个平台都显示出前景，但目前还没有明显的赢家出现。这些见解为教育工作者、政策制定者和更广泛的社区提供了宝贵的指导，帮助他们对这一令人兴奋的领域的发展建立现实的期望。我们的研究结果可以为教育工作者提供有价值的信息，以澄清学生对这些与量子技术相关的重要但令人困惑的问题的疑虑。
