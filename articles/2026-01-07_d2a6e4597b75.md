## Abstract
arXiv:2601.02508v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: We introduce Fermi Sets, a universal and physically interpretable neural architecture for fermionic many-body wavefunctions. Building on a ``parity-graded'' representation [1], we prove that any continuous fermionic wavefunction on a compact domain can be approximated to arbitrary accuracy by a linear combination of K antisymmetric basis functions--such as pairwise products or Slater determinants--multiplied by symmetric functions. A key result is that the number of required bases is provably small: K=1 suffices in one-dimensional continua (and on lattices in any dimension), K=2 suffices in two dimensions, and in higher dimensions K grows at most linearly with particle number. The antisymmetric bases can be learned by small neural networks, while the symmetric factors are implemented by permutation-invariant networks whose width scales only linearly with particle number. Thus, Fermi Sets achieve universal approximation of fermionic wavefunctions with minimal overhead while retaining clear physical interpretability.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.02508v1 公告类型：新 摘要：我们介绍费米集，一种通用且物理可解释的费米多体波函数神经架构。基于“宇称分级”表示[1]，我们证明紧致域上的任何连续费米子波函数都可以通过 K 个反对称基函数（例如成对乘积或 Slater 行列式）乘以对称函数的线性组合来近似到任意精度。一个关键结果是所需碱基的数量可证明很小：在一维连续体（以及任何维度的晶格）中 K=1 就足够了，在二维中 K=2 就足够了，而在更高维度中，K 最多与粒子数呈线性增长。反对称基可以通过小型神经网络来学习，而对称因子则通过排列不变网络来实现，其宽度仅与粒子数成线性比例。因此，费米集以最小的开销实现了费米波函数的通用逼近，同时保留了清晰的物理可解释性。
