## Abstract
A thermodynamic theory of ``weak'' ferromagnetism of $alpha$-Fe2O3, MnCO3 and CoCO3 is developed on the basis of landau's theory of phase transitions of the second kind. It is shown that the ``weak'' ferromagnetism is due to the relativistic spin-lattice and the magnetic dipole interactions. A strong dependence of the properties of ``weak'' ferromagnetics on the magnetic crystalline symmetry is noted and the behaviour of these ferromagnetics in a magnetic field is studied.
