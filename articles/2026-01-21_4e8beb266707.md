## Abstract
arXiv:2601.14100v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: We propose an electrically controlled protocol to detect weak Lorentz-violating (LV) backgrounds through the second-order shift photocurrent in noncentrosymmetric crystals. Using a spinful Rice--Mele model, we show that a stationary LV background induces a momentum-odd correction to the Bloch Hamiltonian, which generates an odd-in-field contribution to the shift current. This leads to a directional asymmetry, whereby the photocurrent distinguishes opposite orientations of an applied static field. The effect originates from an LV-induced deformation of the interband phase and can be isolated experimentally by comparing field-reversed configurations, with vanishing response at transverse orientations, providing an internal consistency check. Our results demonstrate that nonlinear optical responses offer a practical and symmetry-selective route for probing LV effects in solid-state systems.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2601.14100v1 公告类型：新 摘要：我们提出了一种电控协议，通过非中心对称晶体中的二阶位移光电流来检测弱洛伦兹破坏 (LV) 背景。使用旋转的 Rice-Mele 模型，我们表明静止的 LV 背景会引起对布洛赫哈密顿量的动量奇校正，从而对移位电流产生奇场贡献。这导致方向不对称，从而光电流区分所施加的静态场的相反方向。该效应源自 LV 引起的带间相位变形，并且可以通过比较场反转配置进行实验隔离，横向方向上的响应消失，从而提供内部一致性检查。我们的结果表明，非线性光学响应为探测固态系统中的 LV 效应提供了一种实用且对称选择性的途径。
