## Abstract
We show that skyrmions on the surface of a magnetic topological insulator may experience an attractive interaction that leads to the formation of a skyrmion-skyrmion bound state. This is in contrast to the case of skyrmions in a conventional chiral ferromagnet, for which the intrinsic interaction is repulsive. The origin of skyrmion binding in our model is the molecular hybridization of topologically protected electronic orbitals associated with each skyrmion. Attraction between the skyrmions can therefore be controlled by tuning a chemical potential that populates/depopulates the lowest-energy molecular orbital. We find that the skyrmion-skyrmion bound state can be made stable, unstable, or metastable depending on the chemical potential, magnetic field, and easy-axis anisotropy of the underlying ferromagnet, resulting in a rich phase diagram. Finally, we discuss the possibility of realizing this effect in a recently synthesized Cr-doped (Bi2-ySby)2Te3 heterostructure.
