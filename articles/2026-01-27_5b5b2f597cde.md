## Abstract
arXiv:2511.14832v2 Announce Type: replace-cross Abstract: Anomaly detection has the potential to discover new physics in unexplored regions of the data. However, choosing the best anomaly detector for a given data set in a model-agnostic way is an important challenge which has hitherto largely been neglected. In this paper, we introduce the data-driven ARGOS metric, which has a sound theoretical foundation and is empirically shown to robustly select the most sensitive anomaly detection model given the data. Focusing on weakly-supervised, classifier-based anomaly detection methods, we show that the ARGOS metric outperforms other model selection metrics previously used in the literature, in particular the binary cross-entropy loss. We explore several realistic applications, including hyperparameter tuning as well as architecture and feature selection, and in all cases we demonstrate that ARGOS is robust to the noisy conditions of anomaly detection.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2511.14832v2 公告类型：replace-cross 摘要：异常检测有可能在数据的未探索区域中发现新的物理现象。然而，以与模型无关的方式为给定数据集选择最佳的异常检测器是一个重要的挑战，但迄今为止在很大程度上被忽视了。在本文中，我们介绍了数据驱动的 ARGOS 指标，该指标具有良好的理论基础，并且经经验证明可以在给定数据的情况下稳健地选择最敏感的异常检测模型。专注于弱监督、基于分类器的异常检测方法，我们表明 ARGOS 指标优于文献中先前使用的其他模型选择指标，特别是二元交叉熵损失。我们探索了几种实际应用，包括超参数调整以及架构和特征选择，并且在所有情况下，我们都证明了 ARGOS 对于异常检测的噪声条件具有鲁棒性。
