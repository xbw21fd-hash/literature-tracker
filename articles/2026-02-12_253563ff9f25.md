## Abstract
arXiv:2602.10596v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: For low-temperature experiments such as liquid xenon dark matter detectors, it is crucial to read out detector signals from cryostats. Traditionally, photoelectrical signals are transmitted from the cryogenic region to the outside using coaxial cables through vacuum feedthroughs on the cryostats. In this paper, we investigate an analog optical transmission method in which the raw electrical signals are converted into optical signals with light intensity linearly proportional to the electrical amplitude, transmitted out of the cryogenic environment through optical fiber, and subsequently converted back into electrical signals by photoelectric devices while preserving the signal waveform. This new approach offers advantages, including low attenuation over long-distance transmission and reduced crosstalk across the feedthroughs. Additionally, the low-temperature optical wavelength multiplexing scheme has been investigated and applied, increasing the transmission capability of a single fiber. At -100 degree Celsius, the proposed analog optical readout system achieves a -3dB bandwidth of larger than 150MHz, a dynamic range of up to 500mV, and a low cryogenic-region power consumption of 70mW per channel, demonstrating its strong potential for low-temperature experiments.

## 摘要（中文）
arXiv:2602.10596v1 公告类型：新 摘要：对于液氙暗物质探测器等低温实验来说，从低温恒温器中读出探测器信号至关重要。传统上，光电信号使用同轴电缆通过低温恒温器上的真空馈通从低温区域传输到外部。在本文中，我们研究了一种模拟光传输方法，其中原始电信号被转换为光强度与电幅度成线性比例的光信号，通过光纤传输出低温环境，随后由光电器件转换回电信号，同时保留信号波形。这种新方法具有多种优势，包括长距离传输时的低衰减以及减少馈通间的串扰。此外，低温光波长复用方案已得到研究和应用，提高了单纤的传输能力。在-100摄氏度下，所提出的模拟光学读出系统实现了大于150MHz的-3dB带宽、高达500mV的动态范围以及每通道70mW的低低温区功耗，展示了其在低温实验中的强大潜力。
